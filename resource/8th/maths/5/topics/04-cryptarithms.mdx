## What are Cryptarithms?

Cryptarithms are puzzles where digits are replaced by letters.
**Rules:**
1.  Each letter represents a unique digit ($0-9$).
2.  The first digit of a number cannot be 0.
3.  Standard arithmetic rules apply.

## Example 1: Addition
$$
\begin{array}{c}
  \quad A \ 1 \\
+ \ 1 \ B \\
\hline
  \quad B \ 0
\end{array}
$$

**Solution:**
1.  **Units Column:** $1 + B$ ends in $0$.
    *   $1 + B = 10 \implies B = 9$.
    *   Carry over 1.
2.  **Tens Column:** $1 (\text{carry}) + A + 1 = B$.
    *   Since $B = 9$, $2 + A = 9 \implies A = 7$.
3.  **Check:** $71 + 19 = 90$. Correct.

## Example 2: Multiplication
$$
\begin{array}{c}
  \quad A \ B \\
\times \quad \quad 5 \\
\hline
  \quad C \ A \ B
\end{array}
$$

**Analysis:**
1.  **Units Digit:** $B \times 5$ ends in $B$.
    *   Possible values for $B$: $0$ or $5$.
2.  **If B = 0:** $A0 \times 5 = CA0$.
    *   $5 \times A$ ends in $A$. Possible $A$: $5$ (since $5 \times 5 = 25$).
    *   If $A=5, B=0$: $50 \times 5 = 250$.
    *   $C=2, A=5, B=0$. Fits the pattern $CAB$.
3.  **If B = 5:** $A5 \times 5 = CA5$.
    *   $5 \times 5 = 25$ (Carry 2).
    *   $(5 \times A) + 2$ ends in $A$.
    *   Try $A=2$: $(5 \times 2) + 2 = 12$. Ends in 2.
    *   If $A=2, B=5$: $25 \times 5 = 125$.
    *   $C=1, A=2, B=5$. Fits.

**Answer:** Two solutions exist ($50 \times 5 = 250$ or $25 \times 5 = 125$).
