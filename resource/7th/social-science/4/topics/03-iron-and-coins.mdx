## The Iron Revolution

The Harappan civilization (First Urbanisation) mastered copper and bronze. However, the Second Urbanisation was driven by a major technological shift: **Iron Metallurgy**.

*   By the late 2nd millennium BCE, iron tools became widespread.
*   **Agriculture:** Stronger iron ploughs and tools allowed people to clear dense forests and farm the hard, fertile soil of the Ganga plains on a much larger scale.
*   **Warfare:** Iron produced lighter and sharper weapons (swords, spears, shields) compared to bronze. This led to military campaigns, alliances, and the eventual rise of large empires.

## The Birth of Coinage

As agriculture flourished and cities grew, trade expanded rapidly across different regions. Bartering goods was no longer efficient.

<Callout variant="info">
**Punch-Marked Coins:**
To facilitate this growing trade, India saw its first use of coins. The earliest coins were made of **silver** (a soft metal). Symbols were stamped or 'punched' into the metal, giving them the name **punch-marked coins**.
</Callout>

*   Later, coins were also minted in copper, gold, and other metals.
*   Usually, a *mahājanapada* issued its own specific coins, but because trade was so interconnected, coins from one region were widely accepted and used in neighboring regions.
