## Introduction

After the decline of the Harappan civilization (India's First Urbanisation), urban life largely vanished from the subcontinent for nearly a millennium. However, around the 1st millennium BCE, a vibrant new phase began, primarily in the fertile Ganga plains. 

This period, known as the **Second Urbanisation**, witnessed the rise of powerful regional states, the widespread use of iron, the introduction of coinage, and the development of complex social systems.

## Key Terms to Remember

*   **Janapada:** A territory where a clan or group of people settled down (literally "where the people have set foot").
*   **Mahājanapada:** Larger, more powerful states formed by the merging or growth of smaller *janapadas*.
*   **Moat:** A deep, wide ditch surrounding a fort or city, filled with water for defense.
*   **Sabhā / Samiti:** Assemblies or councils where matters concerning the clan were discussed.
*   **Varṇa:** A four-fold social classification system mentioned in Vedic texts (Brahmins, Kshatriyas, Vaishyas, Shudras).
*   **Jāti:** A specific professional or occupational community/group, often hereditary.
