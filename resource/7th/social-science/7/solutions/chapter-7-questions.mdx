## Solutions

**1. Imagine you receive a letter from someone living in the Gupta Empire. Complete the letter with a short paragraph describing life in the Gupta Empire.**

*Answer Example:*
"Greetings from Pāṭaliputra! Life here is vibrant and full of excitement. Just yesterday, I witnessed a massive procession of merchants arriving from the southern seaports, bringing exotic spices and Roman gold. Our city is incredibly prosperous; the markets are overflowing with fine textiles and intricate terracotta figures. What amazes me most is the peace we enjoy. The Emperor's administration is strict but fair, allowing us to focus on trade and learning without the constant fear of war. Last week, I visited a free hospital set up by a wealthy Vaishya family, where doctors were treating the poor using techniques from the newly codified *Suśhruta Saṃhitā*. In the evenings, the city squares are filled with people discussing the latest beautiful plays written by the great poet Kālidāsa, or debating the shocking new astronomical theories proposed by the scholar Āryabhaṭa. It truly feels like we are living in a golden era of knowledge and beauty!"

**2. Which Gupta ruler was also known as the ‘Vikramāditya’?**

*   **Answer:** **Chandragupta II** was also known by the title ‘Vikramāditya’.

**3. “Periods of peace support the development of various aspects of sociocultural life, literature, and the development of science and technology.” Examine this statement in the light of the Gupta empire.**

*   **Answer:** The Gupta Empire is a perfect example of this statement. Because rulers like Chandragupta II managed to secure their borders through strong military campaigns and strategic matrimonial alliances (like with the Vākāṭakas), the inner core of the empire experienced decades of unbroken peace. 
    Without the constant drain of fighting wars, the royal treasury (filled by robust overland and maritime trade) could be spent on patronizing the arts and sciences. This peace and state funding allowed scholars like Āryabhaṭa and Varāhamihira the time and resources to make astronomical breakthroughs. It allowed poets like Kālidāsa to refine Sanskrit literature. It also allowed craftsmen the years required to carve the magnificent, intricate cave temples at Ajanta and Udayagiri. Creativity requires stability, and the "Classical Age" of the Guptas proves it.

**6. Who were the Pallavas and where did they rule?**

*   **Answer:** The Pallavas were a powerful dynasty that emerged in southern India as the Sātavāhana empire declined. They gradually consolidated their power and ruled over parts of present-day Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Telangana, and Andhra Pradesh. Their capital was Kānchipuram. They were great patrons of education (establishing *ghaṭikās*) and architecture, building magnificent rock-cut caves and temples dedicated to Śhiva.
