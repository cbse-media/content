## Questions and Activities

**1. Write names of the various types of government that you have learnt in the chapter.**

**Answer:** 
The various types of government discussed in the chapter are:
1.  **Democracy:** (Direct and Representative - Parliamentary & Presidential)
2.  **Monarchy:** (Absolute and Constitutional)
3.  **Theocracy**
4.  **Dictatorship**
5.  **Oligarchy**

***

**2. Which type of Government does India have? And why is that called that type?**

**Answer:** 
India has a **Parliamentary Democracy** (a form of Representative Democracy). 
It is called this because:
*   **Democracy:** The source of authority is the people, who elect their representatives through Universal Adult Franchise.
*   **Parliamentary:** The members of the executive (the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers) are also members of the legislature (the Parliament). The executive remains in power only as long as it enjoys the confidence of the legislature (the Lok Sabha).

***

**3. You read that an independent judiciary is present in all types of democracies. State any three reasons why you think it is important for the judiciary to be independent.**

**Answer:** 
An independent judiciary is vital for the following reasons:
1.  **Protection of Fundamental Rights:** It ensures that the basic rights of citizens (like freedom of speech and equality) are protected from being violated by the state or other individuals.
2.  **Check on Power (Separation of Powers):** It acts as a check on the Legislative and Executive branches, ensuring they do not misuse their power or act outside the limits of the Constitution.
3.  **Fair and Impartial Justice:** It allows judges to resolve disputes and interpret laws without fear, pressure, or influence from powerful politicians or wealthy individuals.

***

**4. Do you think democratic government is better than other forms of government? Why?**

**Answer:** 
Yes, a democratic government is widely considered better than other forms of government for several reasons:
*   **Accountability:** The government is directly answerable to the people. If they do not perform well, people can vote them out in the next election.
*   **Equality and Freedom:** Democracy guarantees fundamental rights, equality before the law, and freedom of expression, which are absent in dictatorships or absolute monarchies.
*   **Citizen Participation:** It treats citizens not as subjects, but as the ultimate source of power who actively participate in decision-making through elections.

**7. What are possible hurdles in a democracy in achieving its values and ideals? How can they be overcome?**

**Answer:**
*   **Hurdles:** Some major challenges include corruption, massive wealth disparity, excessive influence by a small group of elites (oligarchic tendencies), erosion of the judiciary's independence, and manipulation of information (e.g., fake news).
*   **How to overcome them:** These can be overcome by citizens remaining actively vigilant, participating in the democratic process, educating themselves on issues, demanding transparency from leaders, and protecting the independence of institutions like courts and the media.

***

**8. Democracy is different from monarchy and dictatorship. Explain.**

**Answer:**
*   **Source of Power:** In a democracy, power comes from the *people* through voting. In a monarchy, it is *hereditary* (passed down a royal family). In a dictatorship, power is usually seized by *force* by an individual or small group.
*   **Rules and Constitution:** Democracies operate within the strict limits of a Constitution. Dictators face no constitutional limits and make rules arbitrarily. Absolute monarchs also have unchecked power, though constitutional monarchs do not.
*   **Rights of Citizens:** Democracy ensures fundamental rights, freedom of speech, and equality. In dictatorships and absolute monarchies, citizens are often subjugated, and speaking against the ruler is punished.
*   **Accountability:** Democratic leaders are accountable to the public. Monarchs and dictators are generally not answerable to anyone.
