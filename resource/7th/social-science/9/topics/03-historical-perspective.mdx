## What is a Republic?

A **republic** is a form of government in which the head of state is an elected representative rather than a hereditary monarch (like a king or queen).

## Ancient Indian Republics

The voice of the common people has been valued in India since ancient times. We can find distinct examples of early republican systems in India's history.

### The Mahājanapadas
In ancient India, at least two *mahājanapadas* operated as republics. 
*   **The Vajji (or Vṛiji) Mahājanapada:** The **Lichchhavi clan** practised collective decision-making. 
*   Leaders were chosen based on **merit rather than birth**. 
*   Key positions were filled through elections, and representatives from various clans met regularly to address issues and ensure public welfare.

### The Chola Period (10th Century CE)
A remarkable example of local administrative democracy can be seen in the **Uttaramerur inscriptions** from Tamil Nadu.
*   These inscriptions detail the election of members to the village *sabhā* (local administrative body).
*   The process included **sealed ballot boxes**.
*   It explicitly laid out qualifications for members, their duties, and conditions for dismissal (e.g., immediate removal if found indulging in corruption).

## Republics in Other Parts of the World

In the 5th and 4th centuries BCE, **Rome and Greece** also had republics. 
However, these early global republics were often limited:
*   In Greece, while free men could vote, women, workers, and **slaves** could not.
*   Many areas had systems where only a small, privileged group was allowed to vote or make decisions.

<Callout variant="info">
**Slave:** A person who is forced to work for and obey another and is considered to be their property.
