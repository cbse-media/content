## 1. Monarchy

A monarchy is a government ruled by a king or queen (a monarch). They exercise **sovereign** power, and the position is usually hereditary (passed down within the royal family).

<Callout variant="info">
**Sovereign:** Indicates that the source of power of a country is independent from external influence.
</Callout>

### Monarchies in the Past
Historically, kings ruled with immense power, guided by a *sabhā* or a council of ministers. In India, texts like the *Mahābhārata* emphasized that a king's power was bound by *rājadharma*—ruling according to justice and the welfare of the people. However, in many parts of the world, kings held absolute power, sometimes claiming divine rights, and used force to **subjugate** their people.

### Monarchies Today
Today, monarchies generally fall into two categories:

1.  **Absolute Monarchy:** The monarch has complete, unchecked control over making, enforcing, and adjudicating laws. 
    *   *Example:* Saudi Arabia (ruled by a king governed by Islamic law, where advisory councils exist but their advice is not binding).
2.  **Constitutional Monarchy:** The monarch is the head of state but holds only nominal (ceremonial) power. Real executive and legislative powers are held by elected bodies.
    *   *Example:* The United Kingdom (laws are made by an elected parliament, and executive power lies with the Prime Minister).

## 2. Theocracy

A theocracy is a form of government where the country is ruled by religious rules and religious leaders.
*   *Example:* **Iran** combines theocratic and democratic elements. Its Supreme Leader is selected by Islamic clerics for life and holds ultimate authority, though there is also an elected president for everyday governance.
*   *Other Examples:* Afghanistan, Vatican City.

## 3. Dictatorship

A dictatorship is a form of government where one person or a small group possesses **absolute power**, unconstrained by a constitution or laws.
*   **Adolf Hitler (Germany):** Appointed Chancellor in 1933, he passed laws granting himself absolute power. He eliminated opposition and caused World War II and the Holocaust (killing millions of Jews and Roma).
*   **Idi Amin (Uganda):** A military dictator responsible for the merciless killing of thousands. He famously forced many people of Indian descent to flee Uganda.

## 4. Oligarchy

Derived from Greek (*olígos* meaning "few" and *árkhō* meaning "to rule"), an oligarchy is a government where a **small, powerful group** makes all important decisions.
*   These groups are usually wealthy families, influential businesspeople, or top military officials.
*   *Modern Context:* Political commentators warn that even democracies can show signs of oligarchy if a small group of politicians and wealthy elites gain too much influence over governance.
