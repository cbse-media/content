## Introduction

*"A ruler’s duties in the internal administration of the country are three-fold: rakṣha (protection of the state from external aggression), pālana (maintenance of law and order within the state), and yogakṣhema (safeguarding the welfare of the people)."*
— **Kauṭilya in Arthaśhāstra**

Governments play a vital role in our daily lives, from ensuring peace and building infrastructure to managing the economy and protecting citizen rights. However, not all countries are governed in the same way. This chapter explores the diverse structures of governance around the world, historical roots of democratic systems, and how citizens interact with the state.
