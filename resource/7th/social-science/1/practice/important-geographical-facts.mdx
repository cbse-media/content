## Quick Revision Sheet

Use this guide to review the key facts from the chapter before your exams. These make excellent multiple-choice or short-answer questions!

### 1. Mountain Ranges & Peaks
*   **The Himalayas:** Formed by the collision of the Indian landmass (formerly part of Gondwana) and Eurasia about 50 million years ago. They are still growing!
*   **Three Himalayan Ranges:**
    1.  **Himadri:** Greater Himalayas (highest, snow-covered).
    2.  **Himachal:** Lower Himalayas (hill stations).
    3.  **Shivalik:** Outer Himalayas (foothills).
*   **Aravalli Hills:** Among the oldest mountains in the world (approx. 2.5 billion years old). Highest peak is Mount Abu. Acts as a barrier stopping the Thar desert from expanding east.
*   **Western Ghats:** UNESCO World Heritage site, also known as Sahyadri Hills in the north.

### 2. Deserts
*   **Hot Desert:** The Thar Desert (Great Indian Desert). Spans Rajasthan, Gujarat, Punjab, Haryana. Known for sand dunes up to 150m high and *taanka/kunds* (rainwater harvesting).
*   **Cold Desert:** Ladakh. Known as "moonland". Winter temperatures drop below $-30^\circ\text{C}$. Features salty lakes like *Pangong Tso*.

### 3. Rivers and Deltas
*   **Northern Rivers:** Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra. The Bhagirathi originates at Gaumukh (Gangotri Glacier).
*   **Peninsular Rivers (East-flowing):** Godavari, Krishna, Mahanadi, Kaveri (empty into the Bay of Bengal, forming deltas).
*   **Peninsular Rivers (West-flowing):** Narmada, Tapti (empty into the Arabian Sea, forming estuaries).
*   **Sundarbans:** Located in the delta of the Ganga and Brahmaputra. Famous for mangrove forests and the Royal Bengal Tiger.

### 4. Islands
*   **Lakshadweep:** Located in the Arabian Sea. It is an archipelago primarily made up of coral.
*   **Andaman & Nicobar:** Located in the Bay of Bengal. Volcanic islands. Contains the *Cellular Jail* and *Barren Island* (India's only active volcano).
