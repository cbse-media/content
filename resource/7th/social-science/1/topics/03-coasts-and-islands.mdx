## India's Amazing Coastlines

India boasts an extensive coastline of over 7,500 km, dotted with beaches, rocky cliffs, and rich biodiversity.

### The West Coast
*   **Stretch:** From Gujarat to Kerala (passing through Maharashtra, Goa, and Karnataka).
*   **Features:** Rivers originating in the Western Ghats flow swiftly here. The coastline features coves, creeks, and **estuaries** (the largest being formed by the Narmada and Tapti rivers). It is home to ancient, economically vital ports like Mumbai.

### The East Coast
*   **Stretch:** Lies between the Eastern Ghats and the Bay of Bengal, from the Ganga delta to Kanyakumari.
*   **Features:** Characterized by wide plains and major river **deltas** (formed by the Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, and Kaveri). 
*   **Water Bodies:** It hosts important lagoons and lakes like Chilika Lake and Pulicat Lake.

## Indian Islands

India's territory includes two major island archipelagos scattered across its surrounding oceans.

### 1. Lakshadweep Islands
*   **Location:** Arabian Sea, close to the Malabar coast of Kerala.
*   **Features:** An archipelago of 36 islands primarily made of **coral**. They are known for their pristine marine environments and coral reefs. Not all islands are inhabited.

### 2. Andaman and Nicobar Islands
*   **Location:** Bay of Bengal.
*   **Features:** Comprises over 500 large and small **volcanic islands**.
*   **Significance:** 
    *   *Historical:* The infamous 'Cellular Jail' is located here, where many Indian freedom fighters were imprisoned.
    *   *Geological:* Home to **Barren Island**, which is the *only active volcano* in India.

## The Delta in West Bengal and the Sundarbans

As we follow the coastline eastward, we reach the unique region where the Ganga and Brahmaputra rivers meet the Bay of Bengal.

<Callout variant="tip">
**What is a Delta?**
A delta is a triangular or fan-shaped landform created at the mouth of a river as it deposits sediments into a larger body of water (like an ocean).
</Callout>

*   **The Sundarbans:** This vast delta region is shared between India (West Bengal) and Bangladesh. It is a UNESCO Heritage site.
*   **Biodiversity:** It features dense mangrove forests uniquely adapted to thrive where fresh river water meets salty seawater. It is the famous natural habitat of the **Royal Bengal Tiger**.
