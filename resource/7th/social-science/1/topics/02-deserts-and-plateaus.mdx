## The Great Indian Desert (Thar Desert)

In the western part of India lies the Thar Desert, a vast expanse of golden sand dunes, rugged terrain, and wide-open skies.
*   **Location:** Spans across the states of Rajasthan, Gujarat, Punjab, and Haryana.
*   **Features:** Wind shifts and shapes the sand into hill-like dunes that can rise as high as 150 meters.
*   **Climate:** Very high daytime temperatures, cold nights, and severe water scarcity.
*   **Adaptation:** People have adapted to these harsh conditions. To conserve water, traditional rainwater harvesting systems like **taanka** or **kunds** are widely used. Utensils are often scoured with sand instead of water to save every drop.

## The Aravalli Hills

Tracing eastward from the Thar Desert, we find the Aravalli Hills.
*   **Age:** They are among the oldest fold mountains in the world, estimated to be around 2.5 billion years old!
*   **Highest Peak:** Mount Abu (over 1700 m).
*   **Geographical Role:** They act as a natural barrier, preventing the Thar Desert from expanding further eastward.
*   **Resources:** Rich in minerals like marble, granite, zinc, and copper. Evidence from Zawar shows that Indians mastered the delicate extraction of zinc over eight centuries ago.

## The Peninsular Plateau

The southern part of India is a large, triangular plateau bounded by water on three sides (Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean).

*   **Boundaries:** Bordered by the **Western Ghats** (taller, continuous, UNESCO World Heritage Site, also known as Sahyadri Hills) and the **Eastern Ghats** (lower and broken). The vast flat highlands between them form the **Deccan Plateau**.
*   **Rivers:** The plateau tilts slightly to the east.
    *   *East-flowing rivers (into Bay of Bengal):* Godavari, Krishna, Mahanadi, Kaveri.
    *   *West-flowing rivers (into Arabian Sea):* Narmada, Tapti.
*   **Resources:** Rich in minerals (like coal), dense forests, and fertile land. The uneven rocky surfaces create beautiful waterfalls (like Jog Falls) which are used for hydroelectric power generation.
*   **People:** Home to numerous tribal communities closely connected to nature, such as the Santhal, Gond, Baiga, Bhil, and Korku.

## The Hills of the Northeast

Moving to the northeastern tip of India, we find a region defined by lush greenery, heavy rainfall, and breathtaking waterfalls.
*   **Features:** The Meghalaya Plateau includes the Garo, Khasi, and Jantia hills.
*   **Culture & Adaptations:** Known for unique, sustainable practices like the **living root bridges** (created by weaving tree roots over many years) and Mawlynnong, celebrated as the "cleanest village in Asia."
