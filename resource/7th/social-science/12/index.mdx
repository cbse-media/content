## Introduction

*"Prosperity emanates from the market that develops when people need goods and services that they can’t create themselves."*
— **Adam Smith**, 18th-century economist

Every day, we consume various goods—vegetables, fruits, clothes, groceries, mobile phones, and refrigerators. These goods are the result of primary, secondary, and tertiary economic activities. But how do these goods reach us from the factories and farms where they are produced? The answer lies in **markets**.

![A bustling Indian physical market with vendors selling vegetables, fruits, and clothes](./placeholder.png)

## The Big Questions

In this chapter, we will explore the answers to these fundamental questions:
1. What are markets and how do they function?
2. What is the role of markets in people's lives?
3. What role does the government play in markets?
4. How can consumers assess the quality of goods and services they purchase?
