## 1. Physical and Online Markets

Markets are present everywhere and take various forms.

### Physical Markets
A physical market is where buyers can meet the seller physically and purchase goods or services in exchange for money. 
*   **Examples:** Weekly markets, *haats* (vendors selling vegetables on carts), local neighborhood shops, and large multi-storey malls in cities.

### Online Markets
Today, buyers and sellers do not necessarily need to meet in person. They can transact from thousands of kilometers away using shopping applications (apps) or websites.
*   Goods (like books, clothes, electronics) are delivered to the doorstep.
*   **Inputs for production:** Manufacturers can also buy components online to be used to make other goods.
*   Services (like online classes) can also be purchased and consumed digitally.

<Callout variant="info">
**Manufacturer:** A person or company that makes goods for sale.
**Inputs for production:** Materials or resources that are used in the production of goods and services.
</Callout>

### Other Types of Markets
Not all markets transact physical goods. The **Stock Market** (or share market) is a place where people buy and sell ownership shares of companies!

## 2. Domestic and International Markets

### Domestic Markets
A market where goods and services are bought and sold **within the geographical boundaries** of a country is called a domestic market. 
*   *Example:* To print your textbook, paper was procured from mills located inside India.

### International Markets
International markets exist **outside** a nation's boundary. Trade occurs across the borders of different countries.
*   **Export:** Selling goods or services produced in one country to a buyer in another country. (e.g., India selling software to North America).
*   **Import:** Buying goods or services from other countries and bringing them into one's own country. (e.g., India buying palm oil from Malaysia).

<Callout variant="tip">
**Did you know?**
India is the world's largest importer of vegetable oils like palm oil, sunflower oil, and soybean oil! Most of this comes from Southeast Asian countries.
</Callout>
