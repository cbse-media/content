## Defining the Market

A place where people buy and sell goods is called a **market**. Depending on the region, it is also known as a *bazaar*, *haat* (in Hindi), or *mārukatté* (in Kannada). 

Markets can be physical places or online platforms. They exist to fulfil our basic survival requirements and our personal desires.

<Callout variant="info">
**Key Economic Terms:**
*   **Needs:** Something a person requires to survive, such as food, water, clothing, and shelter.
*   **Wants:** Something a person desires but is not essential for survival (e.g., a new video game or designer shoes).
*   **Trade:** The buying and selling or exchange of goods and services between people or countries.
</Callout>

Through markets, goods and services become available to individuals, households, and businesses. Furthermore, markets do more than just exchange money; they connect people, traditions, and ideas.

## A Glimpse into History: The Glorious Hampi Bazaar

To understand how central markets have always been to society, let's look at a famous market from 16th-century India: the **Hampi Bazaar** in Karnataka.

![The ruins of Hampi Bazaar today](./placeholder.png)

*   **Location:** It was located opposite the Virupaksha temple in the Vijayanagara Empire.
*   **Prosperity:** It was the center of flourishing trade. The famous Portuguese traveller, Domingos Paes, called Hampi "the best-provided city in the world."
*   **Goods Traded:** A massive variety of products were traded here, including grains, seeds, milk, oil, silk, animals (cows, rabbits, horses), birds, precious stones (rubies, diamonds, pearls), and cotton cloths.
*   **Significance:** The sheer abundance described by foreign travellers highlights how a well-functioning market can bring immense prosperity to an entire region.
