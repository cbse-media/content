## 1. Physical vs. Online Shopping (Page 255)

**THINK ABOUT IT:** *What do you think are the pros and cons of online and physical shopping respectively? Explore this question from the point of view of both sellers and buyers.*

**Discussion:**

**From the Buyer's Viewpoint:**
*   **Online Pros:** Ultimate convenience (shopping from bed), huge variety of choices, easy to compare prices, doorstep delivery.
*   **Online Cons:** Cannot touch, feel, or try on the product (like clothes or shoes), delivery takes time, risk of frauds or damaged goods.
*   **Physical Pros:** Immediate possession of the item, ability to check quality/fit in person, enjoyable shopping experience with friends.
*   **Physical Cons:** Takes travel time and effort, limited to the stock available in that specific shop, prices might be higher due to shop rent.

**From the Seller's Viewpoint:**
*   **Online Pros:** Access to millions of customers globally, no need to pay high rent for a fancy shop in a mall.
*   **Online Cons:** High competition, have to pay aggregator commissions, handling returns and shipping logistics can be tough.
*   **Physical Pros:** Can build direct, loyal relationships with local customers, zero shipping costs.
*   **Physical Cons:** Reach is limited only to people living nearby, high costs for electricity, rent, and store maintenance.

## 2. In-Person Services (Page 255)

**THINK ABOUT IT:** *Some services require in-person contact like tailoring and cannot be provided online. Can you suggest other services where physical markets are needed?*

**Discussion:**
Physical presence is absolutely necessary for services that involve physical care, immediate spatial interaction, or machinery that cannot be transported. Examples include:
*   **Healthcare:** Dental checkups, surgeries, physical therapy.
*   **Personal Grooming:** Haircuts (salons), massages, manicures.
*   **Repair Services:** Fixing a flat tire on a car, repairing a broken refrigerator.
*   **Hospitality & Entertainment:** Eating at a fine-dining restaurant, visiting a theme park, or watching a movie in a cinema hall.

## 3. Why Prices Fluctuate (Page 254)

**THINK ABOUT IT:** *Vegetables are sold cheaper late at night at the weekly market compared to during the day. Why? Garment stores offer heavy discounts on woollen clothing at the end of the winter season. Why?*

**Discussion:**
*   **Vegetables at Night:** Vegetables are perishable goods (they spoil quickly). If the seller doesn't sell them by the end of the night, they will rot and become worthless. Therefore, to ensure they sell their remaining supply, they drastically lower the price to attract last-minute buyers.
*   **Winter Clothes:** At the end of winter, the *demand* for woollen clothes drops to almost zero. Shopkeepers need to clear their limited warehouse space to bring in summer clothes. To encourage people to buy items they won't need for another 9 months, sellers drop the prices heavily.

## 4. Onion Prices and Government Intervention (Page 266)

**LET'S EXPLORE:** *In some seasons, the supply of onions comes down in the market. What happens to the price? What should the government do?*

**Discussion:**
*   **Price Change:** When the supply of onions drops drastically (due to bad weather, crop failure, or hoarding) but the demand remains high (since it's a staple food), the **price shoots up rapidly**. 
*   **Government Action:** In this situation, the government should intervene by:
    1. Releasing onions from its own emergency buffer stocks into the market to increase supply.
    2. Banning the *export* of onions to keep domestic supply high.
    3. Putting a cap on how many onions a wholesaler can store, preventing greedy traders from "hoarding" (hiding) onions to artificially drive the price up even further.
