## Recognizing the Divine in Nature

In many Indian belief systems, elements of Nature are not seen as resources to be exploited, but as organs of the supreme Lord's body. Geography, culture, and spirituality fuse together.

### Sacred Rivers and Sangams
Rivers have been worshipped as lifelines of civilization since Vedic times. The *nadīstuti sūkta* of the Ṛigveda invokes the 19 major rivers of the northwest. Today, daily rituals still invoke the presence of the Ganga, Yamuna, Godavari, and Kaveri.
Rivers are often regarded as goddesses (*devīs*), and the places where they merge (*sangams*) are especially holy.

<Callout variant="info">
**The Kumbh Mela**
Hosted every six years in Prayagraj at the confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and the invisible Sarasvati, the Kumbh Mela is the largest peaceful gathering of humans on Earth. It originates from the legend of the churning of the cosmic ocean (*amṛita manthana*).
</Callout>

### Sacred Mountains
Because of their towering height, mountains are universally seen as symbolic gateways from earth to heaven. 
*   **Pilgrimage:** Shrines like Sabarimala (Kerala) or Vaishno Devi (Katra) require arduous treks through mountain trails, testing a pilgrim's mental and physical strength.
*   **Tribal Beliefs:** Tribal communities often regard local hills as the abodes of their supreme deities. For example, the Dongria Khond tribe reveres the Niyam Dongar hill in Jharkhand. Cutting trees there is strictly prohibited.

### Sacred Trees and Groves
Specific trees, like the 'peepul' or 'bodhi tree' (*Ficus religiosa*), are sacred across Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism.

**Sacred Groves** (*Kāvu*, *Devarāī*, *Dev van*):
Many rural and tribal communities dedicate patches of natural forest to local deities. Because these forests are considered the abode of the gods, hunting, tree felling, and mining are strictly forbidden. 
As a result of this spiritual protection, sacred groves have become vital sanctuaries for biodiversity, protecting rare plants, animals, and water sources for centuries!
