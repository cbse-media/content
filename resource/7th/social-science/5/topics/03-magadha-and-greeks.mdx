## The Ascendancy of Magadha

Among the 16 *mahājanapadas*, **Magadha** (in modern-day Bihar) rose to absolute dominance between the 6th and 4th centuries BCE. 

**Why Magadha?**
1.  **Geography:** It was located in the highly fertile Ganga plains, producing massive agricultural surpluses.
2.  **Resources:** It had access to dense forests (providing timber and war elephants) and crucial **iron ore mines** in nearby hills, allowing them to forge superior weapons.
3.  **Transport:** The Ganga and Son rivers provided natural, cheap highways for trade and troop movement.

Under the powerful **Nanda Dynasty** (founded by Mahāpadma Nanda), Magadha conquered neighboring kingdoms, amassed vast wealth, and maintained a terrifyingly large army.

## The Arrival of the Greeks

While Magadha ruled the East, the Northwest of India (Punjab/Sindh) was fractured into smaller kingdoms. 

In **327 BCE**, the young Macedonian king, **Alexander the Great**, having conquered the vast Persian Empire, marched into India. 
*   He defeated local rulers, including King Porus at the Battle of the Hydaspes.
*   However, after brutal fighting, his exhausted, homesick soldiers refused to march further east towards the mighty Nanda Empire in the Ganga plains.
*   Alexander retreated, leaving behind Greek governors called **satraps** to rule the conquered Indian territories.

<Callout variant="info">
**Cultural Exchange:**
While Alexander's political control over India was brief, his invasion opened up permanent trade and cultural contacts between India and the Greek (Hellenistic) world, profoundly influencing art and philosophy in the centuries to come.
