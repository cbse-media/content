## The Fall of the Nandas

The last Nanda emperor, Dhana Nanda, was incredibly rich but highly unpopular due to his oppressive taxation. According to legend, he insulted a brilliant teacher from Taxila University named **Kauṭilya** (also known as Chāṇakya). Kauṭilya vowed to destroy the Nanda dynasty.

## The Rise of Chandragupta Maurya

Around **321 BCE**, Kauṭilya found a capable protégé: **Chandragupta Maurya**. Together, they:
1.  Overthrew the Nanda Empire and captured the capital, Pāṭaliputra.
2.  Marched northwest and defeated the Greek *satraps* left behind by Alexander.
3.  Unified the subcontinent into the massive **Mauryan Empire**.

## Kauṭilya's Blueprint: The Arthaśhāstra

Chandragupta's success was largely due to Kauṭilya's genius. Kauṭilya wrote the **Arthaśhāstra**, a masterclass manual on the "science of governance and economics." 

### The Saptāṅga Theory
Kauṭilya stated that a strong state is made of seven essential limbs (*saptāṅga*):
1.  **Swāmi:** The King.
2.  **Amātya:** The Ministers and officials.
3.  **Janapada:** The territory and its population.
4.  **Durga:** Fortified towns and cities.
5.  **Koṣha:** The treasury/wealth.
6.  **Daṇḍa:** The army and forces of law.
7.  **Mitra:** Allies.

<Callout variant="tip">
**The Welfare State:**
Despite focusing on strict law, order, and military defense, Kauṭilya's ultimate philosophy was remarkably modern: *"In the happiness of his subjects lies the king's happiness... he must give first place to the people's interests."* He heavily promoted building infrastructure in the countryside to boost the economy.
</Callout>

## Life in the Mauryan Period
Under the Mauryas, cities like Pāṭaliputra thrived. Agriculture was robust (with two crops sown a year), granaries were stocked for famine relief, and trade was highly organized with well-planned streets, guilds, and coinage.
