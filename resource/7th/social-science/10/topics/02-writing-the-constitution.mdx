## The Need to Plan for the Future

While India was struggling for independence from British rule, leaders realised they needed a plan for how Indians would govern themselves once free. India was a massive, culturally diverse, and complex country. 

They had to answer big questions:
*   What type of government should we have?
*   Who should have the right to vote?
*   How do we ensure fundamental rights for every citizen?

## The Constituent Assembly

To answer these questions, a **Constituent Assembly** was formed in **1946**. 

*   **Members:** It initially had 389 members, which was reduced to 299 after the Partition of India. It included 15 women. 
*   **Diversity:** These members represented India’s diverse regions, professions, religions, and social groups. They were elected by the legislative assemblies of the provinces.
*   **Leadership:** **Dr. Rajendra Prasad** was the Chairman of the Constituent Assembly.

## The Drafting Process and Timeline

The Constitution was not written overnight. It took the Constituent Assembly **almost three years** of rigorous debate and discussion to develop it.

1.  **Formation:** The Constituent Assembly was formed on **9 December 1946**.
2.  **Drafting Committee:** The initial text was prepared by a Drafting Committee chaired by **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar**, an eminent social reformer and independent India's first Law and Justice Minister.
3.  **Completion:** The work was completed, and the Constitution was adopted on **26 November 1949**.
4.  **Coming into Effect:** The Constitution legally came into effect on **26 January 1950**. We celebrate this historic day annually as **Republic Day**.

**World's Largest Constitution:**
The Constitution of India is the world’s largest written constitution. When it came into effect in 1950, it had 22 Parts and 8 Schedules. Today, it has grown to have 25 Parts and 12 Schedules!
