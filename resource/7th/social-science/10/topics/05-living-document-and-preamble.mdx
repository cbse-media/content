## The Constitution is a Living Document

A "living document" is one that can grow, adapt, and change with the times. Our Constitution makers realised that society would change, and new rules would be needed in the future. 

Changes made to the Constitution are called **Amendments**. 
However, changing the Constitution is not easy. It requires rigorous debate in the Parliament, and sometimes in State Legislative Assemblies as well. Often, public opinion is sought. 

**Examples of Amendments:**
*   **1976:** 'Part IV-A: Fundamental Duties' was added. Also, the terms 'Socialist' and 'Secular' were added to the Preamble (42nd Amendment).
*   **1992:** The Panchayati Raj System, which was not part of the original structure, was integrated via the $73^{\text{rd}}$ Constitutional Amendment Act.
*   **2004:** The Supreme Court interpreted that flying the National Flag is a Fundamental Right to Freedom of Expression, changing an old rule that prevented citizens from flying it at home.

## The Preamble: The Guiding Values

The essence, or the "soul", of the Constitution is written in its introduction, known as the **Preamble**. It lists the core values that guide government policies and citizen behaviour. 

![Preamble of the Indian Constitution](./placeholder.png)

Here is what the key terms in the Preamble mean:

*   **WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA:** The Constitution gets its power from the citizens, not from a king or an outside power.
*   **SOVEREIGN:** India is independent. The people and the government have the supreme right to make decisions on internal and external matters without outside interference.
*   **SOCIALIST:** Wealth is generated socially and should be shared equally. The government should reduce socio-economic inequalities.
*   **SECULAR:** Citizens have complete freedom to follow any religion. There is no official state religion, and the government treats all beliefs with equal respect.
*   **DEMOCRATIC:** A government where people elect their rulers, hold them accountable, and enjoy equal political rights.
*   **REPUBLIC:** The head of the state (the President) is an elected person, not a hereditary monarch (like a King or Queen).
*   **JUSTICE:** Citizens cannot be discriminated against based on caste, religion, or gender. The government must work for the welfare of all, especially disadvantaged groups.
*   **LIBERTY:** Citizens have the freedom to think, express their thoughts, and act upon them, provided there are no unreasonable restrictions.
*   **EQUALITY:** All are equal before the law. The government must ensure equal opportunities for all.
*   **FRATERNITY:** All citizens should behave like members of the same family, treating fellow citizens with dignity and never as inferiors.
