## The Structure of Government

The Constitution comprehensively addresses how India should be governed to ensure accountability and efficiency.

### 1. Separation of Powers
There are three organs of government, and their powers are kept separate so that no single body becomes too powerful:
*   **Legislature:** Makes the laws.
*   **Executive:** Implements the laws (headed by the Prime Minister).
*   **Judiciary:** Ensures laws are in accordance with the Constitution, decides if a law is broken, and hands out punishment.

### 2. Three-Tier System
India has a three-tier system of government:
1.  **Central (Union) Government**
2.  **State Governments**
3.  **Local Governments** (Panchayati Raj system for villages, Municipalities for cities).
The Constitution clearly divides the functions and responsibilities between the Central and State governments.

## The Core Soul: Rights, Duties, and Directives

The Constitution establishes the relationship between the citizens and the state through three crucial segments:

### Fundamental Rights
These are basic rights given to every citizen to ensure a life of dignity. They are promises that **must be kept** by the state. If someone treats you unfairly or violates these rights, you can go to court for justice.
*   *Examples:* Right to Equality (Article 14), Right to Freedom (Article 21), Right to Education (Article 21-A).

### Fundamental Duties
Added later to the Constitution, these outline the moral obligations of citizens to help build a strong nation.
*   *Examples:* To abide by the Constitution and respect the National Flag/Anthem; to protect the natural environment; to value our rich cultural heritage.

### Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP)
These are **goals and guidelines** that the government *should* try to achieve to make India better for all (like social and economic justice). 
*   *Key Difference:* Unlike Fundamental Rights, if the government fails to achieve a DPSP immediately (like providing a perfect standard of living for everyone), you cannot go to a judge and complain. They are long-term visions.
*   *Examples:* Welfare Government (Article 41), Uniform Civil Code (Article 44), Protection of monuments (Article 49).

<Callout variant="tip">
**Why the Difference?**
The framers of the Constitution created this system on purpose. They wanted some rights to be demanded immediately (Fundamental Rights) and set bigger, long-term goals (Directive Principles) that would take time to achieve as the newly independent country progressed economically.
