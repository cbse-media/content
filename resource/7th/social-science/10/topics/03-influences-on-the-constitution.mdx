## A Tapestry of Influences

The Indian Constitution was entirely written by Indians for Indians, but it drew inspiration from several important sources. The framers looked at India's past, its recent struggles, and the best practices from around the world.

### 1. Influence of the Indian Freedom Movement

The freedom struggle against British rule was fought on the principles of dignity, equality, and self-rule. Many leaders of the freedom movement became members of the Constituent Assembly. They ensured that the ideals they fought for were enshrined in the Constitution.

Key values brought from the freedom struggle include:
*   **Equality for all**
*   **Justice for all**
*   **Freedom and Fraternity**
*   **Universal Adult Franchise** (giving every adult citizen the right to vote, to ensure the voices of all Indians were heard).

### 2. India’s Civilisational Heritage and History

In Grade 6, you learned about "Unity in Diversity." This core idea is deeply embedded in the Constitution. Our ancient texts and traditions provided fundamental principles:

*   **Acceptance of different viewpoints.**
*   The concepts of **_vasudhaiva kutumbakam_** ("the world is one family") and **_sarve bhavantu sukhinah_** (an invocation for the well-being of all creatures).
*   Earlier governance systems like *janapadas*, *sanghas*, and Kauṭilya’s concept of *rājadharma* emphasized the duties and welfare of the people. This ancient emphasis on duties inspired the inclusion of **Fundamental Duties** in the Constitution.

<Callout variant="info">
**Art in the Constitution:**
The original Constitution is a beautiful work of art. Calligrapher **Prem Behari Narain Raizada** wrote its text by hand. **Nandalal Bose** and his team illustrated its pages with scenes from Indian history, such as ancient gurukulas, scenes from the *Rāmāyaṇa* and *Mahābhārata*, and the ancient university of Nālandā.
</Callout>

### 3. Learnings from Across the World

The Indian tradition says, *“ā no bhadrāh kratavo yantu viśwatah”* ("Let noble thoughts come to me from every side"). Following this, the Constitution makers studied the constitutions of democratic nations to see what worked best.

| Country | Adopted Principles |
| :--- | :--- |
| **France** | The ideals of **Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity** (which originated during the 1789 French Revolution). |
| **Ireland** | The idea of **Directive Principles of State Policy** (goals for the government to achieve). |
| **USA** | The concept of an **Independent Judiciary** and Fundamental Rights. |

By blending our own civilisational wisdom with the hard-earned lessons of our freedom struggle and global democratic practices, the framers created a robust and unique Constitution.
