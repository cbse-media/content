## Questions and Activities

**1. “The Constituent Assembly had representatives from diverse backgrounds in India.” Why do you think it was important to have a diverse set of representatives from all over India?**

**Answer:**
India is a vast country with immense diversity in religion, language, culture, caste, and economic backgrounds. Having a diverse set of representatives was crucial because:
1.  It ensured that the voices, concerns, and needs of all communities (including minorities and disadvantaged groups) were heard and addressed.
2.  It helped in making a Constitution that was fair, inclusive, and acceptable to every citizen of the newly independent nation.
3.  It fostered a sense of unity and belonging among all Indians, ensuring that the Constitution was truly made by "We, the People."

**2. Read the statements below carefully and identify which key features/values in the Constitution of India are reflected in each statement.**

**a. Sheena, Rajat, and Harsh are standing in a line. They are excited to cast their first vote in the general elections.**
**Answer:** Democratic / Universal Adult Franchise / Political Equality.

**b. Radha, Imon, and Harpreet study in the same class in the same school.**
**Answer:** Equality / Right to Education (Article 21-A) / Secularism (people of different backgrounds studying together).

**c. Parents must make arrangements to ensure their children’s education.**
**Answer:** Fundamental Duties (Specifically: *to provide opportunities for education to his or her child between the age of six and fourteen years*).

**d. People of all castes, genders, and religions can use the village well.**
**Answer:** Equality / Justice / Abolition of Discrimination.

**3. It is said that ‘All citizens in India are equal before the law’. Do you think this is a fact? If yes, why? If not, why not? Formulate your arguments.**

**Answer:**
*   **In Principle (Yes):** Constitutionally, it is a absolute fact. Article 14 guarantees equality before the law. The courts treat everyone—from the Prime Minister to a common citizen—under the same legal rules. If a law is broken, the punishment is the same regardless of the person's caste, wealth, or religion.
*   **In Practice (Challenges):** While the law is equal, accessing justice can sometimes be difficult in reality. Poor or uneducated citizens may struggle to afford good lawyers or understand the complex legal system compared to wealthy and influential people. Therefore, while legal equality exists as a fact on paper, achieving absolute socio-economic equality in accessing that law requires continuous effort.

**4. You have learnt that ‘India is the only country that provided universal adult franchise to its citizens from the beginning.’ Can you explain why India did it?**

**Answer:**
India provided Universal Adult Franchise from the very beginning because the Indian freedom struggle was a mass movement fought by people of all classes, castes, and genders. The leaders believed deeply in the principle of equality. They felt it would be highly unjust to deny the right to vote to the poor or uneducated after they had sacrificed equally for the country's freedom. They trusted the wisdom of the common Indian citizen to choose their leaders.

**5. How did the freedom struggle inspire the making of the Constitution of India? How did India’s civilisational heritage inspire some of the key features in the Constitution of India? Explain.**

**Answer:**
*   **Freedom Struggle Inspiration:** The freedom struggle was fought against inequality, exploitation, and lack of liberty. This inspired the inclusion of **Fundamental Rights** (like freedom of speech and right against exploitation). The demand for self-rule led to the creation of a **Democratic Republic**. The unity seen during the movement inspired the ideals of **Fraternity and Equality**.
*   **Civilisational Heritage Inspiration:** India's ancient values of accepting different viewpoints and the concept of *vasudhaiva kutumbakam* (the world is one family) inspired the secular and inclusive nature of the Constitution. The ancient emphasis on *dharma* and the duties of the people towards society and nature inspired the inclusion of **Fundamental Duties**.

**6. Do you think we, as a society, have achieved all the ideals of the Constitution? If not, what can we each do as citizens to move our country closer to these ideals?**

**Answer:**
No, we have not completely achieved all the ideals. While we have a strong democracy and have made great progress, challenges like poverty, caste-based discrimination, gender inequality, and corruption still exist, which go against the ideals of Justice, Equality, and Socialism.

**What we can do as citizens:**
1.  **Perform Fundamental Duties:** Respect the law, protect the environment, and promote harmony.
2.  **Treat everyone equally:** Do not discriminate against anyone based on religion, gender, or caste in our daily lives.
3.  **Stay Informed and Vote:** Choose responsible leaders who work for the welfare of the people.
4.  **Stand against injustice:** Raise our voices when we see someone's rights being violated.
