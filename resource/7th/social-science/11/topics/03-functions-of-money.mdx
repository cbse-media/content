## "Necessity is the Mother of Invention"

As human societies grew and trade expanded across longer distances, the barter system became too inefficient. People needed a common system that everyone agreed upon. This led to the creation of **money**.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Money:** The common tool that everybody accepts and uses in order to make or receive payments in exchange for goods and services.
</Callout>

Money perfectly solves the five limitations of the barter system through its four primary functions:

### 1. Medium of Exchange
Instead of looking for a double coincidence of wants, you can simply sell your goods for money, and then use that money to buy whatever you want from anyone. Since everyone accepts money, it makes trade incredibly smooth.
*   *Solves: Double coincidence of wants.*

### 2. Store of Value
Unlike wheat that rots or cattle that die, money does not spoil. A farmer can sell his crops, receive money, and keep that money safe in a bank or at home for years. He can use this stored value for purchases far in the future.
*   *Solves: Problem of durability.*

### 3. Common Denomination (Measure of Value)
Money acts as a universal measuring tape for value. You don't have to calculate how many shoes equal one ox. Instead, everything is priced in a common unit (like Rupees). If shoes are ₹500 and an ox is ₹50,000, you immediately understand their relative values.
*   *Solves: Lack of a common standard measure of value.*

### 4. Standard of Deferred Payment
Money allows for borrowing and lending. If you have ₹50 today but a book costs ₹100, money allows you to make a partial payment now and promise to pay the remaining ₹50 later. Because money holds its value, shopkeepers are willing to accept deferred (delayed) payments.
