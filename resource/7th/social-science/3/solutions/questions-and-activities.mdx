## 2. Answer the following questions:

**a) What is the difference between weather and climate?**
*   **Answer:** *Weather* refers to the short-term, day-to-day conditions of the atmosphere at a specific place (e.g., it is raining today). *Climate* is the long-term average pattern of weather in a particular region over several decades (e.g., the desert has an arid climate).

**b) Why do places near the ocean have milder temperatures than places far away from it?**
*   **Answer:** Water heats up and cools down much slower than land. Because of this, the ocean acts as a temperature moderator. During the summer, cool sea breezes keep the coast from getting too hot. During the winter, the ocean retains heat and keeps the coast from freezing. Inland areas do not have this moderating effect and experience extreme temperature swings.

**c) What role do monsoon winds play in affecting India’s climate?**
*   **Answer:** Monsoon winds are the engine of India's climate. The Southwest Summer Monsoon brings heavy, life-sustaining rainfall to the entire subcontinent, dictating the agricultural calendar and filling rivers. The Northeast Winter Monsoon brings dry weather to most of India, but provides crucial winter rainfall to the eastern coast.

**d) Why is Chennai warm or hot throughout the year, while Leh is cold?**
*   **Answer:** This is due to a combination of **Latitude** and **Altitude**. 
    *   **Chennai** is located at a *low latitude* (very close to the Equator), meaning it receives intense, direct sunlight year-round. It is also at sea level.
    *   **Leh** is located at a *high latitude* (far north) and at an extremely *high altitude* in the Himalayas. The oblique angle of the sun's rays, combined with the thin, low-pressure mountain air, results in freezing temperatures.

## 3. Look at a map of India. Identify the climate for these cities:

*   **Leh (Ladakh):** In the high mountains. **Climate:** Cold Desert / Alpine. Extremely cold and dry.
*   **Chennai (Tamil Nadu):** Near the sea, close to the equator. **Climate:** Tropical Wet / Coastal. Hot, humid, and receives winter monsoon rain.
*   **Delhi:** Inland plains, far from the sea. **Climate:** Subtropical / Continental. Extreme temperatures (scorching summers, cold winters).
*   **Panaji (Goa):** Western coast near the sea. **Climate:** Tropical Wet. Warm, highly humid, very heavy summer monsoon rains.
*   **Jaipur (Rajasthan):** Inland, near the desert. **Climate:** Semi-Arid / Arid. Very hot summers, low rainfall, dry winds.

## 7. Identify a natural disaster and write a short essay including causes, impacts, and preventive actions.

*Example Essay Topic: Floods*

**Causes:** Floods occur when water overflows into normally dry land. This is primarily caused by excessively heavy monsoon rainfall that the soil cannot absorb fast enough. In cities, poorly planned construction and concrete surfaces block natural drainage. In the Himalayas, glacial bursts or rapid snowmelt can cause flash floods.
**Impacts:** Floods have devastating consequences. They result in the loss of human and animal life, destroy crops leading to food shortages, wash away roads and bridges, and cause severe soil erosion.
**Actions to Reduce Impact:**
*   **Individuals:** Avoid building houses on natural floodplains. Keep emergency kits ready during monsoon season.
*   **Communities:** Maintain local drainage systems and avoid throwing plastic waste in rivers which causes blockages.
*   **Government:** Build stronger embankments and dams. Plant massive numbers of trees (afforestation) along riverbanks to hold the soil and absorb excess water. Improve early warning systems via the IMD to evacuate people in time.
