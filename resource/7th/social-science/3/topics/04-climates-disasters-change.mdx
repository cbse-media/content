## Climates and Disasters

India’s diverse weather patterns can sometimes bring extreme conditions that result in natural disasters. The National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) is trained to rescue and evacuate people during these events.

1.  **Cyclones:** Massive, violently rotating storms that form over warm oceans (like the Bay of Bengal) in intense low-pressure systems. They cause devastating winds, flooding, and storm surges along the eastern coast.
2.  **Floods:** Occur when heavy monsoon rains generate more run-off water than the land or rivers can absorb. Himalayan regions also face "glacial bursts" where melting glaciers break their ice barriers, sending massive walls of water down valleys (e.g., the 2013 Kedarnath disaster).
3.  **Landslides:** The sudden collapse of rock or soil on mountain slopes. Triggered by heavy rain, earthquakes, or human activities like deforestation and poorly planned road construction.
4.  **Forest Fires:** Uncontrolled fires fuelled by dry conditions, high winds, and often human carelessness. They destroy ecosystems and cause severe air pollution.

## Global Climate Change

The Earth's climate has always changed naturally over millions of years. However, since the 19th century, **human activities** have caused the climate to change at a dangerous, unnatural speed.

### The Greenhouse Effect
Certain gases in the atmosphere (like Carbon Dioxide, $CO_2$) trap heat from the Sun. This is a natural process that keeps Earth warm enough for life.

### The Problem: Fossil Fuels
Humans burn massive amounts of **fossil fuels** (coal, petroleum, natural gas) for industry, electricity, and transportation. 
*   Burning these fuels releases enormous amounts of extra $CO_2$ into the atmosphere.
*   This sudden increase traps too much heat, causing **Global Warming**.

### Consequences and Solutions
Rising temperatures are disrupting the climate patterns that human agriculture relies on. We are experiencing shorter winters, extreme heat waves, erratic monsoons, and melting glaciers.

**What can be done?**
Governments and communities must focus on:
*   **Mitigation:** Taking steps to slow down global warming. This includes cutting down greenhouse gas emissions, stopping deforestation, and switching to renewable energy (solar, wind).
*   **Resilience and Adaptation:** Building infrastructure and agricultural practices that can withstand extreme weather events.
