## The Difference Between Weather and Climate

People often use the words 'weather' and 'climate' interchangeably, but they mean very different things!

*   **Weather:** This is what you experience right now. It can change from hour to hour or day to day. It might be raining in the morning, sunny in the afternoon, and windy at night. 
*   **Climate:** This is the *pattern* of weather an area experiences over a very long period of time (usually 30 years or more). While the weather in a desert might occasionally be rainy, the *climate* of the desert is "Arid" (dry).

## The Rhythm of Seasons

Seasons occur in a yearly cycle as the Earth revolves around the Sun. The weather changes with the season (e.g., hot in summer, cold in winter). 

Traditionally, in many parts of India, the year is divided into **six seasons** (or *ṛitus*):
1.  **Vasanta** (Spring)
2.  **Grīṣhma** (Summer)
3.  **Varṣhā** (Rainy season / Monsoon)
4.  **Śharad** (Autumn)
5.  **Hemanta** (Pre-winter)
6.  **Śhiśhir** (Winter)

Our entire culture, agriculture, food habits, and festivals (like *Vasanta Pañchamī* or *Onam*) are deeply intertwined with this rhythm of seasons.

## Types of Climates in India

Because of its massive size and geographical diversity, India experiences several distinct climate types:

*   **Alpine:** High in the Himalayas. Extremely cold, snowy winters and cool summers.
*   **Temperate:** Lower Himalayas and hill stations. Moderately cold winters, not too hot summers.
*   **Subtropical:** Northern plains (like Punjab/Delhi). Very hot summers and cold winters. Great for growing wheat.
*   **Arid:** Thar Desert. Extremely hot days, cool nights, and very little rainfall.
*   **Tropical Wet:** Western coastal strip and Northeast. Heavy rainfall during monsoons, great for rice and spices.
*   **Semi-Arid:** Central Deccan Plateau. Hot summers, mild winters, moderate rainfall.
