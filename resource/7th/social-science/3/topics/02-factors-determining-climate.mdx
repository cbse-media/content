## What Creates Different Climates?

Why is Kashmir freezing while Kerala is hot and humid? The climate of any region is determined collectively by five major factors:

### 1. Latitude (Distance from the Equator)
*   **Equator (Low Latitudes):** Places near the Equator receive sunlight almost perpendicularly. The solar energy is concentrated on a small area, making these regions (like Kanniyakumari) very **hot**.
*   **Poles (High Latitudes):** As you move away from the Equator, the sun's rays hit the Earth at an oblique (slanted) angle. The energy is dispersed over a larger area and passes through more atmosphere, losing heat. Thus, northern places (like Srinagar) are much **cooler**.

### 2. Altitude (Height above Sea Level)
*   As you climb higher up a mountain, the atmospheric pressure and air density decrease. Less dense air holds less heat.
*   Therefore, **temperature decreases as altitude increases**. This is why hill stations like Ooty, Shimla, and Darjeeling are cool even during the peak of Indian summers!

### 3. Proximity to the Sea
Water heats up and cools down much slower than land. The sea acts as a moderator for temperature.
*   **Coastal Areas (like Mumbai):** Experience **temperate** climates. The sea breezes keep summers from getting too hot and winters from getting too cold.
*   **Inland Areas (like Nagpur/Delhi):** Experience **extreme** climates. Far from the moderating effect of the sea, they have scorching summers and freezing winters.

### 4. Winds
Winds transport heat and moisture. 
*   Winds blowing from the western deserts (Arabia/Afghanistan) bring dry, scorching heat (*Loo*) to Punjab and Rajasthan in summer.
*   Winds coming from the oceans bring moisture and rainfall to the coasts.

### 5. Topography
The physical features of the land act as natural barriers. The mighty Himalayas block the freezing winds from Central Asia, keeping India relatively warm in winter. They also trap the monsoon winds, forcing them to drop their rain over the Indian subcontinent instead of passing into Tibet.
