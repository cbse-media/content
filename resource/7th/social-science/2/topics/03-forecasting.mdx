## The India Meteorological Department (IMD)

The IMD was set up in 1875 to systematically study and predict the weather in India. 
Its motto is a Sanskrit phrase from the Manusmriti: *"Adityat jayate vrishti"* ("From the sun arises rain").

<Callout variant="info">
**Why does rain arise from the sun?**
The Sun's heat evaporates water from oceans and lakes into water vapour. This vapour rises, cools, condenses into clouds, and eventually falls back to Earth as rain!
</Callout>

## Automated Weather Stations (AWS)

To make accurate predictions, we need constant, reliable data. An **Automated Weather Station (AWS)** is a self-operating system, usually powered by solar panels, that uses various sensors to continuously measure and record all five elements of weather. 

Because they don't require humans to operate them, AWS units are placed in remote, harsh environments (like glacial lakes in the Himalayas at $4800\text{m}$) to provide early warnings of extreme weather.

## Why is Forecasting Important?

By studying data over long periods, meteorologists predict how the weather will behave in the coming days.

Accurate predictions are critical for:
1.  **Agriculture:** Helping farmers know when to plant or harvest.
2.  **Aviation and Shipping:** Ensuring planes and boats do not travel into dangerous storms.
3.  **Disaster Management:** In the era of climate change, extreme weather like cyclones, heat waves, and floods are more frequent. Forecasts allow governments to issue color-coded warnings (Yellow for Watch, Orange for Alert, Red for Warning/Take Action) and evacuate coastal areas before disasters strike, saving thousands of lives.
