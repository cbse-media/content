## Introduction

"A change in the weather is sufficient to create the world and oneself anew." – Marcel Proust

Have you ever stepped outside on a crisp winter morning and immediately reached for a jacket? Or felt the sticky, heavy air before a summer thunderstorm breaks? Your body is constantly sensing the weather. In this chapter, we will learn exactly what "weather" is, the scientific elements that make it up, and the incredible instruments we use to measure and predict it.

<Callout variant="info">
**Did you know?**
Almost all weather phenomena that we experience—from rain and snow to heatwaves and hurricanes—take place in the lowest layer of the Earth's atmosphere, called the **troposphere**, which extends just 6 to 18 kilometres above the ground!
</Callout>

## Key Terms to Remember

*   **Weather:** The state of the Earth's atmosphere at a particular time and place.
*   **Meteorology:** The systematic study of weather and its evolution.
*   **Forecast:** To predict or find out in advance what the weather will be like.
*   **Acclimatise:** To adjust the body to a new climate or new environmental condition (like high altitude).
