## Scenario Questions (Page 42)

**2. Jyotsna is deciding what clothes to pack for her school trip to Mumbai in June. The forecast predicts $29^\circ\text{C}$ and $84\%$ humidity. What would be your advice to her?**

*   **Analysis:** A temperature of $29^\circ\text{C}$ is warm. A relative humidity of $84\%$ is extremely high, meaning the air is almost saturated with moisture. 
*   **Effect:** Because the air is so humid, sweat will not evaporate easily from her skin. She will feel very sticky, sweaty, and hotter than the actual temperature suggests.
*   **Advice:** She should pack **light, loose-fitting, breathable cotton clothes**. Cotton absorbs sweat well and allows for better air circulation compared to synthetic fabrics like polyester. She should also carry an umbrella or raincoat, as $84\%$ humidity strongly indicates incoming rain!

**3. Imagine that a small group of students is setting up a rain gauge. Discuss and finalise the site from the options below. Write down the reasons for your decision.**
1. The school vegetable garden.
2. The terrace of the school building.
3. Open ground with elevated platform.
4. Compound wall of school.
5. Verandah of the school laboratory.

*   **Best Option:** **3. Open ground with elevated platform.**
*   **Reasons:** A rain gauge must be placed in a location where it can collect falling rain purely from the sky without any obstruction or interference.
    *   *Why not the vegetable garden?* Plants, tall crops, or trees might block the rain or drip extra water into the gauge.
    *   *Why not the terrace?* Strong winds over the edge of buildings can create updrafts that blow rain away from the gauge, leading to inaccurate readings.
    *   *Why not the compound wall?* It might be shadowed by trees, or wind hitting the wall could disrupt the rain path.
    *   *Why not the verandah?* The roof of the verandah will completely block the rain!
    *   *Why an open ground with an elevated platform?* An open ground has no overhead obstructions. Placing it on a slightly elevated platform prevents splashing from the ground (mud/puddles) from bouncing up and entering the gauge.

Temperatures remain bitterly cold across the region. **Srinagar** recorded a maximum temperature of $6.5^\circ\text{C}$ and a minimum of $0.2^\circ\text{C}$, though this is slightly colder than normal for this time of year. **Gulmarg** remains the coldest station on our map today, struggling to reach a maximum of just $-2.6^\circ\text{C}$, with nighttime temperatures plunging to $-7.6^\circ\text{C}$!

In terms of precipitation, **Qazigund** received the heaviest rainfall over the last 24 hours, recording $36.2 \text{ mm}$, alongside $22 \text{ cm}$ of snowfall. Meanwhile, **Kukernag** experienced $30 \text{ cm}$ of snowfall, keeping the region heavily blanketed.
