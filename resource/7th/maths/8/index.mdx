## Introduction

In earlier classes, you learned what fractions are and how to add and subtract them. But what happens when we need to multiply or divide parts of a whole? If a tortoise crawls $\frac{1}{4}$ of a kilometre in 1 hour, how far does it crawl in $\frac{1}{2}$ an hour? 

In this chapter, we will master the operations of **Multiplication and Division of Fractions**. We will discover ancient rules codified by Indian mathematicians like Brahmagupta and Bhaskara II, and see how fractions are applied in everyday geometry, timekeeping, and sharing!

<Callout variant="info">
**Did you know?**
General rules for performing arithmetic operations on fractions were first codified in their modern form by the Indian mathematician **Brahmagupta** in his *Brāhmasphuṭasiddhānta* around 628 CE!

## Quick Reference: Fraction Rules

Memorize these fundamental rules for working with fractions:

### Multiplication
To multiply two fractions, simply multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
$$ \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d} $$

### The Reciprocal
The reciprocal of a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$ is $\frac{b}{a}$. When you multiply a fraction by its reciprocal, the product is exactly $1$.
$$ \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{b}{a} = 1 $$

### Division
To divide one fraction by another, multiply the first fraction (the dividend) by the **reciprocal** of the second fraction (the divisor).
$$ \frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c} = \frac{a \times d}{b \times c} $$
