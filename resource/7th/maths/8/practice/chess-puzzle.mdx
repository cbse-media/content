## The Queen's Movement

In the game of Chess, the **Queen** is the most powerful piece. She can move any number of empty squares in any direction:
1.  **Horizontally** (left or right along the rank)
2.  **Vertically** (up or down along the file)
3.  **Diagonally** (in an X-shape across the board)

If another piece is in the path of her movement, she is "attacking" that piece.

## The Puzzle: Non-Attacking Queens

**Goal:** Place $8$ Queens on a standard $8 \times 8$ chess grid so that **no two queens attack each other**. 

This means:
*   No two queens can share the same row.
*   No two queens can share the same column.
*   No two queens can share the same diagonal.

<Callout variant="tip">
**How to solve it:**
This is a famous mathematical problem! Because no two queens can share a row or column, we know exactly one queen must be placed in each column, and exactly one queen must be placed in each row.

The trick is making sure the diagonals don't cross. Try starting by placing a Queen in the top-left, and then moving to the next column. Since the row and diagonals are blocked, you'll have to place the next Queen a knight's move away (e.g., down two, right one). Keep testing until all 8 fit!
</Callout>

*(Note: There are exactly 92 distinct solutions to the 8-Queens puzzle! Challenge your friends to find just one of them without looking it up).*
