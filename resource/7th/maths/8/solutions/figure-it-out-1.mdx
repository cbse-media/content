## Figure it Out (Page 4)

**1. Tenzin drinks $\frac{1}{2}$ glass of milk every day. How many glasses of milk does he drink in a week? How many in the month of January?**
*   **In a week (7 days):** $7 \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{7}{2} = \mathbf{3 \frac{1}{2} \text{ glasses}}$.
*   **In January (31 days):** $31 \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{31}{2} = \mathbf{15 \frac{1}{2} \text{ glasses}}$.

**2. A team of workers can make $1\text{ km}$ of a water canal in $8$ days. So, in one day, the team can make \_\_\_ km. If they work 5 days a week, they can make \_\_\_ km in a week.**
*   **In one day:** $1 \div 8 = \mathbf{\frac{1}{8} \text{ km}}$.
*   **In a 5-day work week:** $5 \times \frac{1}{8} = \mathbf{\frac{5}{8} \text{ km}}$.

**3. Manju and two of her neighbours buy $5$ litres of oil every week and share it equally among the $3$ families. How much oil does each family get in a week? How much oil will one family get in $4$ weeks?**
*   **Per family per week:** $5 \text{ litres} \div 3 = \mathbf{\frac{5}{3} \text{ litres}}$ (or $1 \frac{2}{3}$ litres).
*   **Per family in 4 weeks:** $4 \times \frac{5}{3} = \frac{20}{3} = \mathbf{6 \frac{2}{3} \text{ litres}}$.

**4. Safia saw the Moon setting on Monday at 10 pm. Her mother told her that every day the Moon sets $\frac{5}{6}$ hour later than the previous day. How many hours after 10 pm will the moon set on Thursday?**
*   **Days passed:** Tuesday (1), Wednesday (2), Thursday (3). That's $3$ days.
*   **Total delay:** $3 \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{15}{6} = \frac{5}{2} = \mathbf{2 \frac{1}{2} \text{ hours}}$.
*(This means it will set at 12:30 AM on Thursday night / Friday morning).*

## Figure it Out (Pages 11 - 12)

**1. A water tank is filled from a tap. If the tap is open for 1 hour, $\frac{7}{10}$ of the tank gets filled. How much is filled if the tap is open for:**
**(a) $\frac{1}{3} \text{ hour}$** $\implies \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{7}{10} = \mathbf{\frac{7}{30}}$
**(b) $\frac{2}{3} \text{ hour}$** $\implies \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{7}{10} = \frac{14}{30} = \mathbf{\frac{7}{15}}$
**(c) $\frac{3}{4} \text{ hour}$** $\implies \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{7}{10} = \mathbf{\frac{21}{40}}$
**(d) $\frac{7}{10} \text{ hour}$** $\implies \frac{7}{10} \times \frac{7}{10} = \mathbf{\frac{49}{100}}$
**(e) For the tank to be full, how long should the tap be running?** 
*   **Solution:** Full tank = $1$. We need $Time \times \frac{7}{10} = 1$.
    Time $= 1 \div \frac{7}{10} = 1 \times \frac{10}{7} = \mathbf{\frac{10}{7} \text{ hours}}$ (or $1 \frac{3}{7}$ hours).

**2. The government has taken $\frac{1}{6}$ of Somu’s land to build a road. What part remains? She gives half of the remaining to daughter Krishna, and $\frac{1}{3}$ of it to son Bora.**
*   **Remaining land:** $1 - \frac{1}{6} = \mathbf{\frac{5}{6}}$
*   **(a) Krishna's share:** $\frac{1}{2}$ of $\frac{5}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{5}{6} = \mathbf{\frac{5}{12}}$
*   **(b) Bora's share:** $\frac{1}{3}$ of $\frac{5}{6} = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} = \mathbf{\frac{5}{18}}$
*   **(c) Somu keeps:** Remaining total minus what she gave away.
    $S = \frac{5}{6} - \left( \frac{5}{12} + \frac{5}{18} \right)$
    LCM of 12 and 18 is 36. $\left( \frac{15}{36} + \frac{10}{36} \right) = \frac{25}{36}$.
    $\frac{5}{6} = \frac{30}{36}$.
    $S = \frac{30}{36} - \frac{25}{36} = \frac{5}{36}$. Somu kept **$\frac{5}{36}$** of the original land.

**3. Find the area of a rectangle of sides $3 \frac{3}{4} \text{ ft}$ and $9 \frac{3}{5} \text{ ft}$.**
*   **Solution:** Convert to improper fractions: $\frac{15}{4} \text{ ft}$ and $\frac{48}{5} \text{ ft}$.
    Area $= \frac{15}{4} \times \frac{48}{5} = \frac{\cancel{15}^3}{\cancel{4}^1} \times \frac{\cancel{48}^{12}}{\cancel{5}^1} = 3 \times 12 = \mathbf{36 \text{ sq ft}}$.

**4. Tsewang plants 4 saplings in a row. The distance between two saplings is $\frac{3}{4} \text{ m}$. Find the distance between the first and last sapling.**
*   **Solution:** Between 4 saplings, there are **3 gaps**. 
    Total distance $= 3 \times \frac{3}{4} = \mathbf{\frac{9}{4} \text{ m}}$ (or $2 \frac{1}{4}$ m).

**5. Which is heavier: $\frac{12}{15}$ of 500 grams or $\frac{3}{20}$ of 4 kg?**
*   **Item 1:** $\frac{12}{15}$ reduces to $\frac{4}{5}$. $\frac{4}{5} \times 500 = 4 \times 100 = 400 \text{ g}$.
*   **Item 2:** First, convert 4 kg to 4000 g. $\frac{3}{20} \times 4000 = 3 \times 200 = 600 \text{ g}$.
*   **Answer:** $600 \text{ g} > 400 \text{ g}$. Therefore, **$\frac{3}{20}$ of 4 kg is heavier**.
