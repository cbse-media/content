**2. Choose the expression that describes the solution. Then simplify it.**
**(a) Maria bought 8 m of lace. She used $\frac{1}{4}$ m for each bag. How many bags did she decorate?**
*   **Expression:** We are splitting 8 m into chunks of 1/4 m. $\implies$ **(iii) $8 \div \frac{1}{4}$**
*   **Simplify:** $8 \times \frac{4}{1} = \mathbf{32 \text{ bags}}$.

**(b) $\frac{1}{2}$ meter of ribbon is used to make 8 badges. What is the length of the ribbon used for each badge?**
*   **Expression:** We are dividing a half-meter ribbon into 8 equal pieces. $\implies$ **(iv) $\frac{1}{2} \div 8$**
*   **Simplify:** $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{8} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{16} \text{ m}}$.

**(c) A baker needs $\frac{1}{6}$ kg of flour to make one loaf of bread. He has 5 kg of flour. How many loaves can he make?**
*   **Expression:** How many $\frac{1}{6}$ chunks fit into 5? $\implies$ **(iii) $5 \div \frac{1}{6}$**
*   **Simplify:** $5 \times \frac{6}{1} = \mathbf{30 \text{ loaves}}$.

**3. If $\frac{1}{4}$ kg of flour is used to make 12 rotis, how much flour is used to make 6 rotis?**
*   **Solution:** 6 rotis is exactly half of 12 rotis. Therefore, we need half the amount of flour.
    $\frac{1}{2} \text{ of } \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{8} \text{ kg}}$.

**4. Pāṭīgaṇita Problem: What sum will be obtained by adding $1 \div \frac{1}{6}$, $1 \div \frac{1}{10}$, $1 \div \frac{1}{13}$, $1 \div \frac{1}{9}$, and $1 \div \frac{1}{2}$?**
*   **Solution:** Convert divisions to multiplication.
    $1 \times 6 = 6$
    $1 \times 10 = 10$
    $1 \times 13 = 13$
    $1 \times 9 = 9$
    $1 \times 2 = 2$
    Sum $= 6 + 10 + 13 + 9 + 2 = \mathbf{40}$.

**5. Mira is reading a 400-page novel. She read $\frac{1}{5}$ yesterday and $\frac{3}{10}$ today. How many more pages does she need to read?**
*   **Yesterday:** $\frac{1}{5} \times 400 = 80 \text{ pages}$.
*   **Today:** $\frac{3}{10} \times 400 = 120 \text{ pages}$.
*   **Total read:** $80 + 120 = 200 \text{ pages}$.
*   **Remaining:** $400 - 200 = \mathbf{200 \text{ pages}}$.

**6. A car runs 16 km using 1 litre of petrol. How far will it go using $2 \frac{3}{4}$ litres?**
*   **Solution:** $16 \times 2 \frac{3}{4} = 16 \times \frac{11}{4} = \frac{16 \times 11}{4} = 4 \times 11 = \mathbf{44 \text{ km}}$.

**7. Train takes $5 \frac{1}{6}$ hours. Plane takes $\frac{1}{2}$ hour. How many hours does the plane save?**
*   **Solution:** Time saved = Train time - Plane time.
    $5 \frac{1}{6} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{31}{6} - \frac{3}{6} = \frac{28}{6}$.
    Simplify $\frac{28}{6} = \frac{14}{3} = \mathbf{4 \frac{2}{3} \text{ hours}}$ (or 4 hours and 40 minutes).

**8. Mariam finished $\frac{4}{5}$ of a cake. The remaining cake was shared equally by 3 friends. How much did each friend get?**
*   **Remaining Cake:** $1 - \frac{4}{5} = \frac{1}{5}$.
*   **Share per friend:** $\frac{1}{5} \div 3 = \frac{1}{5} \times \frac{1}{3} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{15} \text{ of the cake}}$.

**9. Choose the option(s) describing the product of $(\frac{565}{465} \times \frac{707}{676})$:**
*   **Solution:** Both fractions have a numerator greater than the denominator, meaning both are **greater than 1**. When you multiply two numbers greater than 1, the product is greater than both individual numbers, and greater than 1.
    Therefore, the true statements are: **(a) $> \frac{565}{465}$**, **(c) $> \frac{707}{676}$**, and **(e) $> 1$**.

**10. What fraction of the whole square is shaded?**
*   **Solution:** Imagine the big square is divided into 4 smaller equal squares (quadrants). The shaded region is inside the bottom-right quadrant.
    That quadrant is divided diagonally into two large triangles, and one of those triangles is further split. Based on standard geometric visual puzzles of this type, the shaded triangle represents exactly half of one of those diagonal splits, which makes it $\frac{1}{4}$ of the quadrant.
    Since the quadrant is $\frac{1}{4}$ of the whole, the shaded region is $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{16}}$ of the whole square. *(Alternatively, visually depending on the exact diagonal cuts, it may be $\frac{1}{8}$ if it represents half the quadrant).*

**11. Ant colony branching:**
*   **Solution:** When a path splits, the fraction of ants traveling down each branch multiplies.
    If the colony splits in half, then a branch splits into 3, the ants on one of those sub-branches represent $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6}$ of the original colony. By tracing the specific branches to the Mango Tree and Sugarcane field and adding up the fractions of all paths that lead to them, you find the total fraction for each food source.

**12. What is $(1 - \frac{1}{2}) \times (1 - \frac{1}{3}) \times \dots \times (1 - \frac{1}{10})$?**
*   **Solution:** Let's simplify the brackets first:
    $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{4}{5} \times \frac{5}{6} \times \frac{6}{7} \times \frac{7}{8} \times \frac{8}{9} \times \frac{9}{10}$
    Notice a pattern? The numerator of each fraction cancels out the denominator of the previous fraction! (e.g., the 2s cancel, the 3s cancel...).
    All that is left is the very first numerator ($1$) and the very last denominator ($10$).
    **Final Answer:** $\mathbf{\frac{1}{10}}$.
    **General Statement:** In a chain of $(1 - \frac{1}{n})$ multiplied up to $N$, the product will always collapse to **$\frac{1}{N}$**.
