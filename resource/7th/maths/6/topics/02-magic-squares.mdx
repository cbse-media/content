## What is a Magic Square?

A square grid of numbers is called a **magic square** if the numbers in each row, each column, and each diagonal all add up to the *exact same number*. This number is called the **magic sum**.

### A $3 \times 3$ Magic Square (Using numbers 1 to 9)

Let's try to build a magic square using the digits $1$ through $9$ exactly once.

**Observation 1: Finding the Magic Sum**
The sum of all numbers from $1$ to $9$ is:
$$ 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6 + 7 + 8 + 9 = 45 $$
Since there are $3$ rows and they must all have the same sum, the magic sum for each row must be:
$$ 45 \div 3 = 15 $$

**Observation 2: Finding the Center Number**
Which number must go in the very center? By testing possibilities, we find that the number **$5$** must be at the center. It is the exact median of the numbers $1$ through $9$, ensuring the balance of the diagonals, rows, and columns passing through it.

**Observation 3: Placing 1 and 9**
The numbers $1$ and $9$ cannot occur in the corners. They must occur in the middle positions of the outer edges.

## Magic Squares in History

Magic squares have fascinated mathematicians across different cultures for thousands of years.
*   **Lo Shu Square:** Dates back over 2000 years to ancient China. Legend says it was found on the back of a divine turtle.
*   **Chautīsā Yantra:** Found in a 10th-century Jain temple in Khajuraho, India. It is a $4 \times 4$ magic square where every row, column, and diagonal adds up to $34$ (*Chautīs* means 34 in Hindi).
