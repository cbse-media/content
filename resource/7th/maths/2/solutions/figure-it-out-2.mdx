## Questions (Page 3)

**Use `>`, `<`, or `=` in each of the following expressions to compare them. Can you do it without complicated calculations? Explain your thinking in each case.**

**(a) $245 + 289 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 246 + 285$**
*   **Solution:** **$>$** (Greater than)
*   **Reasoning:** Look at how the numbers change from the left side (LHS) to the right side (RHS). 
    $245$ becomes $246$ (an increase of $+1$).
    $289$ becomes $285$ (a decrease of $-4$).
    Overall, the right side is $3$ less than the left side. Therefore, LHS is greater.

**(b) $273 - 145 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 272 - 144$**
*   **Solution:** **$=$** (Equal to)
*   **Reasoning:** $273$ decreases by $1$ to become $272$. At the same time, the number being subtracted ($145$) also decreases by $1$ to become $144$. Because both the starting amount and the subtracted amount decreased by the exact same value, the final difference remains identical.

**(c) $364 + 587 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 363 + 589$**
*   **Solution:** **$<$** (Less than)
*   **Reasoning:** 
    $364$ becomes $363$ (a decrease of $-1$).
    $587$ becomes $589$ (an increase of $+2$).
    Overall, the RHS gains $+1$ compared to the LHS. Therefore, LHS is smaller.

**(d) $124 + 245 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 129 + 245$**
*   **Solution:** **$<$** (Less than)
*   **Reasoning:** Both sides are adding the number $245$. Since $124$ is less than $129$, the left side must be smaller.

**(e) $213 - 77 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 214 - 76$**
*   **Solution:** **$<$** (Less than)
*   **Reasoning:** On the right side, you start with a bigger number ($214 > 213$), AND you subtract a smaller number ($76 < 77$). Starting with more and taking away less will always result in a larger final value on the RHS.
