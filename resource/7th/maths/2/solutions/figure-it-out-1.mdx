## Questions (Page 2)

**1. Fill in the blanks to make the expressions equal on both sides of the `=` sign:**

**(a) $13 + 4 =$ \_\_\_\_ $+ 6$**
*   **Solution:** First, find the value of the left side (LHS): $13 + 4 = 17$.
    Now, what number added to $6$ gives $17$? 
    $17 - 6 = \mathbf{11}$.
    Answer: **$11$**

**(b) $22 +$ \_\_\_\_ $= 6 \times 5$**
*   **Solution:** Find the value of the right side (RHS): $6 \times 5 = 30$.
    What number added to $22$ gives $30$?
    $30 - 22 = \mathbf{8}$.
    Answer: **$8$**

**(c) $8 \times$ \_\_\_\_ $= 64 \div 2$**
*   **Solution:** Find the value of the RHS: $64 \div 2 = 32$.
    What number multiplied by $8$ gives $32$?
    $32 \div 8 = \mathbf{4}$.
    Answer: **$4$**

**(d) $34 -$ \_\_\_\_ $= 25$**
*   **Solution:** What number subtracted from $34$ gives $25$?
    $34 - 25 = \mathbf{9}$.
    Answer: **$9$**

**2. Arrange the following expressions in ascending (increasing) order of their values.**
(a) $67 - 19$
(b) $67 - 20$
(c) $35 + 25$
(d) $5 \times 11$
(e) $120 \div 3$

**Solution:**
First, evaluate the value of each expression:
*   (a) $67 - 19 = \mathbf{48}$
*   (b) $67 - 20 = \mathbf{47}$
*   (c) $35 + 25 = \mathbf{60}$
*   (d) $5 \times 11 = \mathbf{55}$
*   (e) $120 \div 3 = \mathbf{40}$

Now, arrange the values from smallest to largest:
$40 < 47 < 48 < 55 < 60$

Substitute the original expressions back into this order:
**$120 \div 3$ , $\quad 67 - 20$ , $\quad 67 - 19$ , $\quad 5 \times 11$ , $\quad 35 + 25$**
