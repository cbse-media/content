## Questions (Page 11 & 12)

**1. Find the values of the following expressions by writing the terms in each case.**

**(a) $28 - 7 + 8$**
*   **Terms:** $28, -7, 8$
*   **Value:** $28 - 7 = 21$; $21 + 8 = \mathbf{29}$

**(b) $39 - 2 \times 6 + 11$**
*   **Terms:** $39, -(2 \times 6), 11 \rightarrow 39, -12, 11$
*   **Value:** $39 - 12 = 27$; $27 + 11 = \mathbf{38}$

**(c) $40 - 10 + 10 + 10$**
*   **Terms:** $40, -10, 10, 10$
*   **Value:** $40 - 10 = 30$; $30 + 10 + 10 = \mathbf{50}$

**(d) $48 - 10 \times 2 + 16 \div 2$**
*   **Terms:** $48, -(10 \times 2), (16 \div 2) \rightarrow 48, -20, 8$
*   **Value:** $48 - 20 = 28$; $28 + 8 = \mathbf{36}$

**(e) $6 \times 3 - 4 \times 8 \times 5$**
*   **Terms:** $(6 \times 3), -(4 \times 8 \times 5) \rightarrow 18, -160$
*   **Value:** $18 - 160 = \mathbf{-142}$

**2. Write a story/situation for each of the following expressions and find their values.**

**(a) $89 + 21 - 10$**
*   **Situation:** A farmer had 89 apples. He picked 21 more from his orchard, but then sold 10 apples to a neighbor. How many apples does he have now?
*   **Value:** $89 + 21 = 110$; $110 - 10 = \mathbf{100}$

**(b) $5 \times 12 - 6$**
*   **Situation:** A teacher bought 5 boxes of pencils, with 12 pencils in each box. She gave away 6 pencils to students who forgot theirs. How many pencils are left?
*   **Value:** $60 - 6 = \mathbf{54}$

**(c) $4 \times 9 + 2 \times 6$**
*   **Situation:** At a restaurant, a family ordered 4 pizzas that cost ₹9 each, and 2 drinks that cost ₹6 each. What is their total bill?
*   **Value:** $36 + 12 = \mathbf{48}$

**3. For each of the following situations, write the expression describing the situation, identify its terms and find the value of the expression.**

**(a) Queen Alia gave 100 gold coins to Princess Elsa and 100 gold coins to Princess Anna. Princess Elsa doubled her coins. Princess Anna spent half of hers. How many coins do they have together?**
*   **Expression:** $(100 \times 2) + (100 \div 2)$
*   **Terms:** $(100 \times 2)$ and $(100 \div 2) \rightarrow 200, 50$
*   **Value:** $200 + 50 = \mathbf{250}$

**(b) A metro train ticket is ₹40 for an adult and ₹20 for a child. What is the total cost of tickets:**
**(i) for four adults and three children?**
*   **Expression:** $4 \times 40 + 3 \times 20$
*   **Terms:** $160, 60$
*   **Value:** $160 + 60 = \mathbf{220}$ (₹220)

**(ii) for two groups having three adults each?**
*   **Expression:** $2 \times (3 \times 40)$
*   **Terms:** This evaluates as a single term: $2 \times 120$
*   **Value:** $\mathbf{240}$ (₹240)
