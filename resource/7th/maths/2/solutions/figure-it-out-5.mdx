**2. In the boxes below, fill `<`, `>`, or `=` after analysing the expressions. Use reasoning, not evaluating.**

**(a) $(8 - 3) \times 29 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } (3 - 8) \times 29$**
*   LHS is positive ($5 \times 29$), RHS is negative ($-5 \times 29$). Therefore, **$>$**.

**(b) $15 + 9 \times 18 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } (15 + 9) \times 18$**
*   LHS multiplies only 9 by 18. RHS multiplies both 15 and 9 by 18. Therefore, **$<$**.

**(c) $23 \times (17 - 9) \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 23 \times 17 + 23 \times 9$**
*   LHS is equivalent to $23 \times 17 - 23 \times 9$. The RHS is adding them instead. Therefore, **$<$**.

**(d) $(34 - 28) \times 42 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 34 \times 42 - 28 \times 42$**
*   By the distributive property, these are identical. Therefore, **$=$**.

**4. Find the sum of the numbers given in each picture in at least two different ways.**

*Visual 1: A $3 \times 3$ grid of alternating numbers. Row 1: (4, 8, 4). Row 2: (8, 4, 8). Row 3: (4, 8, 4).*
*   **Method 1 (Grouping by Number):** There are five 4s and four 8s. 
    Sum = $(5 \times 4) + (4 \times 8) = 20 + 32 = \mathbf{52}$.
*   **Method 2 (Row by Row):** Row 1 sum = 16. Row 2 sum = 20. Row 3 sum = 16.
    Sum = $16 + 20 + 16 = \mathbf{52}$.

*Visual 2: A $4 \times 4$ grid containing 5s and 6s. Every row has exactly two 5s and two 6s.*
*   **Method 1 (Grouping by Number):** There are eight 5s and eight 6s overall.
    Sum = $(8 \times 5) + (8 \times 6) = 40 + 48 = \mathbf{88}$.
*   **Method 2 (Row by Row):** Each row has the exact same sum: $(2 \times 5) + (2 \times 6) = 10 + 12 = 22$. Because there are 4 rows, we multiply the row sum by 4.
    Sum = $4 \times 22 = \mathbf{88}$.

**Word Problems (Pages 19-21)**

**(a) Market supply: Rahim supplies 9 kg, Shyam supplies 11 kg of mangoes every day. Find the amount supplied in a week.**
*   **Expression:** $7 \times (9 + 11)$
*   **Value:** $7 \times 20 = \mathbf{140 \text{ kg}}$

**(b) Binu earns ₹20,000 per month. She spends ₹5,000 (rent), ₹5,000 (food), and ₹2,000 (expenses). What is her savings by the end of a year?**
*   **Expression for 1 month savings:** $20000 - (5000 + 5000 + 2000)$
*   **Total Year Savings:** $12 \times (20000 - 12000) = 12 \times 8000 = \mathbf{₹96,000}$

**(c) Snail climbs 3cm during the day, slips down 2cm at night. Post is 10cm high. How many days to reach the top?**
*   Net progress per day = $3cm - 2cm = 1cm$.
*   However, on the final day, the snail will reach the top during the day and won't slip down!
*   At the end of Day 7, it is at $7cm$. On Day 8 morning, it climbs $3cm$, reaching $10cm$ (the top).
*   **Answer:** **8 Days**.

**(d) Melvin reads a two-page story every day except on Tuesdays and Saturdays. How many stories would he complete in 8 weeks? Which expression matches?**
*   He reads $7$ days a week minus $2$ days off $= 5$ reading days a week.
*   Since he reads one (two-page) story per reading day, he reads $5$ stories a week.
*   Total over 8 weeks = $(7 - 2) \times 8 = 5 \times 8 = 40$ stories.
*   **Matching Expression:** **(h) $(7 - 2) \times 8$**
