## What is a Simple Expression?

A simple arithmetic expression combines numbers with mathematical operators like addition (`+`), subtraction (`-`), multiplication (`\times`), and division (`\div`). 

**Example Scenario:**
Mallika spends ₹25 every day for lunch at school. What is the expression for the total amount she spends on lunch in a week from Monday to Friday?
*   Number of days = $5$
*   Amount per day = ₹$25$
*   **Expression:** $5 \times 25$

This expression is read as "5 times 25" or "the product of 5 and 25".

<Callout variant="info">
**Multiple Expressions, Same Value**
Different expressions can have the exact same value. For example, the number $12$ can be expressed in many ways:
*   $10 + 2$
*   $15 - 3$
*   $3 \times 4$
*   $24 \div 2$
</Callout>

## Comparing Expressions

Just as we compare normal numbers using `$=$` (equal to), `<` (less than), and `>` (greater than) signs, we can also compare entire expressions based on their values.

**Example 1:**
$$ 10 + 2 \text{ \_\_\_\_ } 7 + 1 $$
Because the value of $10 + 2 = 12$, and the value of $7 + 1 = 8$, we know $12$ is greater than $8$.
Therefore: **$10 + 2 > 7 + 1$**

**Example 2: Comparing Without Fully Calculating**
Which is greater? $1023 + 125$ or $1022 + 128$?

Imagine a situation:
*   Raja had $1023$ marbles and got $125$ more today.
*   Joy had $1022$ marbles and got $128$ more today.

Let's compare them step-by-step:
1. Raja started with $1$ more marble than Joy ($1023$ vs $1022$).
2. However, Joy got $3$ more marbles today than Raja did ($128$ vs $125$).
3. Because Joy's gain of $3$ is greater than Raja's head-start of $1$, Joy ends up with more marbles.

Therefore: **$1023 + 125 < 1022 + 128$**
