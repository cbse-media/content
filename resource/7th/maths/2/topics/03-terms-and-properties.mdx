## What are "Terms" in an Expression?

When an expression has multiple operations and no brackets, we use the notion of **terms** to determine the order.
Terms are the parts of an expression separated by a `+` sign.

For example, in $12 + 7$, the terms are **$12$** and **$7$**.

### Handling Subtraction
What about $83 - 14$? We know that subtracting a number is the same as adding its inverse (opposite sign).
$$ 83 - 14 = 83 + (-14) $$
Here, the terms are **$83$** and **$-14$**.

### Handling Multiplication and Division
Multiplications and divisions group numbers into a *single term*.
In the expression $6 \times 5 + 3$, the terms are **$(6 \times 5)$** and **$3$**.

## Swapping and Grouping (Properties of Addition)

### 1. Commutative Property (Swapping)
In an expression having only additions (or where subtractions are converted to negative terms), swapping the terms does not change the value.

### 2. Associative Property (Grouping)
Grouping the terms of an expression in either order gives the same value.
$$ (-7) + 10 + (-11) $$
*   Grouping first two: $((-7) + 10) + (-11) = 3 + (-11) = -8$
*   Grouping last two: $(-7) + (10 + (-11)) = (-7) + (-1) = -8$

## Removing Brackets

Removing brackets simplifies expressions so we can easily swap and group terms.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Important Rule for Removing Brackets:**
*   When brackets are preceded by a **negative sign** (`-`), the signs of the terms *inside* the brackets change!
    $$ 200 - (40 + 3) = 200 - 40 - 3 $$
*   When brackets are preceded by a **positive sign** (`+` or none), the terms inside do NOT change their signs.
    $$ 28 + (35 - 10) = 28 + 35 - 10 $$
</Callout>

## The Distributive Property

Consider the expression: $10 \times 98 + 3 \times 98$.
This means taking the sum of "$10$ times $98$" and "$3$ times $98$". Together, that makes $13$ times $98$!

$$ 10 \times 98 + 3 \times 98 = (10 + 3) \times 98 = 13 \times 98 $$

**Rule:** The multiple of a sum (or difference) is the same as the sum (or difference) of the multiples.
