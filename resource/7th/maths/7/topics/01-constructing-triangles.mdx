## Why use a Compass?

If you try to draw a triangle with sides $4\text{cm}$, $5\text{cm}$, and $6\text{cm}$ using *only* a ruler, it requires a lot of guesswork. You might draw the base easily, but getting the other two lines to meet perfectly at the right lengths is frustrating.

A compass solves this problem perfectly because a circle represents all points that are a specific distance away from a center point!

## Construction: When 3 Sides are Given (SSS)

Let's construct a triangle with side lengths $4\text{cm}$, $5\text{cm}$, and $6\text{cm}$.

**Step 1:** Choose the longest side to be the base. Draw a line segment $AB = 6\text{cm}$ using a ruler.
**Step 2:** We need a point $C$ that is $4\text{cm}$ away from $A$. Open your compass to a width of $4\text{cm}$. Place the compass point on $A$ and draw a large arc above the line.
**Step 3:** We also need point $C$ to be $5\text{cm}$ away from $B$. Open your compass to $5\text{cm}$. Place the point on $B$ and draw an arc intersecting the first arc.
**Step 4:** The point where the two arcs cross is exactly $4\text{cm}$ from $A$ and $5\text{cm}$ from $B$! Label this point $C$. Join $A$ to $C$, and $B$ to $C$.

## Construction: Two Sides and the Included Angle (SAS)

How do we construct a triangle if two sides and the angle *between* them are given?
Example: $AB = 5\text{cm}$, $AC = 4\text{cm}$, and $\angle A = 45^\circ$.

**Step 1:** Draw the base $AB$ of length $5\text{cm}$.
**Step 2:** Using a protractor, measure and draw an angle of $45^\circ$ at point $A$. Draw a long ray extending from $A$.
**Step 3:** Open your compass to $4\text{cm}$. Place the point on $A$ and draw an arc to cut the ray you just drew. Mark this intersection point as $C$.
**Step 4:** Join $B$ to $C$ to complete the triangle.

## Construction: Two Angles and the Included Side (ASA)

Example: Base $AB = 5\text{cm}$, $\angle A = 45^\circ$, and $\angle B = 80^\circ$.

**Step 1:** Draw the base $AB$ of length $5\text{cm}$.
**Step 2:** Use a protractor to draw a ray from $A$ at an angle of $45^\circ$.
**Step 3:** Use a protractor to draw a ray from $B$ at an angle of $80^\circ$.
**Step 4:** The point where these two rays naturally cross each other is the third vertex, $C$!
