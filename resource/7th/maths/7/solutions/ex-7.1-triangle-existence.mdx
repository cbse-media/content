## Figure it Out (Page 14 / PDF 159)

**1. Check if a triangle exists for each of the following sets of lengths:**
*(Rule: The sum of the two shorter sides must be strictly GREATER than the longest side).*

**(a) $1, 100, 100$**
*   **Check:** $1 + 100 = 101$. Is $101 > 100$? **Yes.**
*   **Result:** Triangle **exists** (It is a very skinny isosceles triangle).

**(b) $3, 6, 9$**
*   **Check:** $3 + 6 = 9$. Is $9 > 9$? **No.** They are equal.
*   **Result:** Triangle **does not exist** (The lines would lie perfectly flat on top of each other).

**(c) $1, 1, 5$**
*   **Check:** $1 + 1 = 2$. Is $2 > 5$? **No.**
*   **Result:** Triangle **does not exist**.

**(d) $5, 10, 12$**
*   **Check:** $5 + 10 = 15$. Is $15 > 12$? **Yes.**
*   **Result:** Triangle **exists**.

**2. Does there exist an equilateral triangle with sides 50, 50, 50? In general, does there exist an equilateral triangle of any sidelength? Justify your answer.**

*   **Solution:** Let the side length be $a$. In an equilateral triangle, the sides are $a, a, a$. 
    We must check if the sum of any two sides is greater than the third: $a + a = 2a$.
    Since length $a$ must be a positive number (greater than zero), $2a$ is always strictly greater than $a$. ($2a > a$).
    Therefore, the triangle inequality is **always satisfied**. 
    **Yes**, an equilateral triangle exists for *any* positive side length.

**3. For each of the following, give at least 5 possible values for the third length so there exists a triangle having these as sidelengths (decimal values could also be chosen):**

Let the unknown third side be $x$.
The rules are:
1. $x < \text{Side}_1 + \text{Side}_2$ (The sum of the known sides must be greater than $x$).
2. $x > |\text{Side}_1 - \text{Side}_2|$ (The third side must be longer than the difference between the known sides to prevent them from snapping shut).
Therefore: **$|\text{Side}_1 - \text{Side}_2| < x < (\text{Side}_1 + \text{Side}_2)$**

**(a) Known sides: $1, 100$**
*   **Range:** $100 - 1 < x < 100 + 1 \implies \mathbf{99 < x < 101}$.
*   **5 Possible Values:** $99.1, 99.5, 100, 100.2, 100.9$. (Note: $99$ and $101$ are NOT allowed).

**(b) Known sides: $5, 5$**
*   **Range:** $5 - 5 < x < 5 + 5 \implies \mathbf{0 < x < 10}$.
*   **5 Possible Values:** $1, 3, 5, 7, 9.9$.

**(c) Known sides: $3, 7$**
*   **Range:** $7 - 3 < x < 7 + 3 \implies \mathbf{4 < x < 10}$.
*   **5 Possible Values:** $4.5, 5, 6, 8, 9$.
