## Introduction

A triangle is the most basic closed shape in geometry. It is formed simply by the intersection of three lines! It consists of three corner points (called **vertices**) and three line segments (**sides**) that join those vertices. 

In this chapter, "A Tale of Three Intersecting Lines", we will explore everything about triangles: from how to construct them using a compass and ruler, to the unbreakable rules of their side lengths, and the magical property of their internal angles.

<Callout variant="info">
**Did you know?**
Triangles are the strongest, most rigid shape in geometry. Unlike a square, which can be squashed into a slanted parallelogram, a triangle's shape cannot be altered without changing the length of its sides. This is why you see triangles everywhere in bridges and roof trusses!
</Callout>

## Quick Reference: Triangle Classification

Triangles can be classified in two main ways: by their **sides** or by their **angles**.

### Classification by Sides:
*   **Equilateral Triangle:** All three sides are of equal length.
*   **Isosceles Triangle:** Two sides are of equal length.
*   **Scalene Triangle:** All three sides have different lengths.

### Classification by Angles:
*   **Acute-angled Triangle:** All three angles are acute (less than $90^\circ$).
*   **Right-angled Triangle:** Exactly one angle is a right angle (exactly $90^\circ$).
*   **Obtuse-angled Triangle:** Exactly one angle is obtuse (greater than $90^\circ$).
