## Figure it Out (Page 10 / PDF 55)

**In each group, identify the longest and the shortest lengths.**

**(a)** $\frac{3}{10}$ , $\frac{3}{100}$ , $\frac{33}{100}$
*   **Solution:** Let's convert them all to hundredths for easy comparison.
    $\frac{3}{10} = \frac{30}{100}$
    Now compare: $\frac{30}{100}, \frac{3}{100}, \frac{33}{100}$.
    *   **Longest:** $\frac{33}{100}$
    *   **Shortest:** $\frac{3}{100}$

**(b)** $3 \frac{1}{10}$ , $\frac{30}{10}$ , $1 \frac{3}{10}$
*   **Solution:** Convert to mixed fractions or decimals.
    $3 \frac{1}{10} = 3.1$
    $\frac{30}{10} = 3.0$
    $1 \frac{3}{10} = 1.3$
    *   **Longest:** $3 \frac{1}{10}$
    *   **Shortest:** $1 \frac{3}{10}$

**(c)** $\frac{45}{100}$ , $\frac{54}{100}$ , $\frac{5}{10}$ , $\frac{4}{10}$
*   **Solution:** Convert tenths to hundredths.
    $\frac{5}{10} = \frac{50}{100}$ and $\frac{4}{10} = \frac{40}{100}$.
    Compare: $\frac{45}{100}, \frac{54}{100}, \frac{50}{100}, \frac{40}{100}$.
    *   **Longest:** $\frac{54}{100}$
    *   **Shortest:** $\frac{4}{10}$ (which is $\frac{40}{100}$)

**(d)** $3 \frac{6}{10}$ , $3 \frac{6}{100}$ , $3 \frac{6}{10} \frac{6}{100}$
*   **Solution:** Convert to decimals.
    $3 \frac{6}{10} = 3.60$
    $3 \frac{6}{100} = 3.06$
    $3 \frac{6}{10} \frac{6}{100} = 3.66$
    *   **Longest:** $3 \frac{6}{10} \frac{6}{100}$ ($3.66$)
    *   **Shortest:** $3 \frac{6}{100}$ ($3.06$)

## Figure it Out (Page 13 / PDF 58)

**Find the sums and differences:**

**(a)** $\frac{3}{10} + 3 \frac{4}{100}$
*   **Solution:** Convert $\frac{3}{10}$ to $\frac{30}{100}$. 
    $\frac{30}{100} + 3 \frac{4}{100} = 3 \frac{34}{100}$ (or $3.34$)

**(b)** $9 \frac{5}{10} \frac{7}{100} + 2 \frac{1}{10} \frac{3}{100}$
*   **Solution:** Add the whole numbers, tenths, and hundredths separately.
    Whole: $9 + 2 = 11$
    Tenths: $\frac{5}{10} + \frac{1}{10} = \frac{6}{10}$
    Hundredths: $\frac{7}{100} + \frac{3}{100} = \frac{10}{100} = \frac{1}{10}$
    Total: $11 + \frac{6}{10} + \frac{1}{10} = \mathbf{11 \frac{7}{10}}$ (or $11.7$)

**(c)** $15 \frac{6}{10} \frac{4}{100} + 14 \frac{3}{10} \frac{6}{100}$
*   **Solution:** Using decimals is easier here. $15.64 + 14.36$
    $15.64 + 14.36 = \mathbf{30.00}$ (or $30$)

**(d)** $7 \frac{7}{100} - 4 \frac{4}{100}$
*   **Solution:** Subtract whole numbers and fractions.
    $(7 - 4) = 3$. $(\frac{7}{100} - \frac{4}{100}) = \frac{3}{100}$.
    Result: **$3 \frac{3}{100}$** (or $3.03$)

**(e)** $8 \frac{6}{100} - 5 \frac{3}{100}$
*   **Solution:** $(8 - 5) = 3$. $(\frac{6}{100} - \frac{3}{100}) = \frac{3}{100}$.
    Result: **$3 \frac{3}{100}$** (or $3.03$)

**(f)** $12 \frac{6}{100} \frac{2}{100} - 9 \frac{9}{10} \frac{9}{100}$ 
*(Note: $\frac{6}{100} \frac{2}{100}$ is likely a typo for $\frac{6}{10} \frac{2}{100}$)*
*   **Solution:** Let's assume it means $12.62 - 9.99$.
    $12.62 - 9.99 = \mathbf{2.63}$
