**4. Arrange the following quantities in descending (largest to smallest) order:**

**(a) $11.01$, $1.011$, $1.101$, $11.10$, $1.01$**
*   **Solution:** Check the whole numbers first. The numbers starting with $11$ are largest.
    $11.10 > 11.01$.
    Now compare the numbers starting with $1$.
    $1.101 > 1.011 > 1.01$ (which is $1.010$).
    **Answer:** $11.10, 11.01, 1.101, 1.011, 1.01$

**(b) $2.567$, $2.675$, $2.768$, $2.499$, $2.698$**
*   **Solution:** Look at the tenths place: $7 > 6 > 5 > 4$.
    **Answer:** $2.768, 2.698, 2.675, 2.567, 2.499$

**(c) $4.678 \text{g}$, $4.595 \text{g}$, $4.600 \text{g}$, $4.656 \text{g}$, $4.666 \text{g}$**
*   **Solution:** Tenths are mostly $6$. Look at hundredths.
    **Answer:** $4.678 \text{g}, 4.666 \text{g}, 4.656 \text{g}, 4.600 \text{g}, 4.595 \text{g}$

**(d) $33.13 \text{m}$, $33.31 \text{m}$, $33.133 \text{m}$, $33.331 \text{m}$, $33.313 \text{m}$**
*   **Solution:** 
    $33.331 > 33.313 > 33.310$ (which is $33.31$) $> 33.133 > 33.130$ (which is $33.13$)
    **Answer:** $33.331 \text{m}, 33.313 \text{m}, 33.31 \text{m}, 33.133 \text{m}, 33.13 \text{m}$

**5. Using the digits $1, 4, 0, 8, \text{ and } 6$ make:**
**(a) the decimal number closest to 30**
*   **Solution:** The closest whole numbers we can make to $30$ are in the $10$s or $40$s. Since we have a $1$, $4$, $0$, $8$, $6$. We can make $18.406$ or $40.168$. 
Wait, the number can be formed by any place values. We can form tens, units, and then decimals. Let's form `__ . ___`.
What if we form $18.640$? The difference from 30 is $11.36$.
What if we form $40.168$? The difference is $10.168$. 
Is there any other combination? What about $08.146$? No, that's far. What about placing the digits differently? If it's a 5-digit number with a decimal point anywhere. What if it is $1.0468$? No. 
Closest is **$40.168$** (since we don't have a $2$ or $3$ to start the tens digit).

**(b) the smallest possible decimal number between 100 and 1000.**
*   **Solution:** It must be a 3-digit whole number (Hundreds, Tens, Ones) followed by the decimal. To make it as small as possible, start with the smallest non-zero digit ($1$). Then the next smallest ($0$), then the next ($4$).
    Whole number part: $104$.
    Remaining digits for decimal: $6$ and $8$. Order them smallest to largest: $.68$.
    **Answer:** **$104.68$**

**6. Will a decimal number with more digits be greater than a decimal number with fewer digits?**
*   **Solution:** **No.** The value depends on the place value of the digits, not the quantity of digits. For example, $2.1$ (two digits) is greater than $0.99999$ (six digits).

**8. Pinto supplies 3.79 L, 4.2 L, and 4.25 L of milk in the first 3 days. In 6 days, he supplies 25 L. Find the total milk supplied in the last 3 days.**
*   **Solution:**
    Milk in first 3 days $= 3.79 + 4.20 + 4.25 = 12.24 \text{ L}$.
    Milk in last 3 days $= \text{Total} - \text{First 3 days}$
    $25.00 - 12.24 = \mathbf{12.76 \text{ L}}$

**9. Tinku weighed 35.75 kg in January and 34.50 kg in February. Has he gained or lost weight? How much is the change?**
*   **Solution:** $35.75 > 34.50$. Tinku has **lost weight**.
    Change $= 35.75 - 34.50 = \mathbf{1.25 \text{ kg}}$.

**10. Extend the pattern: 5.5, 6.4, 6.39, 7.29, 7.28, 6.18, 6.17, \_\_\_\_, \_\_\_\_**
*   **Solution:** Let's look at the differences:
    $5.5 \to 6.4$ (Add $0.9$)
    $6.4 \to 6.39$ (Subtract $0.01$)
    $6.39 \to 7.29$ (Add $0.9$)
    $7.29 \to 7.28$ (Subtract $0.01$)
    Wait! Let's check $7.28 \to 6.18$. That is a subtraction of $1.1$.
    Let's re-evaluate. This looks like an alternating pattern or two interwoven sequences.
    Seq A (1st, 3rd, 5th, 7th): $5.5, 6.39, 7.28, 6.17...$
    Let's look closely at the math:
    $5.50 + 0.90 = 6.40$
    $6.40 - 0.01 = 6.39$
    $6.39 + 0.90 = 7.29$
    $7.29 - 0.01 = 7.28$
    $7.28 - 1.10 = 6.18$ ? This breaks the $+0.9$ rule.
    Let's re-read the numbers: $5.5, 6.4, 6.39, 7.29, 7.28, 6.18, 6.17$
    Maybe look at the digits? 
    $5.5 \to 5+5=10$.
    $6.4 \to 6+4=10$.
    $6.39 \to 6+3+9=18$.
    $7.29 \to 7+2+9=18$.
    $7.28 \to 7+2+8=17$.
    $6.18 \to 6+1+8=15$.
    This is complex. Let's look at differences again.
    $+0.9, -0.01, +0.9, -0.01, -1.1, -0.01$.
    What comes next? If the major shifts are $+0.9, +0.9, -1.1$, maybe the next is $-1.1$?
    Let's do $6.17 - 1.1 = \mathbf{5.07}$. 
    Then subtract $0.01$ = **$5.06$**.

**11. How many millimeters make 1 kilometer?**
*   **Solution:** 
    $1 \text{ km} = 1000 \text{ m}$.
    $1 \text{ m} = 100 \text{ cm} \implies 1000 \text{ m} = 1,00,000 \text{ cm}$.
    $1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm} \implies 1,00,000 \text{ cm} = \mathbf{10,00,000 \text{ mm}}$. (One million millimeters!)

---

**12. Indian Railways insurance costs 45 paise per passenger. If 1 lakh people opt for it, what is the total fee paid?**
*   **Solution:** 
    $45 \text{ paise} = ₹ 0.45$.
    $1 \text{ Lakh} = 1,00,000$.
    Total fee $= 0.45 \times 1,00,000 = \mathbf{₹ 45,000}$.

**13. Which is greater?**
**(a) $\frac{10}{1000}$ or $\frac{1}{10}$?**
*   **Solution:** $\frac{10}{1000} = 0.01$. $\frac{1}{10} = 0.1$. Since $0.1 > 0.01$, **$\frac{1}{10}$ is greater**.
**(b) One-hundredth or 90 thousandths?**
*   **Solution:** One-hundredth $= \frac{1}{100} = \frac{10}{1000}$ (10 thousandths). 90 thousandths is clearly greater ($90 > 10$). **90 thousandths is greater**.
**(c) One-thousandth or 90 hundredths?**
*   **Solution:** One-thousandth $= 0.001$. $90$ hundredths $= \frac{90}{100} = 0.90$. **90 hundredths is greater**.
