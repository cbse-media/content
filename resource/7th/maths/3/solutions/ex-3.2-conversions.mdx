## Length Conversions

### Millimeters and Centimeters (Page 20 / PDF 65)

**Fill in the blanks below (mm <–> cm)**
*Rule:* $10 \text{ mm} = 1 \text{ cm}$.
*   **$12 \text{ mm} = 1.2 \text{ cm}$**
*   **$56 \text{ mm} = 5.6 \text{ cm}$**
*   **$70 \text{ mm} = \mathbf{7.0 \text{ cm}}$** (or just $7 \text{ cm}$)
*   **$\mathbf{9 \text{ mm}} = 0.9 \text{ cm}$** (Multiply by 10)
*   **$134 \text{ mm} = \mathbf{13.4 \text{ cm}}$** (Divide by 10)
*   **$\mathbf{2036 \text{ mm}} = 203.6 \text{ cm}$** (Multiply by 10)

### Centimeters and Meters (Page 21 / PDF 66)

**Fill in the blanks below (cm <–> m)**
*Rule:* $100 \text{ cm} = 1 \text{ m}$.
*   **$36 \text{ cm} = \mathbf{0.36 \text{ m}}$** (Divide by 100)
*   **$50 \text{ cm} = \mathbf{0.50 \text{ m}}$** (or $0.5 \text{ m}$)
*   **$\mathbf{89 \text{ cm}} = 0.89 \text{ m}$** (Multiply by 100)
*   **$4 \text{ cm} = \mathbf{0.04 \text{ m}}$** (Be careful! $4 \div 100 = 0.04$, not $0.4$)
*   **$325 \text{ cm} = \mathbf{3.25 \text{ m}}$**
*   **$\mathbf{207 \text{ cm}} = 2.07 \text{ m}$**

**Questions:**
*   *How many mm does 1 meter have?*
    $1 \text{ m} = 100 \text{ cm}$. And $1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm}$.
    So, $100 \times 10 = \mathbf{1000 \text{ mm}}$.
*   *Can we write $1 \text{ mm} = \frac{1}{1000} \text{ m}$?*
    **Yes!** Since $1 \text{ m} = 1000 \text{ mm}$, dividing both sides by $1000$ shows that $1 \text{ mm} = 0.001 \text{ m}$.

## Weight Conversions

### Grams and Kilograms (Page 22 & 23 / PDF 67 & 68)

**Fill in the blanks below (g <–> kg)**
*Rule:* $1000 \text{ g} = 1 \text{ kg}$.
*   **$465 \text{ g} = \mathbf{0.465 \text{ kg}}$**
*   **$68 \text{ g} = \mathbf{0.068 \text{ kg}}$** (Requires a leading zero after the decimal!)
*   **$1560 \text{ g} = \mathbf{1.560 \text{ kg}}$** (or $1.56 \text{ kg}$)
*   **$704 \text{ g} = \mathbf{0.704 \text{ kg}}$**
*   **$\mathbf{560 \text{ g}} = 0.56 \text{ kg}$** (Multiply by 1000: $0.56 \times 1000 = 560$)
*   **$\mathbf{2500 \text{ g}} = 2.5 \text{ kg}$**

## Currency Conversions

### Paise and Rupees (Page 24 / PDF 69)

**Fill in the blanks below (rupee <–> paise)**
*Rule:* $100 \text{ paise (p)} = 1 \text{ Rupee (₹)}$.
*   **$10 \text{ p} = \mathbf{₹ 0.10}$** (or $0.1$)
*   **$\mathbf{5 \text{ p}} = ₹ 0.05$**
*   **$\mathbf{36 \text{ p}} = ₹ 0.36$**
*   **$\mathbf{50 \text{ p}} = ₹ 0.50$**
*   **$99 \text{ p} = \mathbf{₹ 0.99}$**
*   **$250 \text{ p} = \mathbf{₹ 2.50}$**
