## Why Do We Need Conversions?

In the real world, we deal with objects of vastly different sizes. The smallest ant might be $0.8 \text{ mm}$ long, while an airplane flies at $12,000 \text{ m}$ high. Using the decimal system makes it incredibly easy to convert between different metric units because the metric system is also based on powers of $10$.

## Length Conversion

### Millimeters to Centimeters
*Rule:* There are $10 \text{ mm}$ in $1 \text{ cm}$. To convert mm to cm, we divide by $10$.
*   $1 \text{ mm} = \frac{1}{10} \text{ cm} = \mathbf{0.1 \text{ cm}}$
*   $12 \text{ mm} = 10 \text{ mm} + 2 \text{ mm} = 1 \text{ cm} + \frac{2}{10} \text{ cm} = \mathbf{1.2 \text{ cm}}$

### Centimeters to Meters
*Rule:* There are $100 \text{ cm}$ in $1 \text{ m}$. To convert cm to m, we divide by $100$.
*   $1 \text{ cm} = \frac{1}{100} \text{ m} = \mathbf{0.01 \text{ m}}$
*   $15 \text{ cm} = \frac{15}{100} \text{ m} = \frac{10}{100} \text{ m} + \frac{5}{100} \text{ m} = \frac{1}{10} \text{ m} + \frac{5}{100} \text{ m} = \mathbf{0.15 \text{ m}}$

## Weight Conversion

### Grams to Kilograms
*Rule:* There are $1000 \text{ g}$ in $1 \text{ kg}$. To convert g to kg, we divide by $1000$.
*   $1 \text{ g} = \frac{1}{1000} \text{ kg} = \mathbf{0.001 \text{ kg}}$
*   $5 \text{ g} = \frac{5}{1000} \text{ kg} = \mathbf{0.005 \text{ kg}}$
*   $254 \text{ g} = \frac{254}{1000} \text{ kg} = \frac{200}{1000} + \frac{50}{1000} + \frac{4}{1000} = \frac{2}{10} + \frac{5}{100} + \frac{4}{1000} = \mathbf{0.254 \text{ kg}}$

## Rupee - Paise Conversion

Until recently, India used coins for 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 25, and 50 paise. While rare now, you still see paise in digital bank statements and bills!

*Rule:* $1 \text{ Rupee} = 100 \text{ paise}$. To convert paise to Rupees, we divide by $100$.
*   $1 \text{ paisa} = \frac{1}{100} \text{ Rupee} = \mathbf{₹ 0.01}$
*   $75 \text{ paise} = \frac{75}{100} \text{ Rupee} = \mathbf{₹ 0.75}$
