## Introduction

Have you ever tried to fix something and realized a screw was just a *tiny* bit too short? Or perhaps you've seen a running race won by a fraction of a second? In our daily lives, whole numbers aren't always enough to give us the exact measurements we need. 

To measure lengths, weights, or money with precision, we must look **beyond the decimal point**. In this chapter, we will learn how to split a single unit into ten, a hundred, or a thousand equal parts, leading us to the fascinating world of **Decimals**.

<Callout variant="info">
**Did you know?**
The modern decimal notation, using a point (`.`) to separate the whole number from the fractional part, was popularized in the 16th century by mathematicians like John Napier and Christopher Clavius!
</Callout>

## Formula & Conversion Sheet

Memorize these fundamental metric and currency conversions! Because our place value system is based on $10$, these conversions involve moving the decimal point to the right or left.

### Length
*   **$1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm}$**
    *   Therefore, $1 \text{ mm} = \frac{1}{10} \text{ cm} = \mathbf{0.1 \text{ cm}}$
*   **$1 \text{ m} = 100 \text{ cm}$**
    *   Therefore, $1 \text{ cm} = \frac{1}{100} \text{ m} = \mathbf{0.01 \text{ m}}$
*   **$1 \text{ km} = 1000 \text{ m}$**
    *   Therefore, $1 \text{ m} = \frac{1}{1000} \text{ km} = \mathbf{0.001 \text{ km}}$

### Weight
*   **$1 \text{ kg} = 1000 \text{ g}$**
    *   Therefore, $1 \text{ g} = \frac{1}{1000} \text{ kg} = \mathbf{0.001 \text{ kg}}$
*   **$1 \text{ g} = 1000 \text{ mg}$**
    *   Therefore, $1 \text{ mg} = \frac{1}{1000} \text{ g} = \mathbf{0.001 \text{ g}}$

### Money
*   **$1 \text{ Rupee (₹)} = 100 \text{ Paise (p)}$**
    *   Therefore, $1 \text{ Paisa} = \frac{1}{100} \text{ Rupee} = \mathbf{₹ 0.01}$
