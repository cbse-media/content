## Figure it Out (Page 18 / PDF 123)

**1. Find the angles marked below.**

*   **(a)** The angle $a$ and the $48^\circ$ angle form a straight line together. They are a linear pair.
    $a = 180^\circ - 48^\circ = \mathbf{132^\circ}$.
*   **(b)** The angle $b$ is directly across from $52^\circ$ at an intersection. They are vertically opposite.
    $b = \mathbf{52^\circ}$.
*   **(c)** A transversal cuts two parallel lines. The $99^\circ$ and $81^\circ$ form a linear pair. Angle $c$ is in the exact same relative position as the $81^\circ$ angle on the lower intersection. They are corresponding angles.
    $c = \mathbf{81^\circ}$.
*   **(d)** Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. The interior angle adjacent to $d$ corresponds to $99^\circ$. $d$ and $81^\circ$ are alternate exterior angles.
    $d = \mathbf{81^\circ}$.
*   **(e)** Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. The angle $e$ is on the opposite side of the transversal and between the lines relative to $69^\circ$. They are alternate interior angles.
    $e = \mathbf{69^\circ}$.
*   **(f)** The angle $f$ and $132^\circ$ are interior angles on the same side of the transversal. They must add up to $180^\circ$.
    $f = 180^\circ - 132^\circ = \mathbf{48^\circ}$.
*   **(g)** Look at the two parallel vertical lines. A transversal cuts them. Angle $g$ is vertically opposite to the angle that corresponds to $122^\circ$. Thus, $g$ is an alternate exterior angle to $122^\circ$.
    $g = \mathbf{122^\circ}$.
*   **(h)** Look at the two parallel horizontal lines. Angle $h$ and $75^\circ$ are interior angles on the same side of the transversal.
    $h = 180^\circ - 75^\circ = \mathbf{105^\circ}$.
*   **(i)** There are two parallel lines and a transversal forming a triangle. Angle $i$ and $54^\circ$ are alternate interior angles.
    $i = \mathbf{54^\circ}$.
*   **(j)** Two parallel lines cut by two transversals forming a parallelogram shape. Angle $j$ corresponds to the angle forming a linear pair with $124^\circ$. The linear pair angle is $180 - 124 = 56^\circ$.
    Therefore, $j = \mathbf{56^\circ}$.

## Figure it Out (Page 19-20 / PDF 124-125)

**2. Find the angle represented by $a$.**
*   **Top Left Image:** The parallel lines are cut by a transversal. The interior angle on the same side as $100^\circ$ is $180 - 100 = 80^\circ$. The angles $80^\circ$, $42^\circ$, and the vertically opposite angle of $a$ form a triangle.
    Sum of triangle angles $= 180^\circ \implies 80^\circ + 42^\circ + a = 180^\circ \implies a = \mathbf{58^\circ}$.
*   **Top Right Image:** The angle $a$ and $62^\circ$ are alternate exterior angles formed by the transversal cutting parallel lines.
    $a = \mathbf{62^\circ}$.
*   **Bottom Left Image:** Three parallel lines cut by a transversal. The exterior angle is $110^\circ$. The alternate interior angle is $a + 35^\circ$. No, wait, looking closely at the image, $a$ and $35^\circ$ are interior angles. The alternate interior to $110^\circ$ is $70^\circ$. Using exterior angle properties of the formed triangle: $a + 35^\circ = 110^\circ$.
    $a = 110^\circ - 35^\circ = \mathbf{75^\circ}$.
*   **Bottom Right Image:** A right triangle is formed by the transversal. One angle is $67^\circ$. The third angle is $180 - 90 - 67 = 23^\circ$. Angle $a$ is alternate interior to this $23^\circ$ angle.
    $a = \mathbf{23^\circ}$.

**3. In the figures below, what angles do $x$ and $y$ stand for?**
*   **Left Image:** $y$ and $65^\circ$ are corresponding angles $\implies y = \mathbf{65^\circ}$. $x$ is vertically opposite to $y$, or alternate exterior to the vertically opposite of $65^\circ$. Thus $x = \mathbf{65^\circ}$.
*   **Right Image:** A triangle is formed with $53^\circ$ and $78^\circ$. The third angle is $180 - (53+78) = 180 - 131 = 49^\circ$. Angle $x$ is alternate interior to this $49^\circ$ angle. Thus, $x = \mathbf{49^\circ}$.

**4. In Fig. 5.33, $\angle ABC = 45^\circ$ and $\angle IKJ = 78^\circ$. Find angles $\angle GEH$, $\angle HEF$, $\angle FED$.**
*   **Solution:**
    *   Line $IA$ is parallel to line $GD$. The line $JF$ acts as a transversal.
    *   $\angle FED$ and $\angle IKJ$ are corresponding angles. Because the lines are parallel, $\angle FED = \angle IKJ = \mathbf{78^\circ}$.
    *   $\angle GEH$ and $\angle FED$ are vertically opposite angles. Thus, $\angle GEH = \mathbf{78^\circ}$.
    *   $\angle HEF$ and $\angle FED$ form a linear pair along line $GD$. Therefore, $\angle HEF = 180^\circ - 78^\circ = \mathbf{102^\circ}$.

**6. What is the measure of angle $\angle NOP$ in Fig. 5.35?**
*   **Solution:**
    *   *Hint:* Draw lines parallel to $LM$ and $PQ$ through points $N$ and $O$. Let's call them $Line_N$ and $Line_O$.
    *   Because $Line_N \parallel LM$, the alternate interior angle to $\angle LMN$ ($40^\circ$) is $40^\circ$.
    *   The total angle $\angle MNO = 96^\circ$. The remaining part of that angle is $96^\circ - 40^\circ = 56^\circ$.
    *   Because $Line_O \parallel Line_N$, the alternate interior angle to this $56^\circ$ part is also $56^\circ$.
    *   Now look at $PQ$. $Line_O \parallel PQ$. The alternate interior angle to $\angle OPQ$ ($52^\circ$) is $52^\circ$.
    *   The total angle $\angle NOP$ is made of these two parts: $56^\circ + 52^\circ = \mathbf{108^\circ}$.
