**Solution:**

When two lines $l$ and $m$ intersect, they form four angles: $\angle a, \angle b, \angle c,$ and $\angle d$.

### Linear Pairs
Linear pairs are adjacent angles that sit on the same straight line and add up to $180^\circ$. By picking one straight line and looking at the two angles on one side of it, we can find all four pairs:
1.  **$\angle a$ and $\angle b$** (On line $l$)
2.  **$\angle b$ and $\angle c$** (On line $m$)
3.  **$\angle c$ and $\angle d$** (On line $l$)
4.  **$\angle d$ and $\angle a$** (On line $m$)

### Pairs of Vertically Opposite Angles
Vertically opposite angles are the angles directly across from each other at the intersection point. They are mathematically equal.
1.  **$\angle a$ and $\angle c$**
2.  **$\angle b$ and $\angle d$**
