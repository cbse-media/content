## Figure it Out (Pages 8-9 / PDF 113-114)

**Question 1: Draw some lines perpendicular to the lines given on the dot paper.**
*   **Solution Strategy:** A perpendicular line intersects another at exactly $90^\circ$. 
    *   For a perfectly horizontal line on the grid, draw a perfectly vertical line intersecting it.
    *   For a diagonal line moving down $1$ dot and right $1$ dot (a $45^\circ$ angle), the perpendicular line must move *up $1$ dot and right $1$ dot*.

**Question 2: In Fig. 5.11, mark the parallel lines using notations. How did you spot the perpendicular and parallel lines?**
*   **Solution Strategy:** 
    *   **(a) Spotting perpendicular lines:** Look for line segments that meet at sharp, square corners ($90^\circ$). You can verify this by checking if the grid steps are exact inverses (e.g., one line goes up 2 right 1, the perpendicular goes down 1 right 2).
    *   **(b) Spotting parallel lines:** Look for lines that follow the exact same slope or "staircase" pattern on the grid. If one line goes up $2$ dots and right $1$ dot, the parallel line must also go up $2$ dots and right $1$ dot everywhere along its path.

**Question 4: Using your sense of how parallel lines look, try to draw lines parallel to the line segments on the dot paper.**
*   **Solution Strategy:** Count the "slope" of the given line. For example, if line $a$ starts at one dot and ends at a dot that is exactly 2 units down and 1 unit right, pick a new starting dot anywhere on the paper and draw a line that also goes 2 units down and 1 unit right!

**Question 5: In Fig. 5.13, which line is parallel to line $a$ — line $b$ or line $c$? How do you decide this?**
*   **Solution Strategy:** Line $a$ and line $c$ are parallel.
    *   **How to decide:** You can imagine extending a transversal line across them. Or, more simply, use the geometry box tools! Take a set square and align one edge with line $a$. Place a ruler against the bottom of the set square. Slide the set square along the ruler. It will perfectly align with line $c$, but it will cross over line $b$, proving that $a$ and $c$ are the parallel pair.
