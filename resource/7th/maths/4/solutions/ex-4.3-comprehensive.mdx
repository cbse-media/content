## Figure it Out (Pages 102 - 105)

**1. One plate of Jowar roti costs ₹30 and one plate of Pulao costs ₹20. If $x$ plates of Jowar roti and $y$ plates of pulao were ordered in a day, which expression(s) describe the total amount in rupees earned that day?**
*   **Solution:** Cost of Jowar $= 30 \times x = 30x$. Cost of Pulao $= 20 \times y = 20y$. Total $= 30x + 20y$. 
    Matching option: **(a) $30x + 20y$**

**2. Pushpita sells champak and marigold. '$p$' customers bought only champak, '$q$' customers only marigold, and '$r$' customers bought both. She gave a flag to every customer. How many flags did she give?**
*   **Solution:** Each unique customer gets one flag. The total number of unique customers is the sum of those who bought only champak ($p$), only marigold ($q$), and both ($r$).
    Matching option: **(a) $p + q + r$**

**3. A snail climbs up '$u$' cm during the day and slips down '$d$' cm at night. This happens for 10 days and 10 nights.**
**(a) Write an expression describing how far away the snail is from its starting position.**
*   **Solution:** In one full 24-hour cycle, the net movement is $(u - d)$. For 10 cycles, the expression is **$10(u - d)$** or **$10u - 10d$**.
**(b) What can we say about the snail’s movement if $d > u$?**
*   **Solution:** If $d > u$, the snail slips down more than it climbs up. It will make no upward progress and will remain at the bottom of the well (or slide backward if it started higher up).

**4. Radha cycles 5 km every day in the first week. Every week she increases the daily distance by ‘$z$’ km. How many kilometers would Radha have cycled after 3 weeks?**
*   **Solution:** 
    Week 1 daily distance $= 5$. Total for Week 1 $= 7 \times 5 = 35 \text{ km}$.
    Week 2 daily distance $= 5 + z$. Total for Week 2 $= 7 \times (5 + z) = 35 + 7z$.
    Week 3 daily distance $= 5 + 2z$. Total for Week 3 $= 7 \times (5 + 2z) = 35 + 14z$.
    Total after 3 weeks $= 35 + (35 + 7z) + (35 + 14z) = \mathbf{105 + 21z}$.

**6. A local train stops at three stations at equal distances. Time between stations is $t$. Train stops for 2 minutes at each station.**
**(a) If $t = 4$, what is the time taken to travel from Yahapur to Vahapur?**
*   **Solution:** There are 4 travel segments between the start, 3 stations, and the end. There are 3 stops.
    Time $= 4t + 3 \times 2$. If $t=4$: $4(4) + 6 = 16 + 6 = \mathbf{22 \text{ minutes}}$.
**(b) Algebraic expression:**
*   **Solution:** **$4t + 6$**

**10. Describe situations corresponding to the following algebraic expressions:**
*   **(a) $8x + 3y$:** You buy $x$ notebooks for ₹8 each and $y$ pens for ₹3 each. This is the total cost.
*   **(b) $15x - 2x$:** A worker earns ₹15 per hour for $x$ hours, but has ₹2 deducted per hour for uniform rental. This is their net pay.

**11. Rope Cutting Pattern:**
If folded $r$ times and cut across all folds, the number of pieces generated follows the pattern **$2^r + 1$**. (0 folds = 2 pieces, 1 fold = 3 pieces).

**12. Matchstick Squares:**
*   **Solution:** 
    1 square requires 4 matchsticks.
    2 squares require 7 matchsticks.
    Each additional square requires 3 more matchsticks. The rule is $3w + 1$.
    For $10$ squares: $3(10) + 1 = \mathbf{31 \text{ matchsticks}}$.
    For $w$ squares: **$3w + 1$**.

**13. Traffic Signal Colours:**
The pattern is Red(1), Yellow(2), Green(3), Yellow(4), Red(5). The cycle repeats every 4 steps (R, Y, G, Y).
To find the color, divide the position by 4 and look at the remainder:
*   Remainder 1: Red
*   Remainder 2: Yellow
*   Remainder 3: Green
*   Remainder 0: Yellow
**Positions:**
*   **90:** $90 \div 4 = 22$ R $2 \implies$ **Yellow**
*   **190:** $190 \div 4 = 47$ R $2 \implies$ **Yellow**
*   **343:** $343 \div 4 = 85$ R $3 \implies$ **Green**

**14. Pattern of Squares:**
Step 1: 5 squares. Step 2: 9 squares. Step 3: 13 squares.
*   **Rule:** Each step adds 4 squares to the outer arms. Formula: **$4n + 1$**.
*   **Step 4:** $4(4) + 1 = \mathbf{17 \text{ squares}}$.
*   **Step 10:** $4(10) + 1 = \mathbf{41 \text{ squares}}$.
*   **Step 50:** $4(50) + 1 = \mathbf{201 \text{ squares}}$.

**15. Numbers written in an endless 4-column grid:**
**(a) Expressions for each column:**
*   Col 1: **$4r - 3$** (where $r$ is the row number)
*   Col 2: **$4r - 2$**
*   Col 3: **$4r - 1$**
*   Col 4: **$4r$**
**(b) In which row and column will the following numbers appear:**
*   **(i) 124:** $124 \div 4 = 31$ R $0 \implies$ **Row 31, Col 4**
*   **(ii) 147:** $147 = 4(36) + 3 \implies$ **Row 37, Col 3**
*   **(iii) 201:** $201 = 4(50) + 1 \implies$ **Row 51, Col 1**
**(c) What number appears in row $r$ and column $c$?**
*   **Expression:** **$4(r - 1) + c$**
