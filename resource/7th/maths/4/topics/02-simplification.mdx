## Grouping and Swapping Terms

Because letter-numbers represent actual numbers, all the rules of arithmetic (Commutative, Associative, and Distributive properties) apply perfectly to algebraic expressions.

For example, to find the perimeter ($p$) of a rectangle with length $l$ and breadth $b$:
$$ p = l + b + l + b $$
Because we can swap and group terms in any order, we can rewrite this as:
$$ p = l + l + b + b $$
$$ p = 2l + 2b $$

The expression **$2l + 2b$** is the **simplified form** of the original expression.

## Like vs. Unlike Terms

<Callout variant="info">
*   **Like Terms:** Sets of terms that involve the exact same letter-numbers (e.g., $5c, 3c, 10c$ or $12n, -4n$).
*   **Unlike Terms:** Sets of terms that have different letter-numbers (e.g., $18c$ and $11d$).
</Callout>

You can only add or subtract **Like Terms** into a single simplified term.

**Example:** A shopkeeper sells pencils for price $c$ and erasers for price $d$. Over three days, she sells $5c$, $3c$, and $10c$ worth of pencils, and $4d, 6d,$ and $1d$ worth of erasers.
*   Total pencil sales $= 5c + 3c + 10c = \mathbf{18c}$
*   Total eraser sales $= 4d + 6d + 1d = \mathbf{11d}$
*   Total revenue $= \mathbf{18c + 11d}$

*Note: We cannot simplify $18c + 11d$ any further because $c$ and $d$ are unlike terms.*

## Opening Brackets with Variables

Recalling how we open brackets in arithmetic, we do the exact same thing in algebra.

**Example:** Simplify $(40x + 75y) - (6x + 10y)$

1.  Remove the first bracket (preceded by a positive sign, so signs remain the same): 
    $40x + 75y$
2.  Remove the second bracket (preceded by a negative sign, so inner signs flip):
    $- 6x - 10y$
3.  Group the **like terms** together:
    $(40x - 6x) + (75y - 10y)$
4.  Simplify:
    **$34x + 65y$**

## The Distributive Property

When a number sits directly outside a bracket containing an algebraic expression, it multiplies *everything* inside the bracket.

**Example:** Simplify $4(x + y) - y$
1.  Distribute the 4: 
    $4x + 4y$
2.  Bring down the rest of the expression:
    $4x + 4y - y$
3.  Combine like terms (Note: $y$ is the same as $1y$):
    $4x + (4y - 1y) = \mathbf{4x + 3y}$
