## Combustion (Burning)

Have you ever used a magnifying glass to focus the sun's rays on a piece of paper until it starts smoking and catches fire? You didn't use a matchstick, yet a fire started! 

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:**
A chemical reaction in which a substance reacts rapidly with oxygen to produce heat and/or light is called **combustion**. Substances that undergo this process are called **combustible substances** (like wood, paper, and kerosene).
</Callout>

### The Fire Triangle
For combustion to occur, three specific requirements must be met simultaneously. If you remove even one of these, the fire will go out!

1.  **Fuel:** The combustible substance itself.
2.  **Oxygen:** A continuous supply of air. (If you cover a burning candle with a glass tumbler, it cuts off the oxygen, and the flame dies).
3.  **Heat (Ignition Temperature):** The minimum temperature at which a substance catches fire. Focusing sun rays on paper provides enough heat to reach this temperature.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Fire Safety:**
If a person's clothes catch fire, wrapping them in a heavy wool blanket cuts off the **oxygen supply**, stopping the combustion. Never use synthetic blankets, as they will melt and stick to the skin!
</Callout>

### Can Physical and Chemical Changes Occur Together?
Yes! Think of a burning candle:
*   The **melting of the solid wax** into liquid, and its evaporation into a gas, are **physical changes**.
*   The actual **burning of the wax vapor** to produce heat, light, and soot is a **chemical change**.

## Rusting of Iron

When iron objects like gates, nails, or tools are left out in the open, they develop a brown, flaky coating called **rust**. 

*   Rusting is a **chemical change** because a completely new substance (Iron Oxide) is formed.
*   **Conditions for Rusting:** Rusting only happens when iron comes into contact with **both moisture (water) and air (oxygen)**. 
*   If you put an iron nail in completely dry air, or in boiled water topped with oil (blocking the air), the nail will not rust!
