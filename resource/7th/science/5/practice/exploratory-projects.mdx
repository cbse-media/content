## Project 1: Invisible Ink Secret Messages

**The Experiment:** 
Write a message on a piece of paper using a cotton swab dipped in lemon juice. Let it dry completely until it is invisible. Under the supervision of an adult, gently press a warm iron over the paper (or hold it carefully over a light bulb or candle flame). 

**What Happens?**
The invisible letters turn dark brown! 

**The Science:** 
Lemon juice contains carbon-based compounds (organic acids). When heated, these compounds break down and oxidize, turning brown. This oxidation process is a **chemical change** driven by the addition of heat. Because the chemical structure of the juice has been destroyed, this change **cannot be reversed**.

## Project 2: How Does Yeast Work?

**The Experiment:**
1.  Make a sugar solution in a small bottle by mixing warm water and sugar.
2.  Add a spoonful of baker's yeast to the bottle.
3.  Stretch an uninflated balloon over the mouth of the bottle and leave it undisturbed for an hour.
4.  Remove the balloon, hold its neck tightly, and attach it to a second bottle containing fresh lime water. Squeeze the air from the balloon into the lime water and shake.

**What do you observe?**
1.  After an hour, the balloon on the first bottle magically inflates on its own!
2.  When the gas from the balloon mixes with the lime water, the lime water turns **milky white**.

**Conclusion:**
Yeast is a microscopic living organism. It "eats" the sugar in the water and breathes out a gas. Because the lime water turned milky, we know the gas produced is **Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$)**. This process of yeast consuming sugar to produce gas is a **chemical change** called fermentation. (This exact gas is what gets trapped in dough to make bread fluffy and soft!).

## Project 3: The Color-Changing Chameleon

![Detailed diagram of a chameleon changing color on a tree branch](./placeholder.png)

Chameleons (Girgits) are famous for changing their skin color to blend in with their surroundings, regulate their body temperature, or communicate danger!

**Is this a physical or chemical change?**
*   It is a **physical change**. Chameleons have a special layer of cells under their skin called *iridophores*, which contain tiny nanocrystals. 
*   When the chameleon relaxes or gets excited, it physically stretches or shrinks the spacing between these microscopic crystals. 
*   Depending on the spacing, the crystals reflect different wavelengths of light (like blue or red). 
*   Because no new chemical substances are being created or destroyed—only the physical arrangement of the crystals is altering the reflection of light—this is a brilliantly complex **reversible physical change**!
