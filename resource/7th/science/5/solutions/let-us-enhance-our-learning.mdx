## Multiple Choice and Concept Questions (Pages 69-70)

**1. Which of the following statements are the characteristics of a physical change?**
(i) The state of the substance may or may not change.
(ii) A substance with different properties is formed.
(iii) No new substance is formed.
(iv) The substance undergoes a chemical reaction.
*   **Solution:** In a physical change, no new substance is formed (iii), and the physical state (solid/liquid/gas) might change (i). 
*   **Answer:** **(c) (i) and (iii)**

**2. Predict which of the following changes can be reversed and which cannot be reversed.**
*   (i) Stitching cloth to a shirt $\rightarrow$ **Cannot be easily reversed** (The cloth is permanently cut/punctured).
*   (ii) Twisting of straight string $\rightarrow$ **Can be reversed** (You can untwist it).
*   (iii) Making idlis from a batter $\rightarrow$ **Cannot be reversed** (Chemical cooking process).
*   (iv) Dissolving sugar in water $\rightarrow$ **Can be reversed** (By evaporating the water).
*   (v) Drawing water from a well $\rightarrow$ **Can be reversed** (Pour the water back in).
*   (vi) Ripening of fruits $\rightarrow$ **Cannot be reversed** (Chemical breakdown process).
*   (vii) Boiling water in an open pan $\rightarrow$ **Cannot be easily reversed** (The steam escapes into the atmosphere. *If closed, it could be condensed*).
*   (viii) Rolling up a mat $\rightarrow$ **Can be reversed** (Unroll it).
*   (ix) Grinding wheat grains to flour $\rightarrow$ **Cannot be reversed**.
*   (x) Forming of soil from rocks $\rightarrow$ **Cannot be reversed** (Takes thousands of years).

**3. State whether the following statements are True or False. Write the correct statement if False.**
*   (i) Melting of wax is necessary for burning a candle. 
    *   **True.** (The wax must melt and vaporize to act as fuel for the flame).
*   (ii) Collecting water vapour by condensing involves a chemical change.
    *   **False.** *Correct Statement:* Condensation is a change of state (gas to liquid), so it is a **physical change**.
*   (iii) The process of converting leaves into compost is a chemical change.
    *   **True.** (Bacteria and fungi break down the leaves into new substances).
*   (iv) Mixing baking soda with lemon juice is a chemical change.
    *   **True.** (It produces carbon dioxide gas).

**4. Fill in the blanks:**
*   (i) Nalini observed that the handle of her cycle has got brown deposits. The brown deposits are due to **rusting**, and this is a **chemical** change.
*   (ii) Folding a handkerchief is a **physical** change and can be **reversed**.
*   (iii) A chemical process in which a substance reacts with oxygen with evolution of heat is called **combustion**, and this is a **chemical** change.
*   (iv) Magnesium, when burnt in air, produces a substance called **magnesium oxide**. The substance formed is **basic** in nature. Burning of magnesium is a **chemical** change.

**5. Are the changes of water to ice and water to steam, physical or chemical? Explain.**
*   **Answer:** They are **physical changes**. In both cases, the chemical makeup of the substance remains exactly the same: $H_2O$. Only the physical state (solid ice, liquid water, gaseous steam) and the spacing between the molecules have changed.

**6. Is curdling of milk a physical or chemical change? Justify your statement.**
*   **Answer:** Curdling of milk is a **chemical change**. The bacteria in the milk convert lactose sugars into lactic acid, creating an entirely new substance (curd) with a different taste (sour) and texture. It cannot be reversed back into fresh milk.

**7. Natural factors, such as wind, rain, etc., help in the formation of soil from rocks. Is this change physical or chemical and why?**
*   **Answer:** It involves **both** physical and chemical changes. The wind, freezing water, and rain cause rocks to break into smaller pieces without changing their makeup (**physical weathering**). However, water and oxygen also react with the minerals in the rocks to form new substances like iron oxide (**chemical weathering**). 

## Story Analysis (Page 70)

**8. Eco-friendly Prithvi Story**
*   "He chops vegetables, peels potatoes, and cuts fruits" $\rightarrow$ **Physical changes** (Just changing size/shape).
*   "He collects the seeds, fruits, and vegetable peels into a clay pot" $\rightarrow$ **Physical change** (Moving objects).
*   "The materials begin to decompose due to bacteria and fungi, forming compost" $\rightarrow$ **Chemical change** (New nutrient substances are formed).
*   "Seeds begin to germinate and small plants start to grow, eventually blooming" $\rightarrow$ **Chemical change** (Growth involves complex chemical reactions like photosynthesis and respiration).
*   *Suggested Title:* Prithvi's Compost Garden.

*   Tearing of paper $\rightarrow$ **A (Physical)**
*   Melting of ice $\rightarrow$ **A (Physical)**
*   Folding of clothes $\rightarrow$ **A (Physical)**
*   Rusting $\rightarrow$ **B (Chemical)**
*   Curdling of milk $\rightarrow$ **B (Chemical)**

*   Mixing baking soda with vinegar $\rightarrow$ **B (Chemical)**

**10. In which case(s) did lime water turn milky and why? (Fig 5.11)**
*   (a) Vinegar and baking soda
*   (b) Vinegar and common salt
*   (c) Lemon juice and baking soda
*   (d) Lemon juice and vinegar
*   **Answer:** Cases **(a) and (c)** will turn the lime water milky. Both vinegar and lemon juice contain acids. When an acid is mixed with baking soda (a carbonate), it causes a chemical reaction that releases **Carbon Dioxide gas ($CO_2$)**. When $CO_2$ passes into the lime water, it forms insoluble calcium carbonate, making the liquid look milky. Salt and mixed acids do not produce this gas.
