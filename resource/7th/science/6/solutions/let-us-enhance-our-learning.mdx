## Questions and Solutions (Pages 85 - 87)

**1. Ramesh, an 11-year-old boy, developed a few pimples on his face. His mother told him that this is because of ongoing biological changes in his body.**
**(i) What could be the possible reasons for the development of these pimples on his face?**
*   **Solution:** During adolescence, hormonal changes cause the sebaceous (oil) glands in the skin to become more active and produce excess oil. This excess oil, along with dead skin cells, can clog the skin pores. This clogging leads to bacterial infections, which manifest as red bumps known as acne or pimples.
**(ii) What can he do to get some relief from these pimples?**
*   **Solution:** Ramesh should practice good personal hygiene by washing his face regularly with mild soap and water to remove excess oil and dirt. He should avoid squeezing or picking at the pimples to prevent scarring and further infection. Eating a healthy diet and drinking plenty of water also helps.

**2. Which of the following food groups would be a better option for adolescents and why?**
*   *(Image (i) shows burgers, fries, donuts, and soda. Image (ii) shows a balanced thali with roti, dal, vegetables, salad, and curd).*
*   **Solution:** Food group **(ii) (the balanced thali)** is the much better option. Adolescence is a period of rapid physical growth and development. The body requires a balanced diet rich in proteins (dal/curd), carbohydrates (roti), vitamins, and minerals (vegetables/salad) for optimal bone growth, blood formation, and energy. Food group (i) represents "junk food," which is high in unhealthy fats and sugars but lacks the essential nutrients required for a growing body.

**4. Shalu told her friend, “Adolescence brings only physical changes, like growing taller or developing body hair.” Is she correct? What would you change in this description of adolescence?**
*   **Solution:** Shalu is **incorrect**. While physical changes are a major part of adolescence, they are not the only changes. I would add that adolescence also brings significant **emotional and behavioural changes** (such as mood swings, stronger emotions, and new ways of thinking), as well as internal biological changes related to **reproductive capability** (like the onset of the menstrual cycle in girls).

**5. During a discussion in the class, some of the students raised the following points. What questions would you ask them to check the correctness of these points?**
**(i) Adolescents do not need to worry about behavioural changes.**
*   *Question to ask:* "If behavioural changes like intense mood swings or increased peer pressure aren't managed thoughtfully, couldn't they lead to poor decision-making or risky behaviours?"
**(ii) If someone tries a harmful substance once, they can stop anytime they want.**
*   *Question to ask:* "If harmful substances are scientifically proven to be highly chemically addictive, altering the brain's chemistry to create strong cravings, is it really that easy to just 'stop anytime' after trying it once?"

**6. Adolescents sometimes experience mood swings. On some days, they feel very energetic and happy, while on other days, they may feel low. What other behavioural changes are associated with this age?**
*   **Solution:** Other behavioural changes include:
    *   Seeking more independence and personal identity.
    *   Increased sensitivity to the opinions and acceptance of peers (peer pressure).
    *   Developing a deeper sense of empathy and a desire to engage in social causes.
    *   Feeling easily irritated or confused due to hormonal fluctuations.

**7. While using a toilet, Mohini noticed that used sanitary pads were scattered near the bin. She got upset and shared her feelings with her friends. What menstrual hygiene and sanitary habits would you suggest to your friends?**
*   **Solution:** 
    1. Always wrap used sanitary pads properly in newspaper or a designated wrapper before disposal.
    2. Dispose of the wrapped pads *inside* the dustbin, never around it or flushed down the toilet (which causes blockages).
    3. Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water after changing pads.
    4. Change pads regularly (every 4-6 hours) to maintain personal hygiene and prevent infections.

**8. Mary and Manoj were classmates. Mary developed a bulge on her neck and the doctor gave her medication and an iodine-rich diet. Manoj developed a bump on his neck, but the doctor said it was a normal part of growing up. What could be the possible reason for advising them differently?**
*   **Solution:** 
    *   **Manoj's Bump:** The bump on Manoj's neck is the **Adam's apple**. This is a normal physical change during male puberty caused by the growth of the voice box (larynx), leading to a deeper voice. It requires no medical treatment.
    *   **Mary's Bulge:** The bulge on Mary's neck is likely a medical condition called **Goitre**, which is the swelling of the thyroid gland. This is caused by a deficiency of iodine in the diet, which is why the doctor prescribed medication and an iodine-rich diet to treat it.
