## Project 1: Mental Health Awareness

Adolescence can be an emotionally overwhelming time. Mental health is just as important as physical health! 

**Task:** Find out about some personalities and organisations working in your locality (or nationally) to improve the mental health of youth. Imagine you get to interview them. List at least five questions you would ask.

**Example Questions:**
1.  What are the most common emotional struggles you see teenagers facing today?
2.  How can a teenager tell the difference between normal "mood swings" and a serious mental health issue like depression?
3.  What are some simple daily habits students can practice to manage exam stress and peer pressure?
4.  How can friends support each other if they notice someone is feeling isolated or sad?
5.  What resources or hotlines are available for adolescents who need someone to talk to confidentially?

## Project 2: Social Awareness Role Play

**Task:** Perform a role play with your classmates on the theme **‘Child marriage: A social evil’**.

**Themes to Highlight:**
*   **Physical Health:** How early marriage negatively impacts the overall well-being and reproductive health of young girls whose bodies are not yet fully matured.
*   **Education:** How it forces children to drop out of school, ending their educational journey and limiting their future career opportunities.
*   **Mental Health:** The emotional trauma and heavy responsibilities forced upon children before they are psychologically ready.

*Goal: Use drama and storytelling to raise awareness and encourage your community to protect the rights of children!*

## Project 3: Physical Fitness through Yoga

**Task:** 21 June is celebrated as International Yoga Day. Organise a small camp with the help of your teachers and practice some *asanas* (yoga postures).

**Why Yoga?**
Regular physical activity is vital during adolescence. Yoga is uniquely beneficial because it:
*   Builds physical stamina and flexibility.
*   Helps manage the hormonal changes and physical awkwardness of rapid growth spurts.
*   Incorporates breathing exercises (Pranayama) which are incredibly effective at calming the mind, reducing stress, and stabilizing mood swings!

<Callout variant="tip">
**Try these beginner Asanas:**
*   **Tadasana (Mountain Pose):** Great for posture and height awareness.
*   **Vrikshasana (Tree Pose):** Excellent for improving focus and balance.
*   **Balasana (Child's Pose):** Perfect for resting and calming a busy mind.
</Callout>
