## Secondary Sexual Characteristics

In the previous section, we discussed changes like voice deepening, the growth of facial hair in boys, and the development of breasts in girls. 

While these changes are easily observable, they are *not* directly involved in the process of reproduction. However, they help distinguish males from females. For this reason, they are called **secondary sexual characteristics**.

The appearance of these characteristics marks the onset of **puberty**.

<Callout variant="info">
**What is Puberty?**
Puberty is the specific stage within adolescence in which the body undergoes internal and external changes to develop into an adult capable of reproduction.
</Callout>

## Changes Indicating Reproductive Capability

Adolescence involves internal changes that are not visible from the outside. One major change is the maturation of the reproductive organs.

### The Menstrual Cycle
An important internal change associated with adolescent girls is the onset of the **menstrual cycle**. 
*   It is a natural process and a key sign of good reproductive health.
*   It generally recurs every 28–30 days (though cycles ranging from 21–35 days are also healthy and normal).
*   **Menstruation:** The specific phase of the cycle when blood discharge occurs from the body. It typically lasts for three to seven days. 
*   Menstruation usually begins at puberty and naturally stops around the age of 45–55, marking the end of a woman's reproductive capability.

### Breaking Myths about Menstruation
There are many wrong beliefs, myths, and taboos surrounding menstruation that lead to unnecessary fear or shame. For example, the physical isolation of menstruating girls is a practice that unfortunately still exists but has absolutely zero scientific basis. Menstruation is a completely normal, healthy biological process.
