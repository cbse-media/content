## Building a Strong Foundation

The curiosity and excitement of adolescence give you a new viewpoint on the world. By making thoughtful decisions and adopting good habits now, you can have a powerful positive impact on your well-being.

### 1. Meeting Nutritional Needs
Because your body is growing rapidly, a nutritious diet is critical. You cannot rely on junk food!
*   **Proteins & Carbohydrates:** Needed for proper growth and energy to perform well on the playground.
*   **Calcium:** (Found in milk, curd, paneer) Essential for optimal bone growth.
*   **Iron:** (Found in spinach, kidney beans, dried fruits) Essential for the formation of blood. 

*Note: Adolescents, especially girls, may sometimes suffer from blood-related health problems (like anaemia) due to a deficiency of iron or Vitamin B12. Eating iron-rich foods is vital!*

### 2. Personal Hygiene
Maintaining body hygiene protects us from potential infections, especially as our sweat and oil glands become more active.
*   **Menstrual Hygiene:** For girls, using sanitary pads or clean, reusable cloth pads is essential for health and comfort. Used pads must be wrapped in paper and disposed of properly in a dustbin to ensure environmental cleanliness.

### 3. Physical Activities
Regular exercise keeps your body and mind fit, builds stamina, and is a great way to boost your mood and manage stress!

### 4. Balanced Social Life and Cyber Safety
We must be respectful and polite when interacting with others. Today, a lot of interaction happens online. 
*   **Cyberbullying:** Using digital devices to harass others, spread rumors, or share personal info without consent is cyberbullying. If you encounter this, do not panic—seek help from parents or teachers immediately.
*   **Online Privacy:** Never share personal photos or sensitive information with strangers online.

### 5. Avoiding Harmful Substances (Say NO!)
During adolescence, you might feel curiosity or peer pressure to try harmful substances like tobacco, cigarettes, alcohol, or drugs.
*   These substances severely damage physical and mental health (e.g., smoking damages the lungs).
*   They are highly **addictive**. A strong urge develops to take them again and again, leading to **substance abuse**.
*   **Say NO** the first time, and every time. Your health and future are in your hands!
