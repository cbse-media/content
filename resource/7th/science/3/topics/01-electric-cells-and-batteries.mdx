## The Electric Cell

An **electric cell** is a portable source of electrical energy. If you look closely at a standard cell (like the ones used in remote controls or clocks), you will notice a few key features:

1.  **Two Terminals:** Every electric cell has two ends, called terminals.
2.  **Positive Terminal (+):** This is the end with the small protruding metal cap.
3.  **Negative Terminal (-):** This is the flat metal disc on the opposite end.

Electricity is generated inside the cell through chemical reactions.

## What is a Battery?

In many devices, a single cell does not provide enough energy, so we use two or more cells together. 

<Callout variant="info">
**Definition:**
A combination of two or more electric cells is called a **battery**.
</Callout>

### How to Connect Cells in a Battery

To make a battery work correctly, the cells must be connected in a specific order:
**The positive terminal (+) of one cell must be connected to the negative terminal (-) of the next cell.**

If you put two positive terminals together, or two negative terminals together, the current will not flow properly!

*Note: In everyday language, people often call a single cell a "battery" (like your mobile phone battery), but technically, a battery is a collection of cells.*

### Cells Side-by-Side
Sometimes, devices like remote controls require you to place cells side-by-side instead of end-to-end. If you look inside the battery compartment, you will see a thick wire or metal strip connecting the positive terminal of one slot to the negative terminal of the next slot, achieving the exact same connection!
