## The Electric Lamp

There are two common types of lamps used in portable devices like torches.

### 1. Incandescent Lamp (Traditional Bulb)
If you look inside the glass bulb of an older torch, you will see a very thin wire. This wire is called the **filament**. 
*   The filament is supported by two thicker wires.
*   One thick wire connects to the **metal case** at the base of the bulb.
*   The other thick wire connects to the **metal tip** at the very bottom center of the base.
*   These two points (the case and the tip) are the two **terminals** of the lamp.

When electric current passes through the filament, it gets extremely hot and glows to produce light!

### 2. Light Emitting Diode (LED)
Modern torches use LEDs. LEDs do not have a filament. They also have two terminals, but they look like two wire legs:
*   The **longer wire** is the **positive (+)** terminal.
*   The **shorter wire** is the **negative (-)** terminal.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Crucial Difference:**
An incandescent bulb will glow no matter which way you connect it to the battery. 
An LED is directional! Current can only flow through it in one direction. The positive (longer) wire *must* connect to the positive terminal of the battery, and the negative (shorter) wire to the negative terminal.
</Callout>

## An Electrical Circuit

To make a lamp glow, we must create a path for the electricity to flow. 

<Callout variant="info">
**Definition:**
An **electrical circuit** is a complete, unbroken path that allows electric current to flow from the positive terminal of a power source, through a device (like a lamp), and back to the negative terminal.
</Callout>

*   The lamp will **only** glow when the circuit is complete.
*   The direction of the electric current is taken to be from the **positive (+)** terminal to the **negative (-)** terminal of the electric cell.

### Why might a lamp not glow?
1.  The circuit is broken (a wire is loose).
2.  The cells are connected incorrectly (e.g., positive to positive).
3.  With an LED, the polarity is reversed (long leg connected to negative).
4.  The lamp is "fused" (the thin filament inside the incandescent bulb is broken, stopping the flow of current).
