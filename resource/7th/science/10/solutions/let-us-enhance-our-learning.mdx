## Data Table & Conceptual Questions (Page 150)

**1. Complete the following table comparing Photosynthesis and Respiration:**

| S.No. | Feature | Photosynthesis | Respiration |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1.** | **Raw materials** | Carbon dioxide, Water (Sunlight & Chlorophyll) | Glucose (Food), Oxygen |
| **2.** | **Products** | Glucose (Carbohydrates), Oxygen | Carbon dioxide, Water, Energy |
| **3.** | **Word equation** | $\text{CO}_2 + \text{Water} \xrightarrow{\text{light}} \text{Glucose} + \text{O}_2$ | $\text{Glucose} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 + \text{Water} + \text{Energy}$ |
| **4.** | **Importance** | Produces food for the plant (and the entire food chain) and replenishes Oxygen in the air. | Releases the usable energy required for the plant's growth, repair, and survival. |

---

**2. Imagine a situation where all the organisms that carry out photosynthesis on the earth have disappeared. What would be the impact of this on living organisms?**
*   **Solution:** If photosynthesis stops, all life on Earth would eventually perish. 
    1.  **Starvation:** Plants are the base of the global food web. Without plants making food, herbivores would starve, followed quickly by the carnivores that eat them. 
    2.  **Suffocation:** Photosynthesis is the primary source of Oxygen ($O_2$) in our atmosphere. Without it, animals and humans would rapidly deplete the remaining oxygen through respiration and suffocate.

**3. A potato slice shows the presence of starch with iodine solution. Where does the starch in potatoes come from? Where is the food synthesised in the plant, and how does it reach the potato?**
*   **Solution:** The starch does not originate in the potato itself (which grows underground in the dark). The food is synthesised in the **leaves** of the potato plant via photosynthesis. Once the glucose is made in the leaves, it is transported downwards through the tube-like structures called **phloem** to the underground stem (the potato tuber), where it is converted into starch and stored for later use.

**4. Does the broad and flat structure of leaves make plants more efficient for photosynthesis? Justify your answer.**
*   **Solution:** Yes, absolutely! A broad and flat structure maximizes the **surface area** of the leaf. A larger surface area allows the leaf to capture the maximum amount of sunlight possible, and provides more space for stomata, maximizing the intake of Carbon Dioxide.

**5. $X$ is broken down using $Y$ to release carbon dioxide, $Z$, and energy.**
$$ X + Y \rightarrow \text{Carbon dioxide} + Z + \text{Energy} $$
**What do $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ stand for?**
*   **Solution:** This is the word equation for Respiration.
    *   **X** = Glucose (Food)
    *   **Y** = Oxygen
    *   **Z** = Water

## Experimental Analysis (Page 151)

**6. Krishna set-up an experiment with two potted plants of the same size. One was placed in sunlight, the other in a dark room. (Fig 10.10)**
**(i) What idea might she be testing through this experiment?**
*   **Answer:** She is testing whether **sunlight is essential for plant growth and photosynthesis**.
**(ii) What are the visible differences in plants in both the conditions?**
*   **Answer:** After several days, the plant in the sunlight will be green, healthy, and growing. The plant in the dark room will appear pale, yellowing, wilted, and its growth will be stunted.
**(iii) According to you, leaves of which plants confirm the iodine test for the presence of starch?**
*   **Answer:** Only the leaves from the plant kept in the **sunlight**. The plant in the dark could not perform photosynthesis and therefore could not synthesize starch.

**7. Vani believes that ‘carbon dioxide is essential for photosynthesis’. She puts up an experimental set-up (Fig 10.11):**
*   (a) Sunlight with $CO_2$
*   (b) Sunlight without $CO_2$ (Likely using Caustic Soda to absorb it)
*   (c) Dark with $CO_2$
*   (d) Dark without $CO_2$

**(i) In which plant(s) will starch be formed?**
*   **Answer:** Only in plant **(a)**. It is the only one that has both sunlight and $CO_2$.
**(ii) In which plant(s) will starch not be formed?**
*   **Answer:** Plants **(b), (c), and (d)**. (b) lacks $CO_2$, (c) lacks sunlight, (d) lacks both.
**(iii) In which plant(s) will oxygen be generated?**
*   **Answer:** Only in plant **(a)**. Oxygen is a byproduct of photosynthesis.
**(iv) In which plant(s) will oxygen not be generated?**
*   **Answer:** Plants **(b), (c), and (d)**.

**8. Ananya took four test tubes (Fig 10.12). She added a carbon dioxide indicator. A (Snail), B (Water plant), C (Snail + Plant), D (Water only). She observed colour changes after 2-3 hours. What is she trying to find out?**
*   **Answer:** She is investigating the **gas exchange relationship between animals and plants** (respiration vs. photosynthesis).
    *   **Tube A:** The snail respires, producing $CO_2$. The indicator will show high $CO_2$.
    *   **Tube B:** In sunlight, the plant performs photosynthesis, consuming $CO_2$. The indicator will show low/decreasing $CO_2$.
    *   **Tube C:** The snail produces $CO_2$ through respiration, and the plant consumes that exact $CO_2$ for photosynthesis! The system is balanced. The indicator should show little to no change in $CO_2$ levels.
    *   **Tube D:** The control tube. No change.
    She will know she is correct if Tube A shows an increase in $CO_2$, Tube B shows a decrease, and Tube C remains relatively neutral.
