## Introduction

We know that animals must eat food to grow and survive. But have you ever seen a plant "eating" food like an animal does? As plants grow from a tiny seed into a massive tree, where does all the material come from to build those new leaves, thick branches, and sweet fruits? 

Plants are incredible because they are nature's ultimate factories. In this chapter, we will explore the vital **Life Processes in Plants**, learning how they magically create their own food using nothing but sunlight, air, and water, and how they transport these vital nutrients from their roots all the way up to their highest leaves!

**Did you know?**
The ancient Indian text *Vrikshayurveda* states: "Trees do not produce fruits and flowers merely because they are planted." It recognized thousands of years ago that soil health, water, and proper agricultural practices are essential for plant growth!

## Key Equations and Concepts

*   **Photosynthesis (Food Production):** Happens only in the presence of sunlight and chlorophyll.
    $$ \text{Carbon dioxide} + \text{Water} \xrightarrow{\text{Sunlight } \& \text{ Chlorophyll}} \text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen} $$
*   **Respiration (Energy Release):** Happens continuously, day and night, in all living cells.
    $$ \text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen} \rightarrow \text{Carbon dioxide} + \text{Water} + \text{Energy} $$
*   **Stomata:** Tiny pores on the leaf surface responsible for exchanging gases ($CO_2$ and $O_2$).
*   **Xylem:** The tissue that transports water and minerals UP from the roots.
*   **Phloem:** The tissue that transports food (sugars) DOWN and around from the leaves.
