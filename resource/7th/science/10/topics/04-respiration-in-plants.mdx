## Releasing the Energy

We know that plants manufacture food (glucose) during the day using photosynthesis. But making the food is only half the story. To actually grow, repair damage, and survive, the plant must **release the energy** stored inside that glucose.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:**
The process by which living things break down glucose in the presence of oxygen to release usable energy, carbon dioxide, and water is called **respiration**. 
</Callout>

**The Word Equation for Respiration:**
$$ \text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen} \rightarrow \text{Carbon dioxide} + \text{Water} + \text{Energy} $$

## Photosynthesis vs. Respiration

It is very easy to confuse these two processes. They are essentially opposites!

| Feature | Photosynthesis | Respiration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Purpose** | To *make and store* food/energy | To *break down* food and *release* energy |
| **When it happens** | Only during the day (requires sunlight) | **Continuously** (Day and Night!) |
| **Gas Exchange** | Takes in $CO_2$, releases $O_2$ | Takes in $O_2$, releases $CO_2$ |
| **Where it happens**| Only in green parts (leaves) containing chlorophyll | In **all** living parts of the plant (roots, stem, leaves, seeds) |

### Testing Plant Respiration

Do seeds really breathe out Carbon Dioxide?
If you place germinating *moong* bean seeds in a sealed flask and connect a tube from the flask into a test tube containing lime water, the lime water will slowly turn **milky white**. 

Because germinating seeds are growing rapidly in the dark, they are not performing photosynthesis. They are only respiring, taking in oxygen and breathing out $CO_2$, which turns the lime water milky!
