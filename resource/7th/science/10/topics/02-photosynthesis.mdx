## Leaves: The 'Food Factories'

Unlike animals, plants do not eat food. They are **autotrophs**, meaning they make their own food! This food production primarily happens in the leaves. 

Leaves are generally broad and flat to catch maximum sunlight. They are green because they contain a vital green pigment called **chlorophyll**. 

### The Iodine Test for Starch
Plants produce food as simple sugars (glucose), but they quickly convert it into **starch** for storage. If we want to know if a leaf has produced food, we test it for starch using Iodine solution. 
*(Note: Iodine turns blue-black in the presence of starch).*

## The Ingredients for Photosynthesis

Through a series of experiments, scientists have proven that four things are required for a plant to make food:

1.  **Sunlight:** The energy source. A plant kept in the dark cannot produce starch.
2.  **Chlorophyll:** If you test a variegated leaf (a leaf with both green and white patches), only the green patches (containing chlorophyll) will turn blue-black with iodine. The non-green patches cannot produce food.
3.  **Water:** Absorbed from the soil.
4.  **Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$):** If you trap a leaf inside a bottle containing caustic soda (which removes $CO_2$ from the air), that leaf will not produce starch!

<Callout variant="tip">
**What is Photosynthesis?**
The process by which plants prepare food (glucose) using carbon dioxide and water in the presence of sunlight and chlorophyll is called **photosynthesis**.
</Callout>

**The Word Equation:**
$$ \text{Carbon dioxide} + \text{Water} \xrightarrow{\text{Sunlight } \& \text{ Chlorophyll}} \text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen} $$

## Gas Exchange: Stomata and Oxygen

During photosynthesis, the plant takes in $CO_2$ and releases Oxygen ($O_2$) as a byproduct. But how do these gases get in and out of the leaf?

If you look at the underside of a leaf under a microscope, you will see tiny pores called **stomata**. These microscopic openings are the "doors" of the leaf, allowing $CO_2$ to enter the food factory and letting the fresh Oxygen escape into the atmosphere for us to breathe!
