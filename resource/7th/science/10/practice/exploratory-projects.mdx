## Project 1: Develop a Bottle Garden (Terrarium)

You can build a self-sustaining ecosystem right in your classroom! 

**How to do it:**
1.  Take a large, clear glass or plastic jar/bottle with a tight-fitting lid.
2.  Add a layer of small pebbles at the bottom for drainage, followed by a layer of activated charcoal (to keep the water clean), and then a thick layer of potting soil.
3.  Carefully plant a small, slow-growing, humidity-loving plant (like a spider plant, fittonia, or moss).
4.  Lightly water the soil so it is damp (not soaking wet).
5.  **Seal the bottle completely tight** and place it in a spot with bright, indirect sunlight.

**What happens?**
You never need to water it again! 
*   **The Water Cycle:** Water evaporates from the soil and transpires from the leaves, condenses on the glass walls, and "rains" back down onto the soil.
*   **The Carbon Cycle:** During the day, the plant performs **photosynthesis**, absorbing $CO_2$ and releasing $O_2$. During the night (and continuously), the plant performs **respiration**, absorbing the $O_2$ and releasing $CO_2$ back into the sealed atmosphere. The plant perfectly recycles its own gases!

## Project 2: Water Transportation Experiment Design

**The Challenge:** Design an experiment to observe if water transportation in plants is quicker in warm or cold conditions. 

**Proposed Experimental Design:**
1.  **Materials:** Two identical stalks of white celery (or white carnation flowers), two beakers, red food coloring, a refrigerator, and a warm, sunny window.
2.  **Procedure:** 
    *   Fill both beakers with the same amount of water and add 10 drops of red food coloring to each.
    *   Cut the bottom 1 inch off the celery stalks and place one in each beaker.
    *   Place Beaker A in the refrigerator (Cold condition).
    *   Place Beaker B in a warm, sunny window (Warm condition).
3.  **Observation:** Check the stalks every 30 minutes. Measure how high the red dye has traveled up the xylem tubes of the celery in both conditions.
4.  **Hypothesis:** The warm condition should show faster transportation. Heat increases the rate of evaporation (transpiration) from the leaves, which creates a stronger pulling force (transpirational pull) to draw water up the xylem faster!

## Project 3: Visit a Greenhouse

If you have a local nursery or agricultural greenhouse, arrange a visit! 

**What to observe:**
A greenhouse is a giant, controlled experiment designed to maximize **photosynthesis**. 
*   **Light:** The transparent glass/plastic roof maximizes sunlight exposure. In winter, they may use artificial grow lights.
*   **Temperature:** The enclosed space traps the sun's infrared radiation (the "greenhouse effect"), keeping the plants warm even in freezing weather. Warmth speeds up the chemical reactions of photosynthesis.
*   **Carbon Dioxide:** Some advanced greenhouses actively pump extra $CO_2$ gas into the air inside the greenhouse to "supercharge" the plants' food-making factories, leading to massive crop yields!
