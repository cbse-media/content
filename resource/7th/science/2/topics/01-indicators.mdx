## What is an Indicator?

We cannot (and should not!) taste every substance to find out if it is an acid or a base. Many chemicals are dangerous. Instead, we use special substances called **indicators**. Indicators change their colour when added to a solution containing an acidic or a basic substance.

Let's explore some amazing natural indicators you can find around you!

### 1. Litmus: The Classic Indicator

Litmus is the most commonly used natural indicator. It is extracted from **lichens** (a living organism formed by the association of a fungus and an alga). It is available as a solution or as strips of paper known as litmus paper.

*   **Blue Litmus Paper:** Turns **RED** when dipped in an acidic solution.
*   **Red Litmus Paper:** Turns **BLUE** when dipped in a basic solution.
*   **Neutral Solutions:** Do not change the colour of either red or blue litmus paper.

### 2. Red Rose Extract

You can make your own indicator using the petals of a red rose! By crushing the petals and soaking them in hot water, you get a coloured extract.

*   In an **Acidic** solution: The rose extract turns a bright shade of **RED** (or dark pink/magenta).
*   In a **Basic** solution: The rose extract turns **GREEN**.

### 3. Turmeric (Haldi)

Turmeric is a common spice in Indian households, known for its bright yellow colour. It acts as an excellent indicator, especially for bases.

*   In an **Acidic or Neutral** solution: Turmeric remains **YELLOW**.
*   In a **Basic** solution: Turmeric turns **RED**.

*Have you ever noticed a yellow curry stain on a white shirt turn red when you apply soap? That's because soap is basic, and turmeric is indicating its basic nature!*

### 4. Olfactory Indicators (Smell)

Did you know some indicators don't change colour, but instead change their smell? These are called **olfactory indicators**.

**Onion** is a great example. If you keep strips of cloth with chopped onions overnight, the cloth will absorb the strong smell of onions.
*   If you add a **Basic** solution (like baking soda) to the cloth, the onion smell disappears!
*   If you add an **Acidic** solution (like vinegar or lemon juice), the onion smell remains.
