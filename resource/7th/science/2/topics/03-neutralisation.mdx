## What Happens When Acid Meets Base?

We have learned that acids turn blue litmus red, and bases turn red litmus blue. But what happens if we mix an acid and a base together?

Let's imagine an experiment:
1. You take a few drops of lemon juice (Acid) in a test tube. It is acidic.
2. You add a drop of blue litmus. The solution turns **red**.
3. Now, you slowly add drops of lime water (Base) and swirl it. 
4. Eventually, the solution changes colour from red back to **blue**!

**Why did this happen?**
The lime water (base) slowly cancelled out the effect of the lemon juice (acid). When the solution of an acid is mixed with the solution of a base in sufficient quantities, they destroy each other's acidic and basic nature. The resulting solution is neither acidic nor basic. 

This reaction is called **Neutralisation**.

<Callout variant="tip">
**The Neutralisation Equation:**
Acid + Base $\rightarrow$ Salt + Water + Heat
*(During a neutralisation reaction, a new substance called a 'salt' is formed, and the test tube will feel warm because heat is released!)*
</Callout>

## Neutralisation in Daily Life

We use neutralisation reactions all the time to solve everyday problems!

### 1. Treating Insect Stings
When a red ant or a bee stings you, it injects an acidic liquid (**formic acid**) into your skin, causing a burning pain. 
**The Remedy:** Because the sting is acidic, we can neutralize it by rubbing a mild base on the skin, such as **moist baking soda** or calamine lotion. This cancels out the acid and relieves the pain.

### 2. Treating Soil for Agriculture
Plants do not grow well when the soil is too acidic or too basic. Excessive use of chemical fertilizers can make the soil very acidic.
**The Remedy:** 
*   If the soil is too **acidic**, farmers treat it with bases like **quicklime** (calcium oxide) or slaked lime (calcium hydroxide).
*   If the soil is too **basic**, organic matter (compost/manure) is added. Organic matter naturally releases acids as it decomposes, which neutralises the basic nature of the soil.

### 3. Factory Wastes
The wastes from many factories contain strong acids. If these are allowed to flow directly into rivers and lakes, the acids will kill fish and other aquatic organisms.
**The Remedy:** Factory wastes must be neutralised by adding **basic substances** before they are safely disposed of into water bodies.

### 4. Indigestion (Antacids)
Our stomach naturally produces hydrochloric acid to help digest food. However, eating spicy food or overeating can cause the stomach to produce too much acid, leading to painful indigestion or "acidity".
**The Remedy:** We take medicines called **antacids** (like Milk of Magnesia or Eno). These are mild bases that neutralise the excess acid in the stomach!
