## Acidic Substances

Are all acidic substances edible? Not all of them! However, the ones we commonly find in our kitchens share a distinct characteristic.

If you taste lemon juice, *amla* (gooseberry), tamarind, or raw mangoes, what do they have in common? They all taste **sour**.

Substances that taste sour tend to contain **acids**. The chemical nature of such substances is said to be **acidic**. The word 'acid' comes from the Latin word *acere*, which means sour. The acids in these substances are natural acids.

### Common Natural Acids

Here are some common edible substances and the acids present in them:

| Substance | Acid Present |
| :--- | :--- |
| Lemon / Orange | Citric acid |
| Amla / Citrus fruits | Ascorbic acid (Vitamin C) |
| Tamarind / Grapes | Tartaric acid |
| Vinegar | Acetic acid |
| Curd (Yogurt) | Lactic acid |
| Tomato / Spinach | Oxalic acid |

<Callout variant="warning">
**Caution!**
Never taste or touch any unknown substance in a laboratory to check if it is acidic or basic. Strong laboratory acids (like Hydrochloric acid or Sulphuric acid) are highly corrosive and can cause severe burns.
</Callout>

## Basic Substances

Now let's think about substances like baking soda. Does it taste sour? No. If you taste a tiny bit, it tastes somewhat **bitter**. 

More importantly, if you take a pinch of baking soda solution and rub it between your fingers, how does it feel? It feels **soapy or slippery**. 

Substances that generally taste bitter and feel slippery to the touch are known as **bases**. The nature of such substances is said to be **basic**.

### Common Bases in Daily Life

| Substance | Common Base Present |
| :--- | :--- |
| Soap / Washing powder | Sodium hydroxide / Potassium hydroxide |
| Lime water (*Chuna* water) | Calcium hydroxide |
| Baking soda | Sodium bicarbonate |
| Window cleaner | Ammonium hydroxide |
| Antacids (Eno) | Magnesium hydroxide |

*Note: Do not confuse 'lime water' (a chemical base made of calcium hydroxide) with lemon water (a citrus drink containing acid).*

## Neutral Substances

Substances that are neither acidic nor basic are called **neutral** substances. They do not change the colour of litmus paper, turmeric, or red rose extract.

Examples of neutral substances include:
*   Pure water (distilled water)
*   Sugar solution
*   Common salt (Sodium chloride) solution
