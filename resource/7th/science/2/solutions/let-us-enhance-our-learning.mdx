## Multiple Choice and Sequence Questions (Page 14)

**1. A solution turns the red litmus paper to blue. Excess addition of which of the following solution would reverse the change?**
*   **Logic:** The original solution turned red litmus blue, meaning it is a **Base**. To reverse the change (turn it back to red), we must neutralize the base and add an excess of an **Acid**.
*   **Options:** (i) Lime water (Base), (ii) Baking soda (Base), (iii) Vinegar (Acid), (iv) Common salt (Neutral).
*   **Answer:** **(iii) Vinegar**

**2. You have three unknown solutions A, B, and C.** 
*   **Test A:** Red litmus turns blue $\implies$ **A is Basic**.
*   **Test B:** Turmeric turns red $\implies$ **B is Basic**.
*   **Test C:** Red rose extract turns green $\implies$ **C is Basic**.
*   **Answer:** **(iv) Basic, basic, and basic**

**3. Label the nature of solutions in the containers using red rose extract strips:**
*   **Fig 2.13:** The strip turns GREEN. $\implies$ **Basic**
*   **Fig 2.14:** The strip turns RED/DARK PINK. $\implies$ **Acidic**
*   **Fig 2.15:** The strip shows NO CHANGE (stays light pink/colorless). $\implies$ **Neutral**

**4. A liquid sample was tested: Red litmus (No change), Blue litmus (Turned red), Turmeric (No change).**
*   **Logic:** Blue litmus turning red is the definitive test for an acid. Turmeric does not change color in acids, which aligns with the observation. Red litmus stays red in acids, which also aligns.
*   **Answer:** The liquid is **Acidic**.

## Analytical Questions (Pages 15-16)

**5. Manya is blindfolded. She is given two unknown solutions to test whether they are acidic or basic. Which indicator should Manya use?**
*   **Answer:** Manya should use an **Olfactory Indicator** (like onion extract or vanilla extract). Since she is blindfolded, she cannot see colour changes (like litmus or turmeric). She must rely on her sense of smell. For example, if she uses onion-soaked strips, the solution that destroys the onion smell is the Base, and the one where the smell remains is the Acid.

**6. Suggest materials for writing the secret message from the beginning of the chapter, and what could be in the spray bottle.**
*   **Scenario 1 (Turmeric & Soap):** 
    *   *Writing Material:* Soap solution (Base) or Baking soda solution.
    *   *Spray Bottle:* Turmeric water (Indicator).
    *   *Colour Obtained:* The writing will turn **Red**.
*   **Scenario 2 (Phenolphthalein - if known):** 
    *   *Writing Material:* Phenolphthalein indicator (dries clear).
    *   *Spray Bottle:* Basic solution (like baking soda).
    *   *Colour Obtained:* The writing will turn **Pink**.

**7. Grape juice was mixed with red rose extract; the mixture got a tint of red colour. What will happen if baking soda is added?**
*   **Answer:** The red tint indicates that grape juice is **acidic**. Baking soda is a **base**. When baking soda is added to the acidic grape juice mixture, a **neutralisation reaction** will occur. As the solution becomes basic, the red rose extract (indicator) will change its colour from red to **green**. You will also likely see bubbles of carbon dioxide gas forming from the baking soda reacting with the acid!

**8. Keerthi wrote a secret message using orange juice. Which indicator would you use to make it visible?**
*   **Answer:** Orange juice contains citric acid (it is acidic). We need an indicator that changes colour in the presence of an acid. 
    *   She could gently spray the paper with **Blue Litmus solution**, which would turn red over the letters.
    *   She could spray **Red Rose extract**, which would turn bright red/magenta over the letters. 
    *(Note: Turmeric would not work well here, as turmeric does not change colour in acids).*

**10. Three liquids are given: vinegar, baking soda solution, and sugar solution. Can you identify them using ONLY turmeric paper?**
*   **Answer:** Yes, with a clever step-by-step process!
    1.  Test all three liquids with turmeric paper. The one that turns the turmeric paper **RED** is the **Baking soda solution** (Base).
    2.  Now you have identified the base. The remaining two (vinegar and sugar) both show no colour change on turmeric.
    3.  Take the turmeric paper that just turned red (from the baking soda). Add drops of the two remaining unknown liquids to this red paper separately.
    4.  The liquid that changes the red paper *back* to yellow is the **Vinegar** (Acid). It neutralizes the baking soda, making the turmeric return to its original colour.
    5.  The liquid that does not change the red colour back to yellow is the **Sugar solution** (Neutral).

**11. Red rose extract turns liquid X to green. What is the nature of X? What happens when excess amla juice is added?**
*   **Answer:** Liquid X is a **Base** (because rose extract turns green in bases). Amla juice contains ascorbic acid (Vitamin C). When excess amla juice is added to the basic liquid X, it will **neutralise** the base and eventually make the whole solution acidic. The colour of the rose extract will change from green, back through its neutral colour, and finally turn **red**.

## Flowchart Completion (Page 15)

**Imagine a garden with plants showing signs of poor health.**

1.  **Box 1:** The soil can be **Acidic** in nature.
2.  **Box 2:** The soil can be **Basic** in nature.
3.  **Center Box:** Which indicator can be used to test the nature of the soil? $\rightarrow$ **Litmus paper** (by making a soil-water suspension).
4.  **Bottom Left Box:** The acidic soil can be treated with $\rightarrow$ **Lime (Calcium oxide / Quicklime)**.
5.  **Bottom Right Box:** The basic soil can be treated with $\rightarrow$ **Organic matter (Manure / Compost)**.
