## What is Convection?

When you boil water, the water at the very bottom of the pan gets heated first. As it heats up, it expands, becomes lighter, and **rises to the top**. The cooler, heavier water from the top and sides then sinks down to take its place. This new water gets heated, rises, and the cycle continues.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:** The process of heat transfer by the *actual movement* of particles of a liquid or gas from one place to another is called **convection**.
</Callout>

This is why smoke from a fire always rises! Smoke is a mixture of hot gases, which are lighter than the surrounding cooler air.

## Land and Sea Breezes

A fascinating natural example of convection occurs in coastal areas, driven by the fact that **land heats up and cools down much faster than water**.

### Sea Breeze (During the Day)
1. During the day, the sun heats both the land and the sea.
2. The land heats up *faster* than the water.
3. The air directly above the hot land becomes hot, expands, and rises.
4. To fill the empty space left by the rising hot air, cooler, heavier air from above the sea rushes towards the land.
5. This movement of cooler air from the sea to the land is called a **sea breeze**. 
*(This is why it feels pleasantly cool on a beach during a hot day!)*

### Land Breeze (At Night)
1. At night, the process reverses. Both the land and the sea begin to cool down.
2. The land cools down *faster* than the sea. Therefore, the sea water remains relatively warmer than the land.
3. The air above the sea is now warmer and rises.
4. Cooler air from the land rushes towards the sea to take its place.
5. This movement of cooler air from the land to the sea is called a **land breeze**.
