## Heat Transfer Without a Medium

If you stand in front of a fireplace, you feel warm. But how does the heat reach you? 
*   It cannot be **conduction** because air is a poor conductor.
*   It cannot be **convection** because hot air rises upwards (towards the ceiling), not sideways towards you.

The heat from the fire reaches you through a process called **radiation**. 

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:** The transfer of heat directly from a hot object to a colder one as waves or rays, *without requiring any material medium*, is called **radiation**.
</Callout>

This is exactly how the heat from the Sun reaches the Earth! It travels through millions of kilometers of empty space (a vacuum) where there is no air or liquid to conduct or convect the heat. 

*Note: ALL hot objects radiate heat to their surroundings. A hot cup of tea cools down primarily by radiating its heat away.*

## Radiation and Colors

Have you ever wondered why people wear white or light-colored clothes in the summer, and dark-colored clothes in the winter?

It has to do with how different colors interact with thermal radiation!

*   **Light-colored surfaces** are excellent *reflectors* of heat. They reflect most of the radiant heat that falls on them, keeping the wearer cool and comfortable during the hot summer.
*   **Dark-colored surfaces** are excellent *absorbers* of heat. They absorb the radiant heat that falls on them, trapping the warmth and keeping the wearer comfortable during the cold winter.
