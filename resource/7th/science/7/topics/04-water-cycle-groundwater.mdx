## The Engine of the Water Cycle

The Sun is the main source of heat for the Earth. Its radiation causes massive natural phenomena.

Water exists in three states on Earth:
1.  **Liquid:** Oceans, rivers, lakes.
2.  **Solid:** Snow, glaciers, ice sheets.
3.  **Gas:** Water vapour in the atmosphere.

The heat from the Sun drives the **Water Cycle**:
1.  **Evaporation:** The Sun's heat causes water in oceans and lakes to evaporate into water vapour. Plants also release water vapour through *transpiration*.
2.  **Condensation:** As the warm water vapour rises, it cools down (loses heat) and condenses to form clouds.
3.  **Precipitation:** The condensed water falls back to the Earth as rain, snow, or hail.

The continuous movement of water upward as vapour and downward as precipitation is essential for redistributing and replenishing freshwater across the globe.

## Seepage and Groundwater

What happens to the rainwater once it hits the ground? Some of it flows into rivers, but much of it seeps down into the earth.

<Callout variant="info">
**Key Definitions:**
*   **Infiltration:** The process of surface water seeping down through the soil and rocks. Water infiltrates fastest through wide, open materials like gravel, slower through sand, and very slowly through dense clay.
*   **Groundwater:** The water that successfully seeps through and gets stored in the pore spaces of sediments and rock openings beneath the surface.
*   **Aquifer:** The underground layers of rocks and sediments that actively store this groundwater. When we dig a well, we are tapping into an aquifer!
</Callout>

### The Groundwater Crisis

Groundwater is NOT unlimited. We are facing a crisis because:
1.  **Over-extraction:** A growing population pumps out water faster than rain can replace it.
2.  **Decreased Infiltration:** Deforestation and covering cities in concrete prevent rainwater from seeping into the ground to recharge the aquifers.

**Solutions:** Techniques like **rainwater harvesting** and digging recharge pits help capture rainwater and force it back underground, ensuring a sustainable groundwater supply.
