## Multiple Choice Questions (Pages 102 - 103)

**1. (i) Your father bought a saucepan made of two different materials, A and B. The materials A and B have the following properties:**
*(Observation of Fig 7.14: Part A is the pan body, Part B is the handle).*
*   **Logic:** The body of the pan (A) needs to transfer heat to cook the food, so it must be a **good conductor**. The handle (B) needs to protect our hands from burning, so it must be a **poor conductor** (insulator).
*   **Answer:** **(c) A is a good conductor and B is a poor conductor of heat.**

**1. (ii) Pins are stuck to a metal strip with wax and a burning candle is kept below the rod. Which of the following will happen?**
*(Observation of Fig 7.15: The candle is placed directly between pins II and III).*
*   **Logic:** Heat transfers through conduction from the hottest part to the colder parts. Because the flame is right in the middle, the heat will travel outwards in both directions simultaneously. Therefore, the wax holding the pins closest to the heat source on both sides will melt first.
*   **Answer:** **(d) Pins II and III will fall almost at the same time.**

**1. (iii) A smoke detector detects smoke and sounds an alarm. The most suitable place for this device will be:**
*   **Logic:** Smoke is composed of hot gases. By the process of **convection**, hot gases expand, become lighter, and always rise upwards. Therefore, the smoke will collect at the highest point in the room.
*   **Answer:** **(c) On the ceiling.**

## Reasoning Questions (Page 103)

**2. A shopkeeper serves you cold lassi in a tumbler. By chance, it had a leak. You were given another tumbler to put the leaky tumbler inside. Will this arrangement help keep the lassi cold for a longer time? Explain.**
*   **Answer:** **Yes!** Placing one tumbler inside another creates a small gap between the two walls. This gap traps a layer of **air**. Air is an excellent **insulator** (a poor conductor of heat). The trapped air prevents the heat from the outside environment from transferring into the cold lassi, keeping it cold for a longer time. (This is the exact principle behind a thermos flask!).

**3. State with reason(s) whether the following statements are True [T] or False [F].**
*   **(i) Heat transfer takes place in solids through convection. [ F ]**
    *   *Reason:* In solids, particles are tightly packed and cannot move from their positions. Convection requires the actual movement of particles. Heat in solids transfers via **conduction**.
*   **(ii) Heat transfer through convection takes place by the actual movement of particles. [ T ]**
    *   *Reason:* This is the exact definition of convection in liquids and gases.
*   **(iii) Areas with clay materials allow more seepage of water than those with sandy materials. [ F ]**
    *   *Reason:* Clay particles are very fine and packed tightly, leaving tiny pore spaces that restrict water flow. Sand and gravel have much larger pore spaces, allowing faster and greater infiltration (seepage) of water.
*   **(iv) The movement of cooler air from land to sea is called land breeze. [ T ]**
    *   *Reason:* This happens at night when the land cools faster than the sea, causing cooler, heavier air to flow outward to replace the rising warm air over the sea.

**4. Some ice cubes placed in a dish melt into water after sometime. Where do the ice cubes get heat for this transformation?**
*   **Answer:** The ice cubes get heat from their **surroundings** (the air in the room, the dish itself). The surrounding air radiates and conducts heat into the colder ice cubes until thermal equilibrium is reached.

**5. A burning incense stick is fixed, pointing downwards. In which direction would the smoke move? Show with a diagram.**
*   **Answer:** The smoke will move **upwards**. Even though the stick points down, the smoke is made of hot gases. Because of **convection**, hot air is lighter than cold air and will always rise against gravity.
*(Diagram: Draw a stick pointing down, with wavy lines of smoke curving out from the tip and traveling straight up to the top of the page).*

**6. Two test tubes with water are heated by a candle flame. Which thermometer (Fig. 7.16a or Fig. 7.16b) will record a higher temperature? Explain.**
*(Observation: In 7.16a, the flame heats the bottom of the tube. In 7.16b, the flame heats the top surface of the water).*
*   **Answer:** **Thermometer in Fig. 7.16(a)** will record a much higher temperature faster.
*   **Explanation:** Water is heated primarily by **convection**. In setup (a), heating the bottom causes the hot water to become lighter and rise, forcing the cold water down to get heated, creating a convection current that heats the *entire* volume of water. In setup (b), only the top layer of water is heated. The hot water stays at the top because it is light, and because water is a poor *conductor*, the heat will not travel down to the thermometer bulb at the bottom!

## Descriptive Questions (Page 104)

**7. Why are hollow bricks used to construct the outer walls of houses in hot regions?**
*   **Answer:** Hollow bricks contain empty spaces that trap **air**. Air is a very poor conductor of heat (an insulator). The trapped air prevents the intense heat from outside from conducting through the walls into the house, keeping the interior cool.

**8. Explain how large water bodies prevent extreme temperature in areas around them.**
*   **Answer:** Water heats up and cools down much more slowly than land. During a hot day, the cooler water creates a **sea breeze** that cools the adjacent land. During a cold night, the water retains its heat longer and creates a **land breeze**, preventing the land from freezing. This constant exchange moderates the climate, preventing extreme temperature spikes.

**9. Explain how water seeps through the surface of the Earth and gets stored as groundwater.**
*   **Answer:** When precipitation (rain) falls, gravity pulls the water downward. It seeps through the tiny gaps and pores between soil particles, sand, and porous rocks—a process called **infiltration**. The water continues to travel downwards until it reaches a layer of impermeable rock. It then collects in the porous layers above this rock, forming underground reservoirs known as **aquifers**.

**10. The water cycle helps in the redistribution and replenishment of water on the Earth. Justify.**
*   **Answer:** The water cycle is a continuous engine driven by the sun. It evaporates salty ocean water, purifies it, and transports it via clouds across continents. It then drops this fresh water as precipitation over landmasses, filling rivers, lakes, and recharging groundwater aquifers through infiltration. Without this cycle, inland areas would turn into deserts, and freshwater resources would never be replenished.
