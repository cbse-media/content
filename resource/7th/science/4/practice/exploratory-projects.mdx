## Dive Deeper: Steel in Suspension Bridges

Have you ever seen wires made of coal or sulfur? Obviously not, because non-metals are not ductile! 

But did you know that the massive ropes supporting enormous suspension bridges (like the Bandra-Worli Sea Link in Mumbai) are made of metal wires? These wires are made of **Steel**.

Steel is not a pure element; it is an **alloy** (a mixture) of Iron (a metal) and Carbon (a non-metal). By adding a small amount of carbon to iron, the resulting steel becomes significantly stronger, harder, and more resistant to stretching than pure iron. This incredible tensile strength allows steel cables to safely lift heavy objects in cranes and support massive bridges!

## Project Ideas

### 1. Traditional Indian Metal Art
India has a rich history of metalworking. Research the following famous metal art styles and find out which states they originate from:
*   **Dhokra:** Ancient technique of metal casting using the lost-wax casting technique. (Prominent in Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Odisha, West Bengal).
*   **Bidriware:** Metal handicraft featuring intricate silver or gold inlay against a blackened zinc and copper background. (Originates from Bidar, Karnataka).
*   **Pembarthi:** Exquisite sheet metal brass and copper engravings. (Originates from Pembarthi, Telangana).
*   **Kamrupi:** Traditional brass and bell-metal crafts. (Originates from Kamrup, Assam).

*Task: Create a visual collage of these beautiful artworks!*

### 2. Anatomy of a Smartphone
Your modern smartphone is a treasure trove of metals and non-metals! Research the materials inside your phone:
*   **The Screen:** Made of glass (Silicon and Oxygen - non-metals), coated with Indium Tin Oxide (a metal oxide) to make it touch-sensitive.
*   **The Battery:** Usually a Lithium-ion battery. Contains Lithium, Cobalt (metals), and Graphite/Carbon (non-metals).
*   **The Wiring & Circuitry:** Contains Gold, Silver, and Copper (metals) because of their excellent electrical conductivity.
*   **The Casing:** Often made of Aluminum alloys (metal) or durable Plastics (non-metals).

### 3. The Great Debate: Metals vs. Environment
Organize a classroom debate on the following topic: *"Should the extraction and use of metals for comfort and luxury be decreased?"*
*   **Points to consider for 'Decreased':** Mining metals causes severe environmental destruction, deforestation, and pollution. Many metals are finite resources. Electronic waste is a growing global crisis.
*   **Points to consider for 'Status Quo/Increased':** Metals are essential for modern technology, medical devices, renewable energy infrastructure (like solar panels and wind turbines), and raising the global standard of living.
*   **The Middle Ground:** Focus on **sustainability and recycling**. In India and globally, recycling metals like iron and aluminium significantly reduces the need for new mining and saves massive amounts of energy!
