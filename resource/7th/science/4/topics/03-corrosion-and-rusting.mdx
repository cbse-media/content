## The Phenomenon of Corrosion

Have you ever noticed a green coating forming on the surface of old copper coins or copper vessels? Or a black tarnish forming on silver jewelry? 

The gradual deterioration of metal surfaces caused by the action of air, moisture, or other chemicals is known as **corrosion**.

## The Rusting of Iron

The most common and economically damaging form of corrosion is the rusting of iron. When iron objects are left in the open, they develop a flaky, brown deposit called **rust**. 

But what exactly causes rust? Let's look at an experiment to find the required conditions.

### The Rusting Experiment

We place three clean iron nails into three separate sealed glass bottles:

| Bottle | Setup Conditions | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A** | Nail + Silica gel (Silica gel absorbs moisture, making the air completely **dry**). | No rust formed. |
| **B** | Nail submerged in boiled water + a layer of oil on top (Boiling removes dissolved air, and oil prevents new air from entering. The nail is exposed to **water only**). | No rust formed. |
| **C** | Nail partially submerged in tap water and left unstoppered (The nail is exposed to **both water and air**). | **Brown rust formed!** |

### Conclusion

From this experiment, we conclude that **both water (moisture) and air (oxygen)** are essential for rust to develop. Moist air is the primary culprit behind the rusting of iron objects.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Preventing Rust:**
Because rust causes immense structural damage to bridges, ships, and vehicles, preventing it is crucial. We can prevent rusting by blocking the iron's contact with air and moisture. Methods include:
1.  Painting
2.  Oiling or greasing
3.  **Galvanisation:** Applying a thin protective layer of zinc metal over the iron.
</Callout>

## The Wonder of Ancient Indian Metallurgy

Not all iron rusts away! The **Iron Pillar of Delhi**, built during the time of Chandragupta II more than $1600$ years ago, stands $8$ metres tall and weighs over $6000$ kilograms. Despite facing centuries of wind, rain, and intense weather, it has barely any rust. It was forged using advanced ancient metallurgical techniques that make it highly resistant to corrosion.
