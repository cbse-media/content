## Multiple Choice Questions (Page 55)

**1. Which metal is commonly used to make food packaging materials as it is cheaper, and its thin sheets can be folded easily into any shape?**
*   **Logic:** The property described is malleability. While gold is highly malleable, it is extremely expensive. Aluminium is cheap, malleable, and safe for food.
*   **Answer:** **(i) Aluminium**

**2. Which of the following metal catches fire when it comes in contact with water?**
*   **Logic:** Sodium is a highly reactive metal that reacts vigorously with oxygen and water, generating enough heat to catch fire. This is why it is stored in kerosene.
*   **Answer:** **(iv) Sodium**

## True or False (Page 55)

**3. State with reason(s) whether the following statements are True [T] or False [F].**

**(i) Aluminium and copper are examples of non-metals used for making utensils and statues. [ ]**
*   **Answer:** **False [F]**
*   **Reason:** Aluminium and copper are **metals**, not non-metals. They are used for utensils because they are good conductors of heat, malleable, and ductile.

**(ii) Metals form oxides when combined with oxygen, the solution of which turns blue litmus paper to red. [ ]**
*   **Answer:** **False [F]**
*   **Reason:** Metals form **basic** oxides. Basic solutions turn red litmus paper blue, not the other way around. (Acidic oxides of non-metals turn blue litmus red).

**(iii) Oxygen is a non-metal essential for respiration. [ ]**
*   **Answer:** **True [T]**
*   **Reason:** Oxygen is a non-metal gas that all living organisms require to breathe and survive.

**(iv) Copper vessels are used for boiling water because they are good conductors of electricity. [ ]**
*   **Answer:** **False [F]**
*   **Reason:** While copper *is* a good conductor of electricity, that is not why it is used to boil water. It is used for boiling water because it is a **good conductor of heat**.

## Reasoning Questions (Pages 55-56)

**4. Why are only a few metals suitable for making jewellery?**
*   **Answer:** Metals used for jewelry (like gold, silver, and platinum) must possess specific properties:
    1.  They must be highly **lustrous** (shiny) and retain their shine over time.
    2.  They must be highly **malleable** and **ductile** so they can be crafted into intricate designs and thin wires.
    3.  They must be highly **unreactive** (corrosion-resistant) so they do not tarnish easily or react with skin, sweat, or air.

**5. Match the uses of metals and non-metals given in Column I with the jumbled names given in Column II.**
*   (i) Used in electrical wiring $\implies$ **(c) P E P O R C $\rightarrow$ COPPER**
*   (ii) Most malleable and ductile $\implies$ **(e) O G D L $\rightarrow$ GOLD**
*   (iii) Living organisms cannot survive without it $\implies$ **(a) E N X Y G O $\rightarrow$ OXYGEN**
*   (iv) Plants grow healthy when fertilisers containing it are added to the soil $\implies$ **(d) T E N G O I N R $\rightarrow$ NITROGEN**
*   (v) Used in water purification $\implies$ **(b) N E C O H I R L $\rightarrow$ CHLORINE**

**6. What happens when oxygen reacts with magnesium and sulfur? What are the main differences in the nature of products formed?**
*   **Answer:**
    *   **Magnesium (Metal):** Reacts with oxygen to form Magnesium Oxide ($MgO$). When dissolved in water, it forms a **basic** solution (turns red litmus blue).
    *   **Sulfur (Non-metal):** Reacts with oxygen to form Sulfur Dioxide gas ($SO_2$). When dissolved in water, it forms an **acidic** solution (turns blue litmus red).
    *   **Main Difference:** Metal oxides are basic, while non-metal oxides are acidic.

**7. Complete the flowchart:**
*   `[ ? ] + Air + Heat $\rightarrow$ Ash`
    *   **Answer:** **Magnesium** (Burning magnesium ribbon produces white ash/powder).
*   `Ash $\xrightarrow{\text{Water}}$ [ ? ]`
    *   **Answer:** **Magnesium Hydroxide solution** (or simply "Basic Solution").
*   `[ ? ] $\xrightarrow{\text{Add blue and red litmus}}$`
    *   `Change in blue litmus $\rightarrow$ [ ? ]` $\implies$ **No change** (remains blue).
    *   `Change in red litmus $\rightarrow$ Blue`

**8. You are provided with: Iron, copper, sulfur, coal, plastic, wood, cardboard. Discuss which material would be your choice to make a pan suitable for boiling water and why?**
*   **Answer:** I would choose **Copper** or **Iron**.
    *   **Why:** They are **metals**. Metals are hard, do not burn easily, and most importantly, they are **excellent conductors of heat**, which is required to boil water quickly.
    *   **Why not the others:** Sulfur and coal are brittle and would shatter or burn. Plastic, wood, and cardboard are poor conductors of heat and would catch fire or melt when placed on a stove.

**9. You are provided with three iron nails, each dipped in oil, water and vinegar. Which iron nail will not rust, and why?**
*   **Answer:** The iron nail dipped in **oil** will not rust.
    *   **Reason:** Rusting requires both oxygen (air) and moisture (water). The oil forms a protective barrier over the nail, cutting off its contact with the oxygen and moisture in the air.

**10. How do the different properties of metals and non-metals determine their uses in everyday life?**
*   **Answer:**
    *   **Ductility & Electrical Conductivity:** Metals like copper and aluminium are used to make electrical wires.
    *   **Thermal Conductivity:** Metals like iron and copper are used to make cooking utensils.
    *   **Malleability:** Aluminium is beaten into foil to wrap food.
    *   **Sonority:** Metals are used for bells and instrument strings.
    *   **Non-metals:** Oxygen is vital for respiration; nitrogen is used in fertilizers; chlorine is used to purify water because of their specific chemical properties.

**11. Protecting iron from rusting involves putting a thin coating of zinc over it. Since sulfur does not react with water, can it be used for this purpose? Justify.**
*   **Answer:** **No**, sulfur cannot be used to coat iron.
    *   **Reason 1:** Sulfur is a non-metal and is highly **brittle**. If coated onto iron, it would easily crack and flake off, exposing the iron to air and moisture again.
    *   **Reason 2:** Sulfur cannot be melted and adhered cleanly to metal surfaces like molten zinc can (galvanisation).
    *   **Reason 3:** Sulfur reacts with oxygen to form acidic oxides, which could actually accelerate the corrosion of the iron underneath!

**12. An ironsmith heats iron before making tools. Why is heating necessary?**
*   **Answer:** While iron is a hard metal at room temperature, heating it in a furnace until it is red-hot makes the metal significantly **softer and more malleable**. This allows the ironsmith to easily beat it with a hammer and shape it into various tools without the iron cracking or breaking. Once it cools down, it regains its hardness and retains the new shape.
