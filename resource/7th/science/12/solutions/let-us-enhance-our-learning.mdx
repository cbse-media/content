## Objective Questions (Pages 184 - 185)

**1. In Fig. 12.17, how many hours of sunlight do the North Pole and the South Pole receive during one rotation of the Earth?**
*   *(Observation of Fig 12.17: The Earth's axis is tilted so that the North Pole (NP) is in the blue/light zone, and the South Pole (SP) is in the dark zone).*
*   **Answer:** The North Pole receives **24 hours** of sunlight (continuous day), and the South Pole receives **0 hours** of sunlight (continuous night).

**2. Fill in the blanks:**
*   (i) Stars rise in the **East** and set in the **West**.
*   (ii) Day and night are caused by the Earth’s **rotation**.
*   (iii) When the Moon fully covers the Sun from our view, it is called a **total** solar eclipse.

**3. State whether True or False:**
*   **(i) Lunar eclipse occurs when the Sun comes between the Earth and the Moon.** 
    *   **False.** (The Sun is the center. The *Earth* comes between the Sun and the Moon during a lunar eclipse).
*   **(ii) Sunrise happens earlier in Gujarat than in Jharkhand.** 
    *   **False.** (Jharkhand is further East than Gujarat. Because Earth rotates West to East, Eastern places see the sunrise first).
*   **(iii) In Chennai, the longest day occurs on the summer solstice.** 
    *   **True.** (Chennai is in the Northern Hemisphere, where the summer solstice marks the longest day).
*   **(iv) We should watch the solar eclipse directly with our naked eye.** 
    *   **False.** (It will cause permanent eye damage).
*   **(v) Seasons occur due to the tilt of Earth’s axis of rotation and its spherical shape.** 
    *   **True.**
*   **(vi) The Earth’s revolution around the Sun causes day and night.** 
    *   **False.** (Revolution causes *seasons*. *Rotation* on its axis causes day and night).

## Analytical Questions (Page 185 - 186)

**4. Padmashree saw the Orion constellation nearly overhead at 8 pm yesterday. When will she see Orion overhead today?**
*   **Answer:** She will see it nearly overhead at **8 PM today** as well. Because the Earth completes one rotation relative to the distant stars in approximately 24 hours, the stars return to almost the exact same position at the exact same time the next day. *(Technically, due to revolution, it will be about 4 minutes earlier, so 7:56 PM, but for general purposes, 8 PM is correct).*

**5. Nandhini saw a group of stars rising at midnight on 21 June. When will she see the same group of stars rising at midnight next year?**
*   **Answer:** She will see them at midnight on **21 June next year**. The Earth takes one full year to revolve around the sun. Therefore, after exactly one year, the Earth returns to the same position in its orbit, and the night sky will look exactly the same.

**6. Abhay noticed that when it was daytime in India, his uncle who was in the USA was generally sleeping as it was night-time there. What is the reason behind this difference?**
*   **Answer:** The Earth is a sphere that rotates on its axis. Because it is a sphere, the Sun can only illuminate one half of it at any given time. India and the USA are on opposite sides of the globe. When India is facing the Sun (experiencing day), the USA is turned away from the Sun in the shadow (experiencing night). 

**7. Four friends used different ways to see the solar eclipse. Who among them was being careless?**
*   (i) Ravikiran used a solar eclipse goggle. (Safe)
*   (ii) Jyothi used a mirror to project the Sun’s image. (Safe)
*   **(iii) Adithya saw the Sun directly with his eyes.** $\implies$ **Careless!** This can cause blindness.
*   (iv) Aruna attended a programme arranged by a planetarium. (Safe)

**8. Fill in the circles in Fig. 12.18 appropriately (Sun, Moon, Earth).**
*   **Top Row (Solar Eclipse):** The alignment must be **Sun $\rightarrow$ Moon $\rightarrow$ Earth**. (The Moon blocks the Sun).
*   **Bottom Row (Lunar Eclipse):** The alignment must be **Sun $\rightarrow$ Earth $\rightarrow$ Moon**. (The Earth casts a shadow on the Moon).

**9. The Moon is much smaller than the Sun, yet it can block the Sun completely during a total solar eclipse. Why is it possible?**
*   **Answer:** It is possible because of **apparent size**. While the Sun is millions of times larger than the Moon, it is also millions of miles farther away. Because the Moon is so much closer to the Earth, they appear to be almost the exact same size in our sky.

**10. The Indian cricket team matches in Australia are often held in December. Should they pack winter or summer clothes?**
*   **Answer:** They should pack **summer clothes**. Australia is in the Southern Hemisphere. Because of the tilt of the Earth's axis, when it is winter in India (Northern Hemisphere) in December, the Southern Hemisphere is tilted *towards* the Sun, experiencing its hot summer season!

**11. Why can lunar eclipses be seen from a large part of the Earth, but a total solar eclipse can only be seen from a small part?**
*   **Answer:** During a lunar eclipse, the Earth casts a massive shadow that completely swallows the Moon. Anyone on the night-side of the Earth can look up and see the eclipsed Moon. 
However, during a solar eclipse, the Moon casts a very tiny, focused shadow onto the Earth's surface. Only the people standing directly inside that tiny moving shadow dot will experience the total solar eclipse.

**12. If the Earth’s axis were not tilted, explain what would be the effect on seasons?**
*   **Answer:** If the Earth's axis were perfectly upright ($0^\circ$ tilt) relative to its orbit, **there would be no seasons**. Every part of the Earth would receive the exact same angle and intensity of sunlight all year round. The equator would always be hot, the poles would always be cold, and every day everywhere would be exactly 12 hours of light and 12 hours of dark, 365 days a year!
