## The Dance of Shadows

We know that when an opaque object blocks light, it casts a shadow. Planets and moons are massive opaque objects! When the Earth, Moon, and Sun align perfectly, they cast massive shadows through space, creating an **eclipse**.

## 1. Solar Eclipse

A solar eclipse occurs when the **Moon comes exactly between the Sun and the Earth**. 

Even though the Moon is millions of times smaller than the Sun, it is also much, much closer to the Earth. Because of this, their *apparent size* in the sky is almost identical.

During a solar eclipse:
*   The Moon blocks the sunlight from reaching the Earth.
*   The Moon casts a dark shadow onto a small patch of the Earth's surface.
*   If you are standing inside that small shadow, the sky turns dark during the middle of the day! You are witnessing a **Total Solar Eclipse**.
*   If you are on the edges of the shadow, the Moon only covers a chunk of the Sun. This is a **Partial Solar Eclipse**.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Safe Viewing!**
Never look directly at a solar eclipse with your naked eyes, regular sunglasses, binoculars, or telescopes! The Sun's intense rays can cause permanent blindness. You must use specialized, certified solar viewing glasses.
</Callout>

## 2. Lunar Eclipse

A lunar eclipse occurs when the **Earth comes exactly between the Sun and the Moon**.

During a lunar eclipse:
*   The Earth blocks the sunlight from reaching the Moon.
*   The Earth casts a massive shadow over the full Moon.
*   If the Moon falls completely into the dark shadow, it is a **Total Lunar Eclipse**. Because Earth's atmosphere bends some sunlight, the Moon doesn't go totally black; instead, it glows an eerie, dark **blood red**!
*   Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse is completely safe to watch with the naked eye, and it can be seen by anyone on the night-side of the Earth.
