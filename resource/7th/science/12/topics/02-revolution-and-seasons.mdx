## Orbiting the Sun

While the Earth spins like a top on its axis, it is also traveling in a huge, nearly circular path around the Sun.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:**
**Revolution** is the motion of an object traveling around another object. The path it takes is called its **orbit**. 
The Earth completes one revolution around the Sun in about **365 days and 6 hours** (one year).
</Callout>

As the Earth revolves around the Sun over the course of a year, our view of the night sky changes. This is why you see different constellations in the summer than you do in the winter!

## Why do we have Seasons?

Many people mistakenly believe that summer happens because the Earth gets closer to the Sun, and winter happens when it gets farther away. **This is completely false!** (In fact, the Earth is actually closest to the Sun in January!).

The real reason for the seasons is **The Tilt of the Earth's Axis**.

The Earth does not spin perfectly upright relative to its orbit; it is tilted. As the Earth revolves around the Sun, it maintains this exact same tilt.

### Summer in the Northern Hemisphere (June)
*   In June, the Northern Hemisphere is tilted **towards** the Sun.
*   **Intensity:** The sun's rays hit the Northern Hemisphere directly, spreading intense heat over a smaller area.
*   **Duration:** The Northern Hemisphere receives sunlight for *more* than 12 hours a day. The days are long, and the nights are short.
*   This combination of intense, direct heat and long days creates the **Summer** season.

### Winter in the Northern Hemisphere (December)
*   In December, the Earth has traveled to the other side of the Sun. Because the tilt remains the same, the Northern Hemisphere is now tilted **away** from the Sun.
*   **Intensity:** The sun's rays hit at a shallow angle, spreading the heat weakly over a larger area.
*   **Duration:** The Northern Hemisphere receives sunlight for *less* than 12 hours a day. The days are short, and the nights are long.
*   This creates the **Winter** season.

*(Note: The seasons are always perfectly reversed in the Southern Hemisphere! When it is summer in India, it is winter in Australia).*

## Solstices and Equinoxes

*   **Summer Solstice (Around June 21):** The longest day and shortest night of the year in the Northern Hemisphere.
*   **Winter Solstice (Around Dec 22):** The shortest day and longest night of the year.
*   **Equinoxes (Around March 21 & Sept 23):** The Earth is neither tilted toward nor away from the Sun. Day and night are exactly equal (12 hours each) everywhere on Earth.
