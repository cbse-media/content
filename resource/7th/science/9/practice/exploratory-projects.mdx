## Project 1: Build a Working Lung Model

In Activity 9.2, the textbook describes building a model of the human respiratory system using a plastic bottle, a Y-shaped tube, two balloons, and a rubber sheet.

**How it works:**
*   **The Y-tube** represents the **windpipe (trachea)** branching into the bronchi.
*   **The two balloons** represent the **lungs**.
*   **The plastic bottle** represents the **rib cage / chest cavity**.
*   **The rubber sheet at the bottom** represents the **diaphragm**.

**The Experiment:**
When you pull the rubber sheet downwards, you are expanding the space inside the bottle. This lowers the air pressure inside the bottle, causing outside air to rush in through the Y-tube and inflate the balloons! This is exactly how your diaphragm pulls air into your lungs during **inhalation**. When you release the sheet, the balloons deflate (**exhalation**).

## Project 2: Air Quality Index (AQI) Investigation

**What is Air Quality and AQI?**
The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a number used by governments to communicate to the public how polluted the air currently is. A higher AQI value indicates a higher level of air pollution and a greater health concern.

**Task:** Find out the effect of poor air quality on the respiratory systems of people working outdoors (like farmers, factory workers, or street vendors).
*   *Research Prompt:* What kind of particles make up "air pollution"? How do microscopic particles (like PM2.5) bypass the mucus in our nasal passages and damage the alveoli in our lungs? What illnesses are caused by chronic exposure to high AQI?

## Project 3: Animal Adaptations - The High-Flying Birds

Both birds and mammals have lungs for breathing. However, certain bird species, like the Bar-headed Goose, can fly over the Himalayas at altitudes where the oxygen levels are incredibly low—levels that would cause a human to pass out!

**Task:** Research how a bird's respiratory system is adapted to survive in such extreme conditions.
*   *Hint:* Look up the concept of **"Bird Air Sacs"** and **"Continuous one-way air flow."** Unlike humans, who breathe air in and out of the same lung sacs (mixing fresh and stale air), birds have a complex system of air sacs that push fresh, highly-oxygenated air across their lungs *both* when they inhale and when they exhale! This makes their respiration vastly more efficient than mammals.
