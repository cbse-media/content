## Do all animals digest food the same way?

No! Animals exhibit variations in the structure and function of their alimentary canals, adapted perfectly to the specific types of food they eat.

### Ruminants (Grass-Eating Animals)
Have you ever noticed cows or buffaloes continuously chewing, even when they aren't eating anything new? 

Grass-eating animals are called **ruminants**. Their digestive process is unique:
1.  They quickly swallow partially chewed grass and store it in a large stomach chamber called the **rumen**.
2.  Here, partial digestion takes place.
3.  Later, while the animal is resting, this partially digested food is brought back up to the mouth in small lumps.
4.  The animal then chews it thoroughly. This process is called **rumination**. (A cow can spend up to 8 hours a day just chewing!).
5.  The thoroughly chewed food is then swallowed again for final digestion.

### Birds
Birds do not have teeth to crush their food!
Instead, they have a specialized muscular chamber in their digestive tract called a **gizzard**. 
*   Birds swallow their food whole, often along with small stones or grit.
*   The muscular walls of the gizzard contract and relax, using the swallowed stones to grind and crush the hard food (like seeds and grains) into a fine paste.
