## Introduction

How does a bite of chapati give you the energy to run and play? How do your lungs pull oxygen from the air, and why is that oxygen so important? 

All living beings perform certain essential functions to survive, such as nutrition, respiration, circulation, and excretion. These are collectively called **life processes**. In this chapter, we will take a detailed journey inside the human body to explore two of the most critical life processes: **Nutrition** (how we digest food) and **Respiration** (how we breathe and generate energy).

## Key Concepts and Definitions

*   **Alimentary Canal:** The long, continuous tube starting from the mouth and ending at the anus, where digestion and absorption of food take place.
*   **Mechanical Digestion:** The physical breakdown of food into smaller pieces, primarily by the teeth.
*   **Chemical Digestion:** The breakdown of complex food molecules into simpler forms using digestive juices and enzymes (like saliva breaking down starch into sugar).
*   **Breathing:** The physical process of inhaling air (rich in oxygen) and exhaling air (rich in carbon dioxide).
*   **Respiration:** The chemical process inside the body's cells where oxygen is used to break down glucose (sugar) to release energy, water, and carbon dioxide.
