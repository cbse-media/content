## Analytical Questions (Pages 135-136)

**2. Sahil placed some pieces of *chapati* in test tube A. Neha placed chewed *chapati* in test tube B, and Santushti took boiled and mashed potato in test tube C. All of them added a few drops of iodine solution. What would be their observations? Give reasons.**
*   **Test Tube A (Chapati):** Will turn **blue-black**. Reason: Chapati contains starch, and iodine turns blue-black in the presence of starch.
*   **Test Tube C (Boiled Potato):** Will turn **blue-black**. Reason: Potatoes are highly rich in starch.
*   **Test Tube B (Chewed Chapati):** Will show **no color change** (or very slight color change). Reason: When chapati is chewed thoroughly, the saliva in the mouth chemically digests the complex starch into simple sugars. Because the starch has been broken down, the iodine will not react to turn blue-black.

**3. What is the role of the diaphragm in breathing?**
*   **Answer:** **(iii) To help in inhalation and exhalation.**
    *   *Explanation:* By moving downwards, the diaphragm expands the chest cavity, pulling air in (inhalation). By moving upwards, it shrinks the chest cavity, pushing air out (exhalation).

**5. Anil claims to his friend Sanvi that respiration and breathing are the same process. What question(s) can Sanvi ask him to make him understand that he is not correct?**
*   **Solution:** Sanvi could ask:
    1.  "Does breathing release energy from our food, or does it just move air in and out of our lungs?"
    2.  "Breathing happens in the lungs, but where does the actual breakdown of glucose happen?" (Answer: Inside every cell of the body!).
    3.  "Breathing is a physical action we do with our muscles, but isn't respiration a chemical reaction?"

**6. Which of the following statements is correct and why?**
Anu: We inhale air.
Shanu: We inhale oxygen.
Tanu: We inhale air rich in oxygen.
*   **Answer:** **Anu** and **Tanu** are correct. 
    *   *Why:* We cannot inhale *pure* oxygen. The atmosphere is a mixture of gases (mostly Nitrogen). When we breathe, we inhale this mixture of "air". However, Tanu is also highly accurate because the air we inhale contains a higher concentration of oxygen ($\sim21\%$) compared to the air we exhale ($\sim16\%$). Shanu is incorrect because we do not exclusively inhale oxygen molecules.

**7. We often sneeze when we inhale a lot of dust-laden air. What can be possible explanations for this?**
*   **Answer:** Sneezing is a defensive reflex of our respiratory system. When we inhale dusty air, the dust particles get trapped in the tiny hairs and mucus inside our nasal passages. These particles irritate the sensitive lining of the nose. The brain triggers a sneeze to forcefully expel the irritants and clear the airway!

**8. Paridhi and Anusha started running for their morning workout. After they finished, Anusha was breathing faster than Paridhi. Provide at least two possible explanations.**
*   **Solution:**
    1.  **Fitness Level:** Paridhi might have higher physical stamina or be more athletic. Her body is more efficient at utilizing oxygen, so she doesn't need to breathe as rapidly to recover.
    2.  **Intensity of Workout:** Anusha might have been running much faster or exerting more effort than Paridhi, requiring her body to demand more oxygen to break down glucose for the extra energy needed.

**9. Yadu conducted an experiment. He added rice flour and water to Test Tubes A and B. To Test Tube B, he added saliva. After 45 mins, he added iodine. Test tube A turned dark (black/blue), while Test tube B remained light (yellow/orange). What do you think he wants to test?**
*   **Answer:** Yadu is testing the **digestive action of saliva on starch**. The rice flour contains starch (which turned Test Tube A black with iodine). In Test Tube B, the saliva broke down the starch into simple sugars over the 45 minutes, so there was no starch left to react with the iodine!

**10. Rakshita designed an experiment. In Test Tube A, she sucked air through the pipe. In Test Tube B, she blew exhaled air through the pipe into lime water. What is she trying to investigate? How can she confirm her findings?**
*   **Answer:** She is investigating whether **exhaled air contains more carbon dioxide than inhaled (atmospheric) air**. 
    *   *Confirmation:* The lime water in Test Tube B (where she exhaled) will turn **milky white** quickly because of the high concentration of carbon dioxide from her lungs reacting to form calcium carbonate. The lime water in Test Tube A will remain relatively clear, proving exhaled air is richer in $CO_2$.
