## Multiple Choice and Matching (Pages 165 - 167)

**1. Which of the following are luminous objects?**
(Mars, Moon, Pole Star, Sun, Venus, Mirror)
*   **Solution:** Luminous objects emit their own light. 
    *   **Sun** and **Pole Star** are stars; they generate huge amounts of light.
    *   Mars and Venus (planets) and the Moon only reflect sunlight. A mirror reflects light.
    *   **Answer:** **Pole Star, Sun**.

**2. Match the items in Column A with those in Column B.**
*   **Pinhole camera** $\implies$ **Forms an inverted image** (Light rays cross at the pinhole).
*   **Opaque object** $\implies$ **Blocks light completely** (Creating a shadow).
*   **Transparent object** $\implies$ **Light passes almost completely through it** (Like clear glass).
*   **Shadow** $\implies$ **The dark region formed behind the object** (Where light is blocked).

**3. Sahil, Rekha, Patrick, and Qasima are trying to observe the candle flame through the pipe (Fig. 11.16). Who can see the flame?**
*(Observation: The diagram shows a straight pipe pointing from Sahil to the flame. The other pipes have bends in them).*
*   **Answer:** **Sahil** can see the flame because light travels in a straight line. The others cannot see it because the light rays cannot bend around the corners of their pipes.

**4. Look at the images shown in Fig. 11.17 and select the correct image showing the shadow formation of the boy.**
*(Observation: The Sun is the light source. The shadow must fall exactly on the opposite side of the boy from the Sun).*
*   **Answer:** **Image (b)** is correct. If the sun is in the top-left of the sky, the shadow must fall to the bottom-right on the ground.

## Shadow Scenarios and Tables (Page 166)

**5. The shadow of a ball is formed on a wall by placing the ball in front of a fixed torch. In scenario (i) the ball is closer to the torch, while in scenario (ii) the ball is closer to the wall. Choose the most accurate representation of the shadows formed.**
*   **Solution:** When an object moves *closer* to the light source (torch), it blocks more of the expanding light rays, casting a much larger shadow on the screen. When it moves *closer* to the screen (wall), it casts a smaller, sharper shadow.
    *   Scenario (i) = Ball is close to torch $\implies$ Large shadow.
    *   Scenario (ii) = Ball is close to wall $\implies$ Small shadow.
    *   **Answer:** The image that shows a larger shadow for (i) and a smaller shadow for (ii).

**6. Based on Fig. 11.18, match the position of the torch with the characteristics of the shadow:**
*   **If the torch is close to the ball** $\implies$ **The shadow would be larger** (The ball blocks a wider angle of light).
*   **If the torch is far away** $\implies$ **The shadow would be smaller** (The light rays are more parallel).
*   **If the ball is removed** $\implies$ **A bright spot would appear on the screen** (No opaque object to block the light).
*   **If two torches are present** $\implies$ **Two shadows would appear on the screen** (Each light source casts its own distinct shadow).

## Analytical Questions (Page 167)

**7. Sketch the outline of the image of a tree formed in a pinhole camera.**
*   **Answer:** The sketch should show the exact shape of the tree, but completely **upside down (inverted)**. The roots should be at the top of the image, and the leaves at the bottom.

**8. Write your name on a piece of paper and hold it in front of a plane mirror. What difference do you notice? Explain.**
*   **Answer:** The name will appear backwards! The letters will be flipped from left to right. This difference is called **lateral inversion**. It happens because the mirror reflects light rays straight back; the right side of the paper reflects to the right side of the mirror, creating a flipped perception in our eyes.

**9. Measure the length of your shadow at 9 AM, 12 PM, and 4 PM.**
*   **(i) At which time is your shadow the shortest?** $\implies$ **12 PM (Noon)**.
*   **(ii) Why do you think this happens?** $\implies$ At noon, the Sun is directly overhead (or at its highest point in the sky). Because the light is shining almost straight down, the object blocks very little horizontal light, casting a very short shadow directly underneath. At 9 AM and 4 PM, the Sun is low on the horizon, shining at a sharp angle, which casts a very long, stretched-out shadow.

**10. On the basis of following statements, choose the correct option.**
*   Statement A: Image formed by a plane mirror is laterally inverted. (True)
*   Statement B: Images of alphabets T and O appear identical to themselves in a plane mirror. (True - T and O are perfectly symmetrical, so flipping them left-to-right looks exactly the same!).
*   **Answer:** **(i) Both statements are true.**

**11. Suppose you are given a U-shaped tube and two plane mirrors. Can this tube be used to make a periscope?**
*   **Answer:** **Yes.** You must place one mirror at the top corner, angled at exactly $45^\circ$ to reflect incoming light straight down the tube. You place the second mirror at the bottom corner, also angled at $45^\circ$, parallel to the first mirror, to reflect the light straight out of the viewing hole.

**12. We do not see the shadow of a bird flying high in the sky, but we see it when it swoops near the ground. Explain why.**
*   **Answer:** A shadow requires a screen to fall on. When a bird is flying very high, the Earth (the screen) is too far away. The sunlight scattered by the atmosphere fills in the small area of blocked light before it reaches the ground, making the shadow diffuse and invisible. When the bird swoops close to the ground, the distance to the screen is short, and the bird can successfully block a sharp patch of light, creating a visible shadow!
