## Introduction

Have you ever wondered why you cast a long shadow in the early morning, but almost no shadow at noon? Or how a flat mirror manages to show you an exact copy of yourself?

In this chapter, we will explore the fascinating world of **Light**. We will learn that light always travels in straight lines, which explains how crisp shadows are formed. We will also discover the difference between objects that make their own light (like the Sun) and objects that only bounce light back (like the Moon), and we will even build our own ancient camera using just a cardboard box and a pinhole!

<Callout variant="info">
**Did you know?**
Light travels incredibly fast, but not instantly! The light emitted by the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to travel through space to reach the Earth. If the Sun were to suddenly disappear, we wouldn't know about it for over 8 minutes!
</Callout>

## Key Concepts and Definitions

*   **Luminous Object:** An object that emits its own light (e.g., Sun, LED bulb, firefly).
*   **Non-Luminous Object:** An object that does not emit its own light. It is seen only when light from a luminous source bounces off it (e.g., Moon, book, chair).
*   **Transparent:** A material that allows light to pass through it completely (e.g., clear glass).
*   **Translucent:** A material that allows only some light to pass through it, making things behind it look blurry (e.g., tracing paper).
*   **Opaque:** A material that blocks light completely, casting a dark shadow (e.g., wood, metal).
*   **Lateral Inversion:** The effect produced by a plane mirror where the left side of an object appears as the right side of the image, and vice versa.
