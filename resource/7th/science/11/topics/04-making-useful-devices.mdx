## 1. The Pinhole Camera

A pinhole camera is an incredible device that forms images of the outside world without using any glass lenses! It relies purely on the fact that light travels in straight lines.

**How it works:**
1.  Light rays from the top of an object (like a tree) travel in a straight line, pass through the tiny pinhole, and hit the *bottom* of the screen inside the camera.
2.  Light rays from the bottom of the object pass through the hole and hit the *top* of the screen.

**The Result:** Because the light rays cross over each other at the tiny hole, the image formed on the tracing paper screen is always **upside down (inverted)**!

## 2. The Periscope

A periscope uses the reflection of light to allow you to see over or around tall obstacles. They are famously used in submarines to see above the water while staying hidden below!

**How to build one:**
You place two plane mirrors inside a Z-shaped cardboard tube.
1. The top mirror is angled at 45 degrees. Light from the outside hits it and reflects straight *down* the tube.
2. The bottom mirror is also angled at 45 degrees. The light traveling down hits it and reflects straight *out* into your eye.

## 3. The Kaleidoscope

A kaleidoscope uses multiple mirrors to create infinitely changing, beautiful symmetrical patterns.

**How to build one:**
1.  Tape three rectangular plane mirror strips together to form a hollow triangle (with the shiny sides facing inward).
2.  Slide the triangle into a cardboard tube.
3.  Seal one end with transparent plastic, toss in some colorful broken bangle pieces or beads, and cover that end with translucent tracing paper.

**How it works:**
When you look through the open end and point it at the light, the three mirrors continuously reflect the colorful beads, and then reflect the *reflections* of the beads! This creates multiple, perfectly symmetrical images that change every time you shake the tube. Designers often use kaleidoscopes to get ideas for new fabric or wallpaper patterns!
