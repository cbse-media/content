## Does Light Travel in a Straight Line?

Imagine looking at a candle flame through a long, straight hollow pipe. You can see the flame clearly. Now, imagine bending the pipe in the middle. Can you still see the flame? **No.**

This simple experiment proves a fundamental rule of physics: **Light always travels in a straight line.** Light cannot curve around corners on its own!

## Shadow Formation

Because light travels in a straight line, what happens when it hits an obstacle it cannot pass through? 

When an **opaque object** is placed in the path of light, it completely blocks the light rays. The area behind the object, where the light rays cannot reach, remains dark. This dark patch is called a **shadow**.

<Callout variant="warning">
**The Three Requirements for a Shadow:**
To observe a shadow, you absolutely must have three things:
1. A source of light.
2. An opaque (or highly translucent) object.
3. A screen (like a wall, the floor, or a piece of cardboard) for the shadow to fall on.
</Callout>

### Properties of Shadows

*   **Color:** Regardless of the color of the object (a red ball, a green apple, a blue book), the shadow formed is always dark/black. Changing the color of the object does *not* change the color of the shadow!
*   **Size and Shape:** The size and sharpness of a shadow depend on the distance between the light source, the object, and the screen. 
    *   Moving the object closer to the light source makes the shadow larger.
    *   Moving the object closer to the screen makes the shadow smaller and sharper.
*   **Information:** Shadows only give a 2D outline of an object. Sometimes shadows can be deceptive and we might not be able to guess what the original object is!
