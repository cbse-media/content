## Reflection of Light

When light traveling in a straight line hits a shiny, highly polished surface (like a steel plate or a mirror), it bounces off. 

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:** This change in the direction of light by a mirror or a shiny surface is called the **reflection of light**.
</Callout>

If you shine a torch onto a mirror in a dark room, you can see the beam of light hitting the mirror and instantly bouncing off in a new, straight direction!

## Images Formed in a Plane Mirror

A plane mirror is a standard flat mirror. When you look into it, you see an **image** of yourself (the **object**). The image formed by a plane mirror has very specific, predictable characteristics:

1.  **Size:** The image is the exact same size as the object.
2.  **Orientation:** The image is upright (erect). Your head is at the top, not upside down.
3.  **Distance:** The image appears to be exactly the same distance *behind* the mirror as the object is *in front* of the mirror. If you step back, your image steps back too!
4.  **Cannot be caught on a screen:** If you place a piece of paper behind the mirror, you won't see your face projected onto it. The image only exists *inside* the mirror.

### Lateral Inversion

There is one major difference between you and your mirror image. 
If you stand in front of a mirror and raise your **left** arm, your image raises its **right** arm! 

This perceived left-right reversal is called **lateral inversion**.
