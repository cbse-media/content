## Thinking Like a Scientist

In school or during tests, you are usually given questions and expected to find the answers. Let's turn that around! To think like a scientist, it is equally important to ask interesting questions. Great scientists don't just answer questions—they ask amazing ones!

**Task:** Look at the answers below. Come up with a curious, creative, and fun question or situation that could lead to these answers. There are no wrong questions!

<Callout variant="tip">
**Example:**
*Answer:* "Just make it half!"
*Possible Questions:* 
* "How do we ensure getting equal shares of the cake?"
* "My essay is too long, what should I do?"
* "I can't fit this paper in the envelope, any ideas?"
</Callout>

## Solutions / Creative Ideas

Here are some sample questions for the given answers. Feel free to use your imagination and come up with your own!

### Scenario 1
**Answer:** Just add some milk.
**Creative Questions:**
*   "How can I make this strong black coffee taste milder?"
*   "My bowl of dry cereal is too crunchy, what should I do?"
*   "What is the secret to making this cake batter softer?"

### Scenario 2
**Answer:** Because the cat’s teeth were crooked.
**Creative Questions:**
*   "Why did the little mouse escape from the cat's mouth unhurt?"
*   "Why did the veterinarian suggest braces for the pet feline?"
*   "Why did the cat leave weird, zig-zag bite marks on the toy?"

### Scenario 3
**Answer:** Don’t panic, I have my towel.
**Creative Questions:**
*   "What if we get caught in a sudden rainstorm while trekking?"
*   "The swimming pool is freezing cold! What will you do after you jump out?"
*   "Oh no, I spilled water all over the table! What do we do?"

### Scenario 4
**Answer:** $42$
**Creative Questions:**
*   "How many apples did you buy if you needed two dozen for the pie and 18 for the jam?" ($24 + 18 = 42$)
*   "If an alien species visits Earth, what might they say is the ultimate answer to life, the universe, and everything?" *(A fun reference to a famous science fiction book!)*
*   "How many kilometers are there in a standard full marathon?"
