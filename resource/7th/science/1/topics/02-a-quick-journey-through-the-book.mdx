## The Interconnected World of Science

You will read about topics in different fields of science, from physics and chemistry to biology and earth sciences. While they might appear as different chapters, they are all deeply interconnected. A scientific idea in one area often inspires discoveries in another, or at least allows us to ask new questions.

Here is a quick preview of what we will explore this year:

### 1. Properties of Materials (Chemistry)
We will look at the properties of the materials around us. We will ask questions about things we experience daily:
*   Why are some fruits sour?
*   What happens when we wash a *haldi* (turmeric) stain on our school uniform?

### 2. Electricity and Metals (Physics)
We will play with electric batteries, lamps, and wires.

![Detailed diagram of a simple electric circuit with a battery, wire, and a metal spoon](./placeholder.png)

*   What kind of materials do we need to make a lamp glow?
*   This exploration will lead us to classify materials into **metals** and **non-metals**.

### 3. Changes Around Us and Heat (Physical Chemistry)
We will explore the changes happening in our environment:
*   Batteries running out, ice melting into water, fruits ripening, and rocks breaking into pebbles.
*   Some changes can be **reversed**, while others **cannot**.
*   We will study how heat flows, from a melting ice cube in a glass to the Sun's heat evaporating seawater to form rain.

### 4. Life Processes (Biology)
Our bodies are changing rapidly, especially during the middle-school years. We will look at:
*   How we eat, breathe, and how blood circulates nutrients.
*   How plants get their food and breathe. Life has figured out how to do this in a beautifully balanced way over millions of years.

### 5. Time, Light, and Shadows (Physics and Astronomy)
How do we measure time? Before digital watches, humans used shadows cast by the Sun.
*   Light helps us see, and we have invented many ways to generate it.
*   Understanding light leads to a deep understanding of the universe we live in. 

![Detailed diagram of a solar eclipse showing the Earth, Moon, and Sun](./placeholder.png)

Even the Earth and the Moon cast shadows, leading to fascinating phenomena like **eclipses**. We will explore how the Earth rotates on its axis and revolves around the Sun, resulting in day and night.
