## What is Science?

Exploration isn’t just about discovering new facts or learning about different things in nature. Science is an **ongoing process of discovery**. It is about a way of thinking that welcomes curiosity, asks questions, and is open to the unknown.

In Grade 7, we aim to ask deeper questions:
*   How do things work?
*   Why do events happen the way they do?
*   What can we learn from the patterns we see in nature?

<Callout variant="tip">
**Key Concept:** To truly learn science, you must step out of the textbook and experience the world through activities, experiments, and observation.
</Callout>

## Science and Responsibility

As young science explorers, you will soon realize that human activities are deeply linked to what happens in the natural world. Our actions are connected to the society we live in. 

Science plays a crucial role in:
1.  Addressing environmental challenges.
2.  Creating a more sustainable world.
3.  Understanding our place on this planet.

Learning science comes with the responsibility of using that knowledge to protect and nurture our environment. Did you know that something as simple as a paper plane inspired real scientific explorations of flight? Let your curiosity lead the way, just like a paper plane taking flight into the skies!
