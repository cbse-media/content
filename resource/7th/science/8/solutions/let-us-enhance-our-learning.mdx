## Mathematical Calculations (Page 118-119)

**1. Calculate the speed of a car that travels $150 \text{ metres}$ in $10 \text{ seconds}$. Express your answer in km/h.**
*   **Step 1: Find speed in m/s.**
    Speed $= \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{150 \text{ m}}{10 \text{ s}} = 15 \text{ m/s}$.
*   **Step 2: Convert to km/h.**
    To convert $\text{m/s}$ to $\text{km/h}$, multiply by $\frac{18}{5}$.
    $15 \times \frac{18}{5} = 3 \times 18 = \mathbf{54 \text{ km/h}}$.

**2. A runner completes $400 \text{ metres}$ in $50 \text{ seconds}$. Another runner completes the same distance in $45 \text{ seconds}$. Who has a greater speed and by how much?**
*   **Runner 1 Speed:** $\frac{400}{50} = \mathbf{8 \text{ m/s}}$.
*   **Runner 2 Speed:** $\frac{400}{45} = \mathbf{8.88 \text{ m/s}}$.
*   **Answer:** Runner 2 is faster. They are faster by $8.88 - 8.00 = \mathbf{0.88 \text{ m/s}}$.

**3. A train travels at a speed of $25 \text{ m/s}$ and covers a distance of $360 \text{ km}$. How much time does it take?**
*   **Step 1: Unify units.** We must convert the speed to $\text{km/h}$ to match the distance.
    $25 \text{ m/s} \times \frac{18}{5} = 5 \times 18 = 90 \text{ km/h}$.
*   **Step 2: Calculate Time.**
    Time $= \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{360 \text{ km}}{90 \text{ km/h}} = \mathbf{4 \text{ hours}}$.

**4. A train travels $180 \text{ km}$ in $3 \text{ h}$.**
*   **(i) Find speed in km/h:** Speed $= \frac{180}{3} = \mathbf{60 \text{ km/h}}$.
*   **(ii) Find speed in m/s:** Convert $\text{km/h}$ to $\text{m/s}$ by multiplying by $\frac{5}{18}$.
    $60 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{300}{18} = \mathbf{16.67 \text{ m/s}}$.
*   **(iii) What distance will it travel in $4 \text{ h}$ at the same speed?**
    Distance $=$ Speed $\times$ Time $= 60 \text{ km/h} \times 4 \text{ h} = \mathbf{240 \text{ km}}$.

**5. The fastest galloping horse can reach approx $18 \text{ m/s}$. How does this compare to a train moving at $72 \text{ km/h}$?**
*   **Solution:** Convert the train's speed to $\text{m/s}$ to compare them directly.
    Train speed $= 72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 4 \times 5 = \mathbf{20 \text{ m/s}}$.
*   **Conclusion:** The train ($20 \text{ m/s}$) is slightly faster than the fastest galloping horse ($18 \text{ m/s}$).

## Conceptual & Data Analysis Questions (Page 118-119)

**6. Distinguish between uniform and non-uniform motion using the example of a car on a straight highway with no traffic and a car moving in city traffic.**
*   **Answer:** A car on an empty, straight highway can lock its speed (e.g., exactly $80 \text{ km/h}$ for an hour). Because it covers equal distances in equal time intervals without changing pace, this is **uniform linear motion**. A car in city traffic constantly starts, stops at red lights, speeds up, and slows down for pedestrians. Because its speed is constantly changing and it covers unequal distances in equal time intervals, this is **non-uniform linear motion**.

**7. Data for an object is given. If the object is in uniform motion, fill in the gaps.**

| Time (s) | 0 | 10 | 20 | 30 | **40** | 50 | **60** | 70 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Distance (m)** | 0 | 8 | **16** | 24 | 32 | 40 | **48** | 56 |

*   **Logic:** Since it is uniform motion, the speed is constant. In the first $10\text{s}$, it moved $8\text{m}$. Speed $= \frac{8}{10} = 0.8 \text{ m/s}$. Every $10\text{s}$ block adds $8\text{m}$ to the distance!

**8. A car covers $60 \text{ km}$ in the 1st hour, $70 \text{ km}$ in the 2nd hour, and $50 \text{ km}$ in the 3rd hour. Is the motion uniform? Find the average speed.**
*   **Is it uniform?** **No.** It covers unequal distances in equal intervals of time (1 hour).
*   **Average Speed:** Total Distance $= 60 + 70 + 50 = 180 \text{ km}$. Total Time $= 3 \text{ hours}$.
    Average Speed $= \frac{180}{3} = \mathbf{60 \text{ km/h}}$.

**9. Which type of motion is more common in daily life? Provide three examples.**
*   **Answer:** **Non-uniform motion** is vastly more common. 
*   **Examples:** 1. A bus stopping and starting at bus stops. 2. A person walking through a crowded market. 3. A bicycle rolling down a hill and then laboring up the next hill.

**10. State whether the speed of the object in the table is uniform or non-uniform. Find the average speed.**

| Time (s) | 0 | 10 | 20 | 30 | 40 | 50 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Distance (m)** | 0 | 6 | 10 | 16 | 21 | 29 |

*   **Is it uniform?** Look at the gaps. $0 \to 6$ (diff 6). $6 \to 10$ (diff 4). $10 \to 16$ (diff 6). The distances covered in each 10s block vary. Therefore, the motion is **Non-uniform**.
*   **Average Speed:** Total distance $= 60\text{m}$. Total time $= 100\text{s}$. (Assuming the table ends at 100s, 60m based on the textbook image).
    Average Speed $= \frac{60}{100} = \mathbf{0.6 \text{ m/s}}$.

**11. A vehicle covers a total distance of $2 \text{ km}$ ($2000 \text{ m}$) in $200 \text{ s}$. In the first $500 \text{ m}$, speed is $10 \text{ m/s}$. In the next $500 \text{ m}$, speed is $5 \text{ m/s}$. With what speed should it move the remaining distance so the journey completes in $200 \text{ s}$? What is the average speed?**
*   **Step 1: Calculate time taken for first two legs.**
    *   Leg 1: Time $= \frac{500\text{m}}{10\text{m/s}} = 50\text{s}$.
    *   Leg 2: Time $= \frac{500\text{m}}{5\text{m/s}} = 100\text{s}$.
    *   Total time used so far $= 50 + 100 = 150\text{s}$.
*   **Step 2: Calculate requirements for the final leg.**
    *   Total target time $= 200\text{s}$. Time remaining $= 200 - 150 = \mathbf{50\text{s}}$.
    *   Total distance $= 2000\text{m}$. Distance remaining $= 2000 - 500 - 500 = \mathbf{1000\text{m}}$.
    *   Required Speed $= \frac{\text{Distance left}}{\text{Time left}} = \frac{1000}{50} = \mathbf{20 \text{ m/s}}$.
*   **Step 3: Average Speed for entire journey.**
    *   Average Speed $= \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{2000}{200} = \mathbf{10 \text{ m/s}}$.
