## What is Speed?

If two runners are competing in a 100-meter sprint, the runner who reaches the finish line in less time is running faster. Conversely, if two cars drive for exactly one hour, the car that travels the greatest distance is moving faster.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Definition:**
The distance covered by an object in a unit of time (like one second or one hour) is called the **speed** of the object.
</Callout>

In everyday life, an object rarely travels at a perfectly constant pace for an entire journey. A train speeds up when leaving a station and slows down when arriving. Therefore, when we calculate speed over a long journey, we are actually calculating the **average speed**.

## The Speed Formula

To calculate the speed of an object, you divide the total distance it covered by the total time it took.

$$ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Total distance covered}}{\text{Total time taken}} $$

### Units of Speed
*   The SI unit of distance is the meter ($\text{m}$) and time is the second ($\text{s}$). Therefore, the **SI unit of speed is metres per second (m/s)**.
*   For vehicles and long distances, we commonly use **kilometres per hour (km/h)**.

## Rearranging the Formula

Once you know the speed formula, you can rearrange it using basic algebra to solve for Distance or Time!

**To find Distance:**
$$ \text{Total distance covered} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Total time taken} $$

**To find Time:**
$$ \text{Total time taken} = \frac{\text{Total distance covered}}{\text{Speed}} $$

## Example Calculations

**Example 1: Finding Distance**
Raghav is on a bus moving at a speed of $50 \text{ km/h}$. If it takes $2 \text{ hours}$ to reach his destination, how far is the city?
*   Speed = $50 \text{ km/h}$
*   Time = $2 \text{ h}$
*   Distance = Speed $\times$ Time = $50 \times 2 = \mathbf{100 \text{ km}}$.

**Example 2: Finding Time**
A train is travelling at a speed of $90 \text{ km/h}$. How much time will it take to cover a distance of $360 \text{ km}$?
*   Distance = $360 \text{ km}$
*   Speed = $90 \text{ km/h}$
*   Time = Distance $\div$ Speed = $360 \div 90 = \mathbf{4 \text{ hours}}$.
