## Introduction

In previous classes, you began your exploration of the world of real numbers and encountered irrational numbers. In this chapter, we continue our discussion on real numbers. We will focus on two very important properties of positive integers:
1. **The Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic:** This deals with the multiplication of positive integers. It states that every composite number can be expressed as a product of primes in a unique way.
2. **Revisiting Irrational Numbers:** Using the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, we will prove the irrationality of numbers such as $\sqrt{2}$, $\sqrt{3}$, and $\sqrt{5}$.

## Important Formula Sheet

Below are the most important mathematical relationships you need to memorize for this chapter:

### 1. Relationship between HCF and LCM (Two Numbers)
For any two positive integers $a$ and $b$:
$$
\text{HCF} (a, b) \times \text{LCM} (a, b) = a \times b
$$

### 2. Properties for Three Numbers (Advanced)
For any three positive integers $p$, $q$, and $r$, the simple product rule does **not** apply. Instead, the relationships are:

$$
\text{LCM} (p, q, r) = \frac{p \cdot q \cdot r \cdot \text{HCF}(p, q, r)}{\text{HCF}(p, q) \cdot \text{HCF}(q, r) \cdot \text{HCF}(p, r)}
$$

$$
\text{HCF} (p, q, r) = \frac{p \cdot q \cdot r \cdot \text{LCM}(p, q, r)}{\text{LCM}(p, q) \cdot \text{LCM}(q, r) \cdot \text{LCM}(p, r)}
$$
