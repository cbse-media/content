# The Other Side of Zero

Welcome to the world of Integers! Until now, we have mostly dealt with counting numbers ($1, 2, 3...$) and zero. But what happens when you go below zero? This chapter introduces **negative numbers**, used to describe temperatures below freezing, floors below ground, or debts in a bank account.

## Key Concepts

<Callout variant="tip">
**Integers:** The collection of numbers comprising whole numbers $(0, 1, 2, 3...)$ and their negatives $(-1, -2, -3...)$.
</Callout>

*   **Positive Numbers:** Numbers greater than $0$ (e.g., $+1, +5, +100$).
*   **Negative Numbers:** Numbers less than $0$ (e.g., $-1, -5, -100$).
*   **Zero:** The reference point. It is neither positive nor negative.

## Formula & Rules Sheet

### 1. Opposites & Inverses
Every number has an **additive inverse**. When added together, they equal zero.
$$
a + (-a) = 0
$$
*Example:* $+3 + (-3) = 0$

### 2. Addition (Movement Rule)
Think of addition as movement on a number line:
*   Adding a **positive** number $\rightarrow$ Move **Right** (or Up).
*   Adding a **negative** number $\rightarrow$ Move **Left** (or Down).
$$
\text{Starting Position} + \text{Movement} = \text{Target Position}
$$

### 3. Subtraction (Difference Rule)
Subtraction can be thought of as finding the distance or movement needed to get from one number to another.
$$
\text{Target} - \text{Start} = \text{Movement Needed}
$$
Alternatively, subtracting a number is the same as **adding its inverse**:
$$
a - b = a + (-b)
$$
*Example:* $5 - 2 = 5 + (-2) = 3$

### The Lift Analogy

In this building, a lift takes you between floors.
*   Pressing the **'+'** button moves you **UP**.
*   Pressing the **'-'** button moves you **DOWN**.

If you are at the Ground Floor ($0$):
1.  Press '+' twice $\rightarrow$ You go up 2 floors $\rightarrow$ Floor $+2$.
2.  Press '-' twice $\rightarrow$ You go down 2 floors $\rightarrow$ Floor $-2$.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Inverse Operations:**
If you go up 3 floors ($+3$), how do you get back to zero? You must go down 3 floors ($-3$).
This means $+3$ and $-3$ are **Inverses**. Adding them brings you back to the start:
$$
(+3) + (-3) = 0
$$
</Callout>

### Comparison
*   Floor $+3$ is **higher** than Floor $+2$ ($+3 > +2$).
*   Floor $-1$ is **higher** than Floor $-2$ ($-1 > -2$). Even though $2$ looks bigger than $1$, $-2$ is *lower down* (more negative).

## Comparing Integers

On the number line:
*   Numbers to the **Right** are **Larger**.
*   Numbers to the **Left** are **Smaller**.

### Examples
1.  **$+4$ vs $+1$:** $+4$ is to the right of $+1$, so $+4 > +1$.
2.  **$-1$ vs $-4$:** $-1$ is to the right of $-4$, so $-1 > -4$.
3.  **$-5$ vs $0$:** Any negative number is to the left of 0, so $-5 < 0$.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Common Mistake:**
Do not ignore the sign! While $100$ is bigger than $1$, $-100$ is much smaller than $-1$. Imagine debt: owing ₹100 is worse (less money) than owing ₹1.
</Callout>

## Addition: The Movement Rule

We can solve integer problems using the formula:
$$
\text{Starting Point} + \text{Movement} = \text{Target Point}
$$

*   **Positive Movement:** Move Right.
*   **Negative Movement:** Move Left.

### Example 1: $(+2) + (+3)$
1.  Start at **$+2$**.
2.  Add **$+3$** (Move 3 steps Right).
3.  Land on **$+5$**.
$$
(+2) + (+3) = +5
$$

### Example 2: $(+2) + (-5)$
1.  Start at **$+2$**.
2.  Add **$-5$** (Move 5 steps Left).
3.  Cross 0 and land on **$-3$**.
$$
(+2) + (-5) = -3
$$

## Subtraction: Distance Rule

Subtraction answers the question: "How much do I need to move to get from Start to Target?"

$$
\text{Target} - \text{Start} = \text{Movement Needed}
$$

### Example: $2 - 5$
*   Target: $2$
*   Wait! Let's rephrase. Usually subtraction is $A - B$. 
*   Standard view: Start at $A$, take away $B$.
*   Better View for Integers: **Adding the Inverse**.

### The Golden Rule of Subtraction
To subtract a number, simply **add its inverse**.

$$
A - B = A + (-B)
$$

1.  **Example:** $5 - 2 = 5 + (-2) = 3$
2.  **Example:** $2 - 5 = 2 + (-5) = -3$
3.  **Example:** $5 - (-2)$
    *   Inverse of $-2$ is $+2$.
    *   So, $5 + (+2) = 7$.
    *   *Subtracting a negative is like adding a positive!*

## Tokens and Zero Pairs

Imagine we have two types of tokens:
*   **Positive Token (+):** Represents $+1$ (e.g., Green/Red).
*   **Negative Token (-):** Represents $-1$ (e.g., Red/Blue).

### The Zero Pair
One positive token and one negative token cancel each other out. Their sum is zero.
$$
(+1) + (-1) = 0
$$

## Addition with Tokens

**Task: Add $(+3) + (-2)$**
1.  Place 3 positive tokens: `(+) (+) (+)`
2.  Place 2 negative tokens: `(-) (-)`
3.  Group them into zero pairs.
    *   Pair 1: `(+)` and `(-)` $\rightarrow$ Cancel.
    *   Pair 2: `(+)` and `(-)` $\rightarrow$ Cancel.
4.  What is left? One `(+)`.
    *   **Result:** $+1$.

<div class="flex justify-center my-6">
```mermaid

    subgraph Calculation
    A((+)) --- B((+)) --- C((+))
    D((-)) --- E((-))
    style A fill:#9f9,stroke:#333
    style B fill:#9f9,stroke:#333
    style C fill:#9f9,stroke:#333
    style D fill:#f99,stroke:#333
    style E fill:#f99,stroke:#333
    
    A --- D
    B --- E
    C
    end
    
    C --> Result[Result: +1]
```
</div>

## Subtraction with Tokens

**Task: Subtract $(+3) - (-2)$**
1.  Start with 3 positive tokens: `(+) (+) (+)`
2.  We need to "take away" 2 negative tokens.
3.  **Problem:** We don't have any negative tokens to take away!
4.  **Solution:** Add **Zero Pairs**. Adding zero pairs doesn't change the value.
    *   Add 2 zero pairs: `(+) (-)` and `(+) (-)`.
    *   Now we have: `(+) (+) (+)` and `(+) (-)` `(+) (-)`.
5.  Now, take away the 2 negative tokens `(-) (-)`.
6.  What is left?
    *   Original `(+) (+) (+)` PLUS the two positives from the zero pairs `(+) (+)`.
    *   Total: 5 Positives.
    *   **Result:** $+5$.

This proves why $3 - (-2) = 3 + 2 = 5$.

## Real World Integers

### 1. Temperature
We measure temperature in degrees Celsius (°C).
*   **0°C:** Freezing point of water.
*   **Positive:** Hotter days (e.g., 30°C in Summer).
*   **Negative:** Very cold places (e.g., -10°C in Ladakh).

### 2. Banking (Debits and Credits)
*   **Credit (+):** Money deposited or added to your account.
*   **Debit (-):** Money spent or taken out.
*   If you have ₹100 and spend ₹150, your balance is $100 - 150 = -50$. You owe the bank ₹50.

### 3. Elevation
*   **Sea Level:** 0 meters.
*   **Mount Everest:** +8848m (Above sea level).
*   **Mariana Trench:** -10,994m (Below sea level).

## A Pinch of History

Integers, including zero and negative numbers, have deep roots in India.
*   **Brahmagupta (628 CE):** An Indian mathematician who wrote the *Brāhma-sphuṭa-siddhānta*. He gave rules for computing with zero and negative numbers long before they were accepted in Europe.

### Brahmagupta's Rules (Simplified)
1.  **Addition:**
    *   Sum of two positives is positive.
    *   Sum of two negatives is negative.
    *   Sum of a positive and negative is the difference between them.
2.  **Zero:**
    *   A number plus its inverse is Zero.
    *   Subtracting a number from itself is Zero.

## Question
**What do you press to go four floors up? What do you press to go three floors down?**

### Solution
1.  **To go four floors up:**
    *   Each press of `+` moves you up one floor.
    *   We need to press `+` four times.
    *   Expression: $+4$ or $++++$.

2.  **To go three floors down:**
    *   Each press of `-` moves you down one floor.
    *   We need to press `-` three times.
    *   Expression: $-3$ or $---$.

## Example 1: The Mine Shaft

**Problem:** A lift in a mine moves at a speed of 5 meters per minute. It starts from 10 meters above the ground.
1.  Where will it be after 1 hour if it moves down?
2.  How long will it take to reach -350 meters?

### Solution

**Part 1: Position after 1 hour**
*   **Start Position:** $+10$ m.
*   **Speed:** $5$ m/min downwards ($-5$).
*   **Time:** $1$ hour = $60$ minutes.
*   **Total Movement:** $60 \times (-5) = -300$ m.
*   **Final Position:** $\text{Start} + \text{Movement} = 10 + (-300) = -290$ m.
*   **Answer:** 290 meters below ground.

**Part 2: Time to reach -350m**
*   **Target:** $-350$.
*   **Start:** $+10$.
*   **Distance to travel:** $\text{Target} - \text{Start} = -350 - 10 = -360$ m.
*   The lift must travel 360 meters down.
*   **Speed:** 5m/min.
*   **Time:** $360 \div 5 = 72$ minutes.
*   **Answer:** 1 hour and 12 minutes.

## Example 2: Magic Square

In a magic square, each row, column, and diagonal sums to the same number. Check if this is a magic square:

| 5 | -1 | -4 |
|---|----|----|
| -5| -2 | 7 |
| 0 | 3 | -3 |

**Check Rows:**
1.  $5 + (-1) + (-4) = 0$
2.  $-5 + (-2) + 7 = 0$
3.  $0 + 3 + (-3) = 0$

**Check Columns:**
1.  $5 + (-5) + 0 = 0$
2.  $-1 + (-2) + 3 = 0$
3.  $-4 + 7 + (-3) = 0$

**Check Diagonals:**
1.  $5 + (-2) + (-3) = 0$
2.  $-4 + (-2) + 0 = -6$ (Wait! This is not 0).

**Conclusion:** Since one diagonal sums to -6, this is **NOT** a magic square.
