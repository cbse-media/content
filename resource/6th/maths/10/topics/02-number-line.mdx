*   **Right of 0:** Positive Integers (Forward direction).
*   **Left of 0:** Negative Integers (Backward direction).

## Comparing Integers

On the number line:
*   Numbers to the **Right** are **Larger**.
*   Numbers to the **Left** are **Smaller**.

### Examples
1.  **$+4$ vs $+1$:** $+4$ is to the right of $+1$, so $+4 > +1$.
2.  **$-1$ vs $-4$:** $-1$ is to the right of $-4$, so $-1 > -4$.
3.  **$-5$ vs $0$:** Any negative number is to the left of 0, so $-5 < 0$.

<Callout variant="warning">
**Common Mistake:**
Do not ignore the sign! While $100$ is bigger than $1$, $-100$ is much smaller than $-1$. Imagine debt: owing ₹100 is worse (less money) than owing ₹1.
</Callout>
