## Beyond Counting Numbers

We know counting numbers start from $1, 2, 3...$. We also know $0$ represents "nothing". But are there numbers *before* 0? 

Imagine a building with floors above the ground and floors below the ground (basements). 
*   The **Ground Floor** is marked **0**.
*   Floors **above** ground are **Positive** ($+1, +2, +3...$).
*   Floors **below** ground are **Negative** ($-1, -2, -3...$).

### The Lift Analogy

In this building, a lift takes you between floors.
*   Pressing the **'+'** button moves you **UP**.
*   Pressing the **'-'** button moves you **DOWN**.

If you are at the Ground Floor ($0$):
1.  Press '+' twice $\rightarrow$ You go up 2 floors $\rightarrow$ Floor $+2$.
2.  Press '-' twice $\rightarrow$ You go down 2 floors $\rightarrow$ Floor $-2$.

<Callout variant="tip">
**Inverse Operations:**
If you go up 3 floors ($+3$), how do you get back to zero? You must go down 3 floors ($-3$).
This means $+3$ and $-3$ are **Inverses**. Adding them brings you back to the start:
$$
(+3) + (-3) = 0
$$
</Callout>

### Comparison
*   Floor $+3$ is **higher** than Floor $+2$ ($+3 > +2$).
*   Floor $-1$ is **higher** than Floor $-2$ ($-1 > -2$). Even though $2$ looks bigger than $1$, $-2$ is *lower down* (more negative).
