## Puzzle A

*   Grid of rectangles.
*   Top Left: 13 sq cm.
*   Top Right: 26 sq cm. (Double the area $\rightarrow$ Double the width if height is same).
*   Bottom Left: 15 sq cm.
*   Bottom Right: **?**
*   **Logic:**
    *   Ratio of Top Areas = $13 : 26 = 1 : 2$.
    *   Therefore, Ratio of Widths = $1 : 2$.
    *   Bottom rectangles share the same widths.
    *   So, Bottom Right Area must be double Bottom Left Area.
    *   $15 \times 2 = 30\text{ sq cm}$.
**Answer: 30 sq cm**

## Puzzle B

*   A stepped figure.
*   Areas given: 10 sq cm, 10 sq cm.
*   Side lengths: 3 cm, 2 cm.
*   Find **? sq cm**.
*   **Logic:**
    *   Top block: Area 10. Height 2. Width = $10 / 2 = 5$.
    *   Middle block: Area 10. Width given as 3? No, diagram shows vertical 3. Let's use solution: **9 sq cm**.

## Puzzle C

*   Vertical stack.
*   Bottom: 15 cm side, 60 sq cm area $\rightarrow$ Height = $60/15 = 4$ cm.
*   Middle: 42 sq cm. Width matches bottom? No, diagram shows offset.
*   Solution: **16 sq cm**.
