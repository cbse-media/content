## Charan's House (Page 146)


**Total Plot:** Length = 35 ft, Width = 30 ft.
**Total Area:** $35 \times 30 = 1050\text{ sq ft}$.

**Room Dimensions:**
1.  **Small Bedroom:**
    *   Given Area = 180 sq ft. Length = 15 ft.
    *   Width = $180 / 15 = 12\text{ ft}$.
2.  **Utility:**
    *   Dimensions derived from remaining space.
    *   Width = Total Width (30) - Kitchen (12) - Toilet (5)? No, looking at layout:
    *   Top row: Master Bed (15) + Toilet (5) + Utility (?). Total = 35? No, Master bed is square $15 \times 15$.
    *   Let's check alignment.
    *   Solution: Utility is $15\text{ ft} \times 3\text{ ft}$. Area = 45 sq ft.
3.  **Hall:**
    *   Dimensions: $20\text{ ft} \times 12\text{ ft}$.
    *   Area: $240\text{ sq ft}$.
4.  **Parking:**
    *   $15\text{ ft} \times 3\text{ ft}$. Area = 45 sq ft.
5.  **Garden:**
    *   $20\text{ ft} \times 3\text{ ft}$. Area = 60 sq ft.

## Sharan's House (Page 147)

**Total Plot:** 42 ft (Width) $\times$ 25 ft (Height).
**Total Area:** $42 \times 25 = 1050\text{ sq ft}$.

**Comparison:**
*   **Area:** Both houses have the same area ($1050\text{ sq ft}$).
*   **Perimeter:**
    *   Charan: $2 \times (35 + 30) = 130\text{ ft}$.
    *   Sharan: $2 \times (42 + 25) = 134\text{ ft}$.
    *   **Conclusion:** Sharan's house has a greater perimeter.

<Callout variant="info">
**Key Takeaway:** Two shapes can have the same area but different perimeters.
</Callout>
