## Chapter Overview

In this chapter, we explore two fundamental concepts of geometry: **Perimeter** and **Area**. We will learn how to calculate the distance around closed figures and the amount of space enclosed within them.

### What You Will Learn
1.  **Perimeter:** Measuring the boundary of polygons (rectangles, squares, triangles).
2.  **Regular Polygons:** General formulas for shapes with equal sides.
3.  **Area:** Quantifying the region inside a closed shape.
4.  **Area Estimation:** Using grid paper to find areas of irregular shapes.
5.  **Triangle Area:** Deriving the area of a triangle using rectangles.

### Formula Sheet

<Callout variant="tip">
**Quick Reference**

| Shape | Perimeter Formula | Area Formula |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rectangle** | $2 \times (\text{length} + \text{breadth})$ | $\text{length} \times \text{breadth}$ |
| **Square** | $4 \times \text{side}$ | $\text{side} \times \text{side}$ |
| **Equilateral Triangle** | $3 \times \text{side}$ | (Derived from base and height) |
| **Regular $n$-gon** | $n \times \text{side}$ | - |
</Callout>
